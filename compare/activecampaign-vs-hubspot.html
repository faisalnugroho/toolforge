ActiveCampaign vs HubSpot

ActiveCampaign and HubSpot are the two leading all-in-one marketing platforms for SMBs — but they sit at different points on the price/complexity curve. ActiveCampaign is the automation-first platform under $500/mo — best visual automations in the business + built-in CRM. HubSpot is the enterprise-grade suite — CRM, marketing, sales, service, content, and ops all integrated. Both have AI features in 2026. Here's how they compare for real teams.

✓ What ActiveCampaign does better

  • Best automation builder in the business at this price
  • Built-in CRM eliminates separate HubSpot/Pipedrive spend
  • AI predictive content improves open/click rates
  • Pricing is more transparent than HubSpot

✗ Where ActiveCampaign falls short

  • No free tier (only 14-day trial)
  • Pricing is per contact — escalates fast at scale
  • UI is functional but not as polished as HubSpot
  • No content/SEO/blogging tools like HubSpot

✓ What HubSpot does better

  • Free CRM is industry-leading (unbeatable)
  • All-in-one: marketing + sales + service + content + ops
  • Best-in-class content management and SEO tools
  • Brand recognition — buyers know HubSpot

✗ Where HubSpot falls short

  • Sticker shock once you leave the free tier
  • Marketing Hub Pro is $890/mo — enterprise pricing
  • Complex onboarding — often requires a HubSpot consultant
  • Some AI features gated to enterprise tiers

The verdict

For SMBs and B2B SaaS companies needing powerful automations under $500/mo, ActiveCampaign wins on value and feature density. For enterprise and mid-market teams that need the full marketing/sales/service suite and have budget, HubSpot is the default — the free CRM alone is industry-leading. ActiveCampaign gets you 80% of HubSpot's functionality at 20% of the price.

Winner: ActiveCampaign (SMB value) · HubSpot (enterprise suite)
