Beehiiv vs Substack

Beehiiv is the newsletter platform built for growth. Substack is the platform built for writers. Both added AI writing tools and ad networks in 2026. We tested both for deliverability, monetization, design flexibility, and ease of use. Here's who wins for creators who want to make money.

The verdict

Use Beehiiv if you want to grow a media business — referral programs, ad network, advanced analytics, multiple monetization streams. Use Substack if you're a writer who wants zero friction — just write, get paid, and tap into the network of readers browsing the Substack app. Different tools for different jobs.

Winner: Beehiiv (growth/monetization) · Substack (writing/network)

Monetization

Beehiiv has more monetization paths: paid subscriptions (0% platform fee), ad network (Beehiiv Ad Network), boosts, sponsor network. Substack takes 10% of paid subscriptions but offers built-in discoverability through the Substack app and recommendations. For raw revenue potential, Beehiiv wins. For "publish and pray someone finds you" simplicity, Substack wins.

Design and customization

Beehiiv lets you fully customize the email template, embed any HTML, build landing pages, use your own domain. Substack's templates are minimalist and opinionated — you can't change much. If brand consistency matters, Beehiiv. If "just write" matters, Substack.

AI features (2026)

Beehiiv added AI writing assistant (GPT-4 powered) + AI subject line A/B testing. Substack added an AI transcription tool for podcasts and basic grammar suggestions. Beehiiv's AI is more marketing-focused; Substack's AI is more writing-focused.

Discoverability and network

Substack's built-in recommendation engine and reader app drive significant organic discovery — many newsletters grow on Substack purely from the network. Beehiiv has no equivalent network; you grow through your own referral program and SEO. If you don't have an existing audience, Substack's network matters.

How we tested

We ran two newsletters (a B2B marketing newsletter and a personal essay newsletter) on both platforms for 90 days each. We scored on deliverability, growth, monetization, design flexibility, and AI quality. We don't accept payment for reviews — see our full disclosure.
