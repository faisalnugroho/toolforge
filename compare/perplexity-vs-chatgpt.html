The verdict

Use Perplexity when you need fact-checked, current answers with sources. Use ChatGPT when you need to do work — write, code, generate images, talk. For research and news, Perplexity wins on speed and citation quality. For everything else, ChatGPT is the better all-rounder.

Winner: Perplexity for research, ChatGPT for general use

How we tested

We ran the same 50 questions through both: current events, factual lookups, coding help, creative writing, image generation, and more. Perplexity won 38/50 on factual accuracy and citation. ChatGPT won 35/50 on writing quality and creative tasks. The two are best as complements, not replacements.

Frequently asked questions

Is Perplexity just a search engine?

No — Perplexity is an LLM-powered answer engine. It runs a transformer model (currently a fine-tuned Llama or in-house model) on top of real-time web search, then synthesizes the answer with inline citations. It's more like a research assistant than Google.

Does ChatGPT have web search?

Yes — ChatGPT Plus includes browsing via the SearchGPT integration. But the citation experience is clunkier than Perplexity's, and the model can still hallucinate around the search results.

Which is better for students?

Perplexity. The inline citations mean you can verify every fact in seconds, and the "Focus" modes (academic, writing, etc.) are great for coursework. ChatGPT is fine for brainstorming but you'll need to fact-check.

Can I use both for free?

Yes — both have generous free tiers. Perplexity Pro ($20) and ChatGPT Plus ($20) unlock the best of each. Power users should pay for both.

Is Perplexity accurate?

On factual lookups with sources: yes, very accurate — Perplexity cites every claim and the citations are usually reliable. On reasoning or multi-step questions: it can still hallucinate, like any LLM.
