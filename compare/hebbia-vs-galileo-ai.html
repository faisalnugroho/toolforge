Hebbia vs Galileo AI

Hebbia and Galileo AI are two of the most-talked-about AI tools of 2026, but they solve entirely different problems. Hebbia is the AI knowledge worker for finance — it ingests thousands of documents, runs multi-step analysis, and produces investment-grade research. Galileo AI is the AI UI design tool — it turns text prompts into editable Figma files in 30 seconds. Different jobs, different users. Here's how to think about each.

✓ What Hebbia does better

  • Ingest 1M+ pages and reason across them like a junior analyst
  • Used by major banks and PE firms for due diligence
  • Matrix view lets you compare answers across documents
  • Massive productivity gain on document-heavy workflows

✗ Where Hebbia falls short

  • Enterprise pricing — only accessible to funded companies
  • Steep learning curve to set up effective analyses
  • No consumer-facing tier
  • Document-heavy use case only — not general AI

✓ What Galileo AI does better

  • Fastest text-to-UI on the market
  • Output quality is genuinely impressive (needs <5 min of tweaks)
  • Real Figma export — full design control preserved
  • Free trial gives 3-5 generations to evaluate

✗ Where Galileo AI falls short

  • Not a replacement for a real designer on complex flows
  • Sometimes generates inconsistent component styling
  • Mobile UI patterns still weaker than web
  • No real-time collaboration like Figma natively

The verdict

These tools serve completely different use cases. If you're in finance, legal, or consulting and need AI to analyze massive document sets, Hebbia is in a class of its own (now valued at $700M+, used by major banks). If you're a product designer, founder, or PM who needs fast UI mockups, Galileo AI is the fastest path from idea to clickable prototype. They're not competitors — they're both best-in-class for their jobs.

Winner: Hebbia (document analysis for finance/legal) · Galileo AI (UI design generation)
