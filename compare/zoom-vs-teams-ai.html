Zoom AI vs Teams AI

Zoom and Microsoft Teams both now have AI meeting assistants built in. We tested both for 4 weeks across 60+ meetings — internal standups, client calls, and team reviews. Here's which handles summaries, transcription, action items, and post-meeting workflows better.

Where Zoom AI wins

  • Included with Zoom paid plans: No extra cost if you already pay for Zoom. Teams AI requires the $30/mo Copilot add-on on top of Microsoft 365.
  • Better summary quality: Zoom's summaries are more concise and better structured — key points, decisions, and action items are clearly separated. Teams summaries tend to be wall-of-text.
  • External meetings: Zoom AI works well for calls with external participants (clients, partners). Teams AI is optimized for internal meetings within your organization.
  • Whiteboard AI: Zoom's AI whiteboard generation (turn text prompts into diagrams) is genuinely useful for visual brainstorming. Teams Whiteboard is more basic.
  • Simpler to enable: One toggle in settings. Teams AI requires Copilot licensing and admin configuration.

Where Teams AI wins

  • Deep Microsoft 365 integration: Teams AI can pull context from your Outlook emails, SharePoint docs, and OneNote. Zoom only knows what happens in the meeting itself.
  • Document Q&A during meetings: Copilot can answer questions about documents shared in the meeting — "what does the Q3 report say about revenue?" Zoom can't do this.
  • Post-meeting action: Teams AI can draft follow-up emails, create Planner tasks, and update Loop components directly. Zoom exports summaries but doesn't act on them.
  • Chat analysis: Teams AI summarizes the meeting chat alongside the conversation — useful when people share links and notes in chat during the call.
  • Enterprise compliance: Teams AI inherits Microsoft's enterprise compliance certifications (HIPAA, FedRAMP). Zoom's compliance is good but less comprehensive.

The verdict

Use Zoom AI if you primarily do external video meetings and want AI summaries without paying extra. The summary quality is better, and it's included with your existing Zoom subscription. Use Teams AI if you live in the Microsoft 365 ecosystem and want AI that can access your documents, draft follow-ups, and create tasks automatically. Teams AI is more powerful but costs $30/mo extra. If budget matters: Zoom. If integration matters: Teams.

Winner: Zoom for value, Teams for integration

How we tested

Both tools were used daily for 4 weeks across 60+ meetings — internal standups, client calls, team reviews, and 1:1s. We scored on summary quality, action item accuracy, transcription, integration, and ease of use. See our full disclosure.
