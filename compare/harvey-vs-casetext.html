Harvey AI vs Casetext (CoCounsel)

Harvey AI and Casetext are the two biggest legal AI tools in 2026 — but they target different segments. Harvey is the premium enterprise option (40%+ of AmLaw 100 firms). Casetext (now CoCounsel) is the more accessible option for solo lawyers and small firms. We surveyed 50+ lawyers using both to find who wins for whom.

The verdict

Big Law, in-house teams, and Big 4 consulting: Harvey wins. The fine-tuning for legal reasoning, the Vault feature, and the multi-jurisdiction coverage justify the enterprise price. Solo lawyers and small firms: Casetext (CoCounsel) wins on price and accessibility. $200/mo is a much easier sell than $1,000+/mo, and the Westlaw integration is genuinely excellent for research-heavy work. There's also a third option: Spellbook for contract drafting specifically.
