SaneBox vs Superhuman AI

SaneBox and Superhuman are the two best AI-powered email tools in 2026 — but they take opposite approaches. SaneBox is a $7/mo add-on that works with any email client (Gmail, Outlook, Apple Mail, Fastmail) and automatically sorts your inbox. Superhuman is a $30/mo full email client replacement with AI built in. We tested both for 4 weeks on the same inbox (200+ emails/day). Here is the honest comparison.

The verdict

Use SaneBox if you want to keep your existing email client (Apple Mail, Outlook, Spark, etc.) and just need AI to manage the chaos. At $7/mo it is the best value in the category. Use Superhuman AI if you live in Gmail or Outlook, send 100+ emails a day, and want the fastest possible email experience with built-in AI. Superhuman is the right answer for executives, founders, and power users for whom email is the primary work surface. SaneBox is the right answer for everyone else — it works with your existing setup, costs a quarter of the price, and the inbox-zero feature alone is worth $7/mo.

How we tested

Both tools were used for 4 weeks on the same 200-email/day inbox. We scored on time saved per day, AI quality, workflow integration, and pricing. We do not accept payment for reviews — see our full disclosure.

FAQ

Can I use SaneBox with Apple Mail?

Yes. SaneBox works with any IMAP email account, including Apple Mail, Gmail, Outlook, Fastmail, ProtonMail, and most other clients. Superhuman is Gmail and Outlook only.

Is Superhuman worth $30/mo?

For executives and founders who live in email all day, yes. The keyboard shortcuts, AI triage, and snippets save 30-60 minutes/day. For occasional email users, no — SaneBox at $7/mo will save you almost as much time at a quarter of the price.

Which has better AI features?

Both are solid. Superhuman's AI is more deeply integrated (auto-drafted replies, tone matching, calendar-aware scheduling). SaneBox's AI is narrower but more reliable (it never mis-sorts an email that you actually needed to see).
