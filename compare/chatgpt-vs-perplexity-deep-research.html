The verdict

Use Perplexity Deep Research for any research task where sources matter — academic literature reviews, market analysis, competitive intelligence, fact-checking claims. Use ChatGPT for everything else — writing, coding, brainstorming, image gen, voice. For serious research work in 2026, the $20/mo for both is worth it. For casual users, Perplexity's free tier is genuinely useful; ChatGPT Plus is the better single subscription if you only pick one.

Winner: Perplexity for research · ChatGPT for everything else

ChatGPT wins at:

  • Generalist — writes, codes, generates images, takes voice
  • Stronger at long-form creative writing
  • Better code generation and debugging
  • GPT-4o image understanding (analyze photos, diagrams)
  • Best-in-class custom GPTs (1000s of specialized bots)

Perplexity Deep Research wins at:

  • Cites every source with live links (ChatGPT can hallucinate)
  • Reads 50+ sources in 5 minutes for a Deep Research query
  • Live web access — current events, today's news
  • Free tier is genuinely useful (5 Deep Research queries/day)
  • No hallucinated citations — major edge for fact-finding

How we tested

Both tools were used for 30 distinct research queries over 6 weeks — market sizing, competitive analysis, academic literature reviews, fact-checking news claims, and technical deep dives. We scored on source accuracy, depth of research, citation quality, and time to a useful answer. We don't accept payment for reviews — see our full disclosure.
