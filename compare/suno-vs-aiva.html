The verdict

Use Suno if you need full songs with vocals, fast generation, or pop/rock/hip-hop styles. Use AIVA if you need editable MIDI scores for film, games, or classical/orchestral work. They're not really competitors — they solve different problems. Most serious musicians end up subscribing to both.

Frequently asked questions

Is Suno or AIVA better for music?

For songs with vocals, Suno wins — it's the only major tool that does it well. For instrumental scores you can actually edit in a DAW, AIVA wins — MIDI export is the killer feature.

Can I use Suno music on YouTube?

Yes, on the Pro plan ($10/mo) you get commercial rights. Free tier is for non-commercial use only and includes a usage cap.

Can I use AIVA music in games?

Yes, on Standard ($15/mo) and Pro ($33/mo) plans you have commercial rights. Pro grants full copyright ownership.

Which is better for a composer?

AIVA, without question. MIDI export lets you refine the AI's output in your DAW. Suno gives you audio you can listen to but can't edit musically.
