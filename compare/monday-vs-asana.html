The verdict

Use monday.com if you want a flexible work OS that non-technical teams actually enjoy using. The AI Blocks feel native and the visual design is best-in-class. Use Asana if you need structured workflows, dependencies, and the new AI Studio for building custom AI automations. Both are excellent — pick by team culture.

Winner: monday for flexibility and UX, Asana for workflow rigor

Core differences

monday.com is a work OS — boards for projects, CRM, marketing, HR, IT, anything. Highly visual, drag-and-drop, color-coded. Asana is a work management platform — focused on projects and tasks with strong workflow primitives (dependencies, milestones, portfolios). monday is more flexible; Asana is more opinionated.

AI features in 2026

monday AI: AI Blocks drop into any column to summarize, classify, generate, or extract. AI Assistant handles conversational queries. AI can write formulas. Available across all paid tiers (with credit limits). Asana AI: AI Studio lets you build custom AI workflows with a visual canvas — chain LLM steps, call external APIs, trigger on any event. Smarter but more setup.

Pricing comparison

monday.com Basic: $12/seat/mo (8 seats minimum). Pro: $19/seat/mo. Enterprise: custom. AI is included with credit limits on paid tiers. Asana Starter: $10.99/user/mo (up to 10 users). Advanced: $24.99/user/mo. AI Studio is a Business+ feature. monday is slightly cheaper at small scale; Asana is cheaper at very small scale (no 8-seat minimum).

Use case fit

monday.com: ops teams running 5+ parallel projects, agencies managing client work, marketing teams with mixed campaigns, any team that wants flexibility. Asana: product teams managing sprints, marketing teams running structured campaigns, organizations with strict approval workflows, teams that need Portfolio and Goals features.

Integrations

Both have 200+ integrations. monday.com: Slack, Gmail, Outlook, Salesforce, HubSpot, Jira, GitHub, Figma, Zoom, Google Drive. Asana: same set plus stronger Zapier/Make recipes, deeper Google Workspace, native Adobe Creative Cloud. Tie, with slight edge to Asana for Google-heavy teams.

How we tested

We ran a 60-person marketing operations team on both platforms for 30 days each. We measured time-to-complete common tasks, AI usage patterns, user satisfaction surveys, and total cost. We don't accept payment for reviews — see our full disclosure.
