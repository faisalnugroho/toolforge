Krea vs Leonardo

Krea and Leonardo are the two best-in-class tools for AI image generation in 2026. Krea pioneered real-time generation — you sketch and the AI fills in as you draw. Leonardo is the production workhorse — models, training, canvas editor, and API. Both are excellent; the choice is about your workflow.

The verdict

Pick Krea if you want real-time, iterative image generation and you're a designer or illustrator. Pick Leonardo if you need production-grade art at scale — game assets, product shots, training your own models. Krea feels like a creative partner; Leonardo feels like a tool.

Winner: Krea for creative flow, Leonardo for production

How we tested

We generated 500 images on each platform across 20 prompts: photoreal portraits, game assets, product mockups, illustrations, and abstract art. Krea's real-time feedback was unbeatable for ideation. Leonardo's model variety (Phoenix, Kino, Lightning) and fine-tuning produced more production-ready results.

Frequently asked questions

Is Krea better than Leonardo for video?

Yes — Krea Video is significantly ahead in real-time, controllable video generation. Leonardo has motion tools but they're more traditional frame-by-frame. If video is your primary need, Krea wins.

Can I train custom models on both?

Yes — both support custom model training. Leonardo's training is more mature (more documentation, more community models, more training presets). Krea's "AI Trainer" is simpler and faster for quick fine-tunes.

Which is cheaper?

Leonardo is cheaper at the entry tier ($12/mo vs $24/mo). Both have free tiers with limited generations. For high-volume generation, Leonardo's credit system is more transparent and predictable.

Is Krea good for non-artists?

Yes — Krea's "Enhance" and "Ideate" modes are great for non-artists who want to iterate on prompts and see results fast. The learning curve is gentler than Leonardo's.

Do they have APIs?

Yes — both have APIs. Leonardo's is more mature with better documentation and enterprise features. Krea's API is newer but covers the core generation features.
