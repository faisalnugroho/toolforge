Sonix vs Otter

Sonix and Otter are the two most popular AI transcription services in 2026. Sonix is built for content creators who edit transcripts all day; Otter is built for meeting attendees who want live notes and summaries. We tested both across 50+ hours of interviews, podcasts, and meetings. Here's who wins.

The verdict

Use Sonix if you transcribe for a living — journalists, podcasters, lawyers, researchers. The editor and 49-language support are unmatched. Use Otter if you want free meeting notes and live Zoom integration — it's still the best meeting assistant in 2026.

Winner: Sonix for editing power, Otter for meetings and free tier

Accuracy comparison

On English audio with clear speakers: both services achieve ~95% word accuracy on benchmarks. Sonix slightly edges Otter on multi-speaker podcasts (better speaker diarization). Otter slightly edges Sonix on Zoom meeting audio (trained on more meeting data). For accented English (Caribbean, regional Indian, East Asian), Sonix's custom models perform noticeably better.

Speed comparison

Sonix is faster: 5-8 minutes for a 1-hour file. Otter: 12-15 minutes. Both are vastly faster than Rev ($1.50/min + 24-hour wait). For real-time meeting transcription, Otter wins because it joins Zoom and writes notes live — Sonix requires you to upload finished recordings.

Editor comparison

This is where Sonix wins decisively. The word-level in-browser editor with side-by-side audio playback is best-in-class — like Google Docs for transcripts. Otter's editor works but is clunkier; corrections are bulk-based rather than per-word.

Pricing comparison

Otter wins on price for casual users — the free tier (300 min/mo) is genuinely useful. Sonix charges by the hour ($10/hr for Standard) or $22/mo subscription (10 hours/mo). Heavy users pay more for Sonix; casual users save with Otter.

How we tested

We transcribed 50+ hours of audio — interviews, podcasts, internal meetings, and YouTube content — across both services. We scored on accuracy, speed, editor usability, language support, pricing, and integrations. We don't accept payment for reviews — see our full disclosure.
