Moz Pro vs Semrush

Moz and Semrush are the two longest-standing SEO platforms in 2026. Moz invented Domain Authority; Semrush has the bigger keyword database. We tested both across 4 client sites (one local, one SaaS, one ecommerce, one news publisher) over 60 days. Here's who wins and when.

The verdict

Use Moz Pro if you're an in-house SEO team that values Domain Authority, beginner-friendly UX, and the best SEO education in the industry. Use Semrush if you're an agency that needs the deepest keyword database, content marketing tools, and paid search overlap. Moz wins for solo + SMB in-house. Semrush wins for agencies and content-heavy publishers.

Pricing

Moz Pro starts at $99/mo for the Standard plan (10 campaigns, 500 keyword tracking, 25K page crawl). Semrush starts at $139/mo for Pro (5 projects, 5K keyword tracking, but full database access). At the entry level, Moz is cheaper. At the agency tier, Semrush's Guru ($249/mo) and Business ($499/mo) give more features per dollar for multi-client work.

Keyword Research

Semrush wins decisively. 26 billion keywords vs. Moz's ~1.25 billion. Semrush also includes paid search volume (Google Ads), which Moz lacks. For organic-only research at SMB scale, Moz is enough. For agencies managing multi-market clients, Semrush is the only real choice.

Backlink Analysis

Closer than people think. Moz's 40T+ backlink index is solid. Semrush's 43T backlinks is slightly larger. Moz's Spam Score is more actionable for disavow work; Semrush's Authority Score is better for outreach prioritization. For pure link audit, Moz wins on UX; for link prospecting at scale, Semrush wins.

Site Audits

Both are excellent. Moz's crawl is faster (25K pages on Medium plan vs. Semrush's 20K). Semrush catches more technical issues (JavaScript rendering, log file analysis on higher tiers). Moz's prioritized recommendations are clearer for beginners. For technical SEO specialists, Semrush wins; for content teams, Moz wins.

Content Marketing

Semrush wins outright. SEO Content Template, Topic Research, and the SEO Writing Assistant are 3-4 tools Moz doesn't have. If you're producing content at scale, Semrush is the only choice between the two.

Winner: Tie — Moz for in-house + beginners, Semrush for agencies + content

How we tested

Both tools were tested across 4 client sites for 60 days. We scored on keyword database, backlink index, audit quality, content tools, and value. We don't accept payment for reviews — see our full disclosure.
