Where Kling 3 wins

Kling 3 excels at physics-based motion. Water splashing, objects falling, people running — the physics just looks more natural than Sora 4. It's also cheaper ($10/mo vs $20/mo), has a generous free tier (66 credits/day), and supports 15-second clips. For content creators on a budget who need realistic motion, Kling 3 is the value pick.

Where Sora 4 wins

Sora 4 produces the most cinematic, photorealistic clips. Its prompt adherence is the best in the industry — describe a specific shot, camera angle, and lighting, and Sora delivers. The native audio generation (ambient sound, dialogue) is a killer feature that Kling lacks. Sora also handles complex multi-subject scenes better. If quality is your top priority and budget isn't a concern, Sora 4 is the winner.

The verdict

Use Kling 3 if you want the best value, realistic physics, and a free tier. Use Sora 4 if you need cinematic quality, native audio, and the best prompt accuracy. For most creators, Kling 3 at $10/mo is the smarter choice. Professional studios and ad agencies should go with Sora 4.

Winner: Kling 3 for value, Sora 4 for quality
