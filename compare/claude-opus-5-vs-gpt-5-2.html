When Claude Opus 5 wins

Reach for Claude Opus 5 when the output is judged by a human reader, not a test suite. Long-form essays, executive memos, marketing copy that has to sound like a person wrote it — Claude's prose has a cadence and restraint GPT-5.2 still can't quite match. It also wins on careful code review: it's the model most likely to say "this works, but here's why it'll bite you in three months," which is the only kind of review that actually prevents incidents.

The tunable thinking budget is the second reason. On hard problems you can dial it up to 64K thinking tokens and watch it work; on routine tasks you dial it down to keep latency reasonable. GPT-5.2's reasoning is more of an on/off switch.

When GPT-5.2 wins

Anywhere you need breadth or an agent that does things. GPT-5.2's computer use is meaningfully better than Claude's — it clicks more reliably, recovers from errors, and handles multi-step workflows without hand-holding. Persistent agents (the kind that keep working while you sleep) are first-class in ChatGPT and barely exist in Claude. Native image generation, the GPTs ecosystem, voice mode that actually interrupts gracefully, and a 1M context window all add up.

Math, multi-step reasoning, and "do five things in a row" agentic tasks: GPT-5.2, often by a wide margin.

The verdict

Use Claude Opus 5 when the deliverable is prose or code review a human will read carefully. Use GPT-5.2 for everything else — agents, math, image gen, voice, large context, and any task that benefits from the deepest plugin ecosystem. Most knowledge workers in 2026 should pay for both and route by task type. If you can only afford one: GPT-5.2 covers more ground, but Claude Opus 5 is irreplaceable for the work it does best.

Winner: Claude Opus 5 on craft · GPT-5.2 on breadth

How we tested

Both models were used daily for 4 weeks across 50 real-world tasks: 12 writing, 12 coding, 8 math/reasoning, 8 agentic (computer use + tool calls), 6 long-context, and 4 multimodal.
