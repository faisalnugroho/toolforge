Upscayl vs Let's Enhance

Upscayl is the free, open-source desktop upscaler that runs locally on your GPU. Let's Enhance is the cloud upscaler with a real API for $9/mo. Both use Real-ESRGAN-class models. Here's who wins — and when.

The verdict

Use Upscayl if you have a GPU and want unlimited, free, private upscaling. Use Let's Enhance if you don't have a GPU, need to pipe upscaling into a workflow via API, or are on a Chromebook. Quality is comparable (both use Real-ESRGAN-class models). The decision is purely about hardware, API, and budget.

Winner: Upscayl for GPU owners, Let's Enhance for everyone else

How we tested

We upscaled 200 product photos (12MP → 48MP) and 100 old family photos (640x480 → 2560x1920) on both. Output quality was within 5% on blind A/B tests — both look great. Upscayl was free and private but took 8 hours for the full batch. Let's Enhance processed the same batch in 12 minutes via cloud but cost $14 in credits. For one-off use, Upscayl wins. For batch + API, Let's Enhance wins.
