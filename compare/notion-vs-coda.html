Notion vs Coda

Notion and Coda are the two most-loved doc-tools-that-became-platforms in 2026. Both have AI. Both have databases. Both let you build custom workflows without code. But they take fundamentally different approaches: Notion is doc-first (write a doc, add a table if needed); Coda is doc-and-table-first (every doc IS a table). We tested both for 12 weeks on a 6-person marketing team and a solo operations workflow.

The verdict

Pick Notion if your team is primarily writing, sharing knowledge, and managing projects via docs and wikis. The AI add-on is excellent for Q&A on your knowledge base. Pick Coda if your work involves lots of structured data, custom workflows, and you want AI in every cell. Coda's Brain feature is the standout for operational teams.

Who should pick Notion?

Content teams, design agencies, founders managing personal knowledge, anyone whose work is 'docs with some tables'. Notion's AI Q&A across your entire workspace is genuinely magical for teams who write a lot. The template marketplace is 10x larger than Coda's.

Who should pick Coda?

Operations teams, sales teams, agencies managing client data, anyone whose work is 'tables with some docs'. Coda's tables are dramatically more powerful than Notion's (formulas, buttons, automations). The AI is included free, no add-on needed. Brain knows every doc in your workspace.
