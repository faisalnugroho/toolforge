Gamma vs Canva

Gamma and Canva both promise 'AI design for non-designers,' but they come at it from different angles. Gamma is AI-native: you describe what you want, and it generates a full deck, doc, or webpage in seconds. Canva is the design platform that added AI: 250M+ users, thousands of templates, and the new Magic Studio AI features. We tested both on real presentations, social graphics, and one-pagers. Here's who wins and when.

Gamma

AI-native presentation, document, and webpage builder

Price: $8/mo (free tier)
Best for: AI-first decks and docs, fast iteration, no design skills needed
✓ Pros
  • Fastest AI generation — full deck in 30 seconds from a prompt
  • No design skills needed — AI handles layout, typography, imagery
  • Built for the AI era — export to PPT, PDF, web, share link
  • Real-time collaboration like Notion/Google Docs
  • Free tier is genuinely useful (10 AI credits, no watermark)
✗ Cons
  • Less design control than Canva (AI makes the design choices)
  • Smaller template library (Gamma is newer)
  • Brand kit features are basic vs Canva
  • Image generation quality is average
  • Less mature for social media graphics vs Canva

The verdict

Use Gamma if you need a presentation, document, or webpage generated fast from a prompt. It's the fastest path from 'I need a deck' to 'I have a deck' — and the AI does the design work. Use Canva for design-heavy graphics, brand-consistent templates, and team collaboration on visual assets. Canva is the design tool; Gamma is the AI-generation tool.

Winner: Gamma (for AI-first speed) · Canva (for design depth and team workflows)

How we tested

We built 20 real artifacts on each: 10 presentations (5 from prompts, 5 from templates), 5 social graphics, and 5 one-page documents. We scored on speed, design quality, customization, AI quality, and team collaboration. We tested Gamma Pro ($8/mo) and Canva Pro ($13/mo).
