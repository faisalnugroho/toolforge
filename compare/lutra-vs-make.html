Lutra vs Make

Lutra builds workflows from plain-English prompts. Make builds them from a visual node graph. Both automate across your SaaS stack — but they're optimized for very different users and use cases. We migrated 12 workflows from Make to Lutra (and back). Here's what we learned.

Where each one wins

  • Setup speed: Lutra ships a working workflow in 5-10 minutes from a prompt. Make takes 30-90 minutes for the same workflow (node-dragging, field mapping, error handling).
  • Integration depth: Make has 1,500+ apps with deep field-level access. Lutra has 50+ but covers the common SaaS stack. If your workflow needs an obscure integration, Make wins.
  • Complex branching: Make's visual router/filter/router pattern handles 10-branch conditional logic cleanly. Lutra can do it but the natural-language approach gets unwieldy past 3-4 branches.
  • Native LLM steps: Lutra's killer feature. Summarization, classification, extraction, and routing are first-class. Make has OpenAI/Anthropic modules but they're bolt-ons, not native.
  • Pricing model: Make charges per operation (cheap at low volume, expensive at scale). Lutra charges per workflow (predictable, scales better).
  • Error handling: Make has robust error routes, retry policies, and break statements. Lutra is still catching up here.

The verdict

If your workflow has an LLM doing real work in the middle — summarize → classify → route → email — Lutra ships faster and runs cheaper. If your workflow is pure plumbing with complex branching, rare integrations, or strict error-handling requirements — Make is still the right tool. For most teams, the answer is both: Lutra for the AI-heavy stuff, Make for the integration backbone.

Winner: Lutra for AI workflows · Make for plumbing

How we tested

We migrated 12 production workflows from Make to Lutra over 8 weeks, then back to Make where Lutra couldn't handle the complexity.
