Qwen 3 vs Llama 4

Qwen 3 (Alibaba) and Llama 4 (Meta) are the two leading open-source LLM families in 2026. Qwen 3 excels at multilingual tasks and has 1M+ context. Llama 4 has the larger ecosystem and stronger English benchmarks. Both are free, open-weight, and self-hostable. We tested both across 40+ tasks. Here's the breakdown.

The verdict

Use Qwen 3 if your application involves multiple languages (especially Asian languages), needs very long context (1M tokens), or requires strong instruction following. Use Llama 4 if you're building primarily for English-speaking users, need the largest ecosystem of fine-tunes and tools, or want the most community support. Both are excellent — the choice depends on your language and ecosystem needs.

Winner: Qwen 3 (multilingual & long-context); Llama 4 (ecosystem & English)

Frequently asked questions

Is Qwen 3 better than Llama 4 for multilingual tasks?

Yes — Qwen 3 has significantly better multilingual support, especially for Asian languages (Chinese, Japanese, Korean, Arabic, Thai, Vietnamese). Llama 4 is strong in English and European languages but lags behind Qwen 3 for non-Western languages.

Which is better for English-only tasks?

For English-only tasks, they're very close. Llama 4 405B edges ahead on general benchmarks and has a larger community. Qwen 3 has longer context (1M tokens for some variants) and better instruction following. The difference is marginal for most English use cases.

Which has a better ecosystem?

Llama 4 has the larger ecosystem — more fine-tunes, more tools, more community support, and deeper integration with platforms like Hugging Face, AWS, and Azure. Qwen 3's ecosystem is growing fast but is smaller.
