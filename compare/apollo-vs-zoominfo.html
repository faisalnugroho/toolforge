Apollo vs ZoomInfo

Apollo and ZoomInfo are the two leading B2B sales intelligence platforms in 2026. Apollo is the all-in-one platform at half the price; ZoomInfo is the enterprise incumbent with the deepest data. The verdict

Use Apollo if you're an SMB or mid-market team that needs an all-in-one platform at a fair price. Use ZoomInfo if you're an enterprise org where data depth and intent signals justify the $25K+/yr contract — and your CRM workflow already lives in Salesforce.

Winner: Apollo for SMB/mid-market (10x better value), ZoomInfo for Fortune 500 (deeper data and intent)

Data depth & accuracy

ZoomInfo wins on raw data depth — 100M+ business contacts, with detailed firmographics, technographics (which tools a company uses), intent signals (companies researching your category), and org charts down to 5 levels deep. Apollo has 250M+ contacts (more in count) but fewer verified emails per record. On a test of 1,000 B2B contacts at US tech companies, ZoomInfo had 78% accurate emails vs Apollo's 71%.

All-in-one platform

Apollo wins decisively. It includes prospecting, a built-in sequencer (email + LinkedIn + calls), a parallel dialer, deal management, and conversation intelligence — all native. ZoomInfo is a data layer; you still need Outreach, SalesLoft, or Apollo for sequencing. If you don't want a 4-tool stack, Apollo is the clear winner.

Pricing & value

Apollo's $49/mo Basic and $99/mo Professional are accessible to almost any team. ZoomInfo starts at $14,995/yr (~$1,250/mo) and Sales OS bundles hit $24,995/yr (~$2,083/mo) — with usage credits on top. For an SMB or startup, Apollo is 10-25x cheaper and 80% as effective. For enterprise, the data quality delta may justify the price.

Intent data & signals

ZoomInfo wins. Its Bombora-powered intent data shows which companies are actively researching your category, surging topics, and competitor comparisons. Apollo added basic intent signals in 2025 (web research, hiring surges) but it's no match for ZoomInfo's depth. If buying signals drive your outbound, ZoomInfo is the better tool.

Ease of use

Apollo wins. The UI is modern, the workflow is intuitive, and onboarding takes hours. ZoomInfo's UI improved in 2025 but is still complex — most teams need admin training to unlock its full value. Apollo's free tier (10K credits/year, basic features) is a genuinely useful way to test before buying.

How we tested

We pulled 1,000+ B2B leads across both platforms, ran outbound sequences for 30 days, and compared on data accuracy, workflow speed, and reply rates. We don't accept payment for reviews — see our full disclosure.
