Notion vs Roam

Notion and Roam Research represent two philosophies of knowledge management. Notion is the structured, all-in-one workspace. Roam is the networked thought tool for researchers and thinkers. We used both for 60 days to find which is better for different types of work.

The verdict

Use Notion if you need a team workspace — docs, databases, project boards, wikis, and client portals in one tool. Use Roam Research if you're an individual researcher, writer, or deep thinker who wants to connect ideas across notes — its bidirectional linking creates a personal knowledge graph that Notion can't replicate.

Winner: Notion for teams, Roam for solo knowledge workers

Key differences

Structure vs network: Notion is hierarchical — pages within pages, databases with properties. Roam is a graph — every page links to every other page bidirectionally. Notion is better for organized, structured information. Roam is better for emergent, connective thinking.

Daily notes: Roam's signature feature. Start every day with a blank page, write notes, tag pages with [[brackets]], and Roam automatically builds connections. Notion has a "daily journal" template but it's not the core workflow.

Databases: Notion wins hands down. Filterable, sortable databases with views (table, board, calendar, gallery) are Notion's killer feature. Roam has no database concept — everything is a page with block references.

Collaboration: Notion is built for teams — comments, mentions, permissions, shared workspaces. Roam is a solo tool (Believer plan adds limited sharing, but it's not the focus).

AI features: Notion AI ($10/mo add-on) can write, summarize, and answer questions about your workspace. Roam has no native AI features — you'll need to pair it with ChatGPT or Claude.

Mobile: Notion's mobile app is excellent and fully featured. Roam's mobile experience is functional but slow and limited.

How we tested

Both tools were used as our primary knowledge management system for 60 days. We tracked note-taking speed, retrieval ease, team collaboration quality, and overall workflow integration. We don't accept payment for reviews — see our full disclosure.
