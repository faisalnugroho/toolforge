The verdict

Use Ideogram if you need perfectly-rendered text in images — posters, ads, social graphics, book covers. Use Recraft if you need a design tool that produces vector + raster output for brand systems, icon sets, and multi-format exports. Ideogram wins on text. Recraft wins on design systems.

Text in Images

Ideogram wins decisively. Across 25 prompts requiring accurate typography (quotes, headlines, brand names), Ideogram produced correct spelling in 92% of outputs. Recraft hit 78%. Neither is perfect, but Ideogram is the clear winner for posters, ads, and social graphics where text is the point.

Vector Output

Recraft wins outright. It produces true SVG vector output, which Ideogram doesn't. For brand work that needs to scale (logos, icons, packaging), Recraft is the only choice. You can hand the SVG to a designer or import it directly into Figma/Illustrator.

Style Consistency

Recraft wins. Brand kit features let you lock in colors, fonts, and styles across generations. Ideogram 2.0 added style references but they're less consistent. If you're producing 50 social posts in one brand voice, Recraft is faster.

Pricing

Ideogram is cheaper. Free tier is generous (~25 prompts/day). Basic at $8/mo vs. Recraft Pro at $12/mo. For pure image generation, Ideogram wins on price. For brand work, Recraft's higher price buys real productivity gains.

Photo Realism

Ideogram wins on photorealism. Recraft is more design-oriented; its outputs lean stylized/illustrative. For AI photo generation, Ideogram is closer to Midjourney. For design-first outputs, Recraft wins.

Winner: Ideogram for text-in-image, Recraft for design systems

How we tested

Both tools were tested across 100 prompts covering typography, brand systems, vector output, and photorealism. Outputs were scored on accuracy, consistency, and design utility. We don't accept payment for reviews — see our full disclosure.
