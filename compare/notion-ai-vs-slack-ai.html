Notion AI vs Slack AI

Both Notion and Slack bolted an AI assistant onto their surfaces in 2023–2024. By 2026 they've diverged sharply: Notion AI is a writing & knowledge-search assistant living in your docs; Slack AI is a conversation-summarizer and channel-search assistant. We ran both for 8 weeks across a 40-person org. Here's where each actually earns its keep.

The verdict

This isn't really an "either/or" — it's a question of where your team's long-term knowledge lives. If decisions get written down in Notion, Notion AI's doc-grounded Q&A is more valuable. If decisions live in Slack threads, Slack AI's recap is more valuable. For most teams in 2026 the honest answer is: pay for whichever surface is the source of truth, and skip the other. Many small teams skip both and use Claude/ChatGPT instead.

Winner: Whichever surface your team already treats as the source of truth

How we tested

We ran both add-ons across a 40-person org for 8 weeks. Tasks: drafting meeting notes, summarizing channels, finding decisions, writing release notes. We measured accuracy ("did the AI cite the right doc/message?"), time saved, and adoption (% of team using it weekly).
