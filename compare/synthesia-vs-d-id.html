Synthesia vs D-ID

Synthesia and D-ID are the two leading AI avatar video platforms in 2026. Both turn text into a talking-head video with a realistic presenter, but they target very different use cases. We tested both across 30+ training, marketing, and localization videos. Here's who wins and when.

The verdict

Use Synthesia for polished corporate training, multi-language localization, and any video where lip-sync quality matters. Use D-ID when you need to animate a still photo — historical figures, customer testimonials from a headshot, or chatbot avatars at low cost. For 80% of business video use cases, Synthesia wins on quality. For one-off experiments or photo animation, D-ID is 5x cheaper.

Winner: Synthesia for production video, D-ID for photo animation and prototyping

Avatar quality

Synthesia's stock avatars are full-body, well-lit, and look like professional corporate presenters. Lip-sync is near-perfect in 140+ languages. D-ID animates any photo you upload — the result is more "uncanny valley" and works best with high-resolution front-facing portraits. Synthesia has 230+ stock avatars; D-ID works with any face.

Pricing

Synthesia starts at $29/mo for 10 video minutes. D-ID's Lite plan is $5.9/mo for 5 minutes — 5x cheaper for short content. For ongoing corporate video production, Synthesia's per-minute pricing drops significantly on the Enterprise plan. For hobbyist or low-volume use, D-ID wins on cost.

How we tested

We produced the same 2-minute training video on each platform (English + Spanish versions), then a 30-second marketing clip animating a historical figure's portrait. We scored on avatar realism, lip-sync accuracy, language support, pricing, and editor UX. We don't accept payment for reviews — see our full disclosure.
