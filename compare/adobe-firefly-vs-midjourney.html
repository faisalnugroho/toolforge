The verdict

Use Adobe Firefly if you're a designer who needs commercial indemnification, Photoshop integration, or vector output. Use Midjourney if you're optimizing for raw image quality and don't care about indemnification. The best workflow in 2026 is actually using both — Midjourney for exploration, Firefly for client deliverables.

Frequently asked questions

Is Adobe Firefly better than Midjourney?

For commercial work, yes — Firefly is the only major image model with full IP indemnification. For raw image quality, no — Midjourney still wins on photorealism and artistic range.

Can I use Firefly images commercially?

Yes, on paid plans ($5+/mo). Adobe offers full IP indemnification — if you get sued, they cover you. Free tier is for personal/evaluation only.

Can I use Midjourney images commercially?

Yes, on paid plans ($10+/mo) you own the assets. But Midjourney is currently facing multiple copyright lawsuits, which creates legal ambiguity. Firefly is the safer pick for client work.

Which is better for designers?

Firefly. Photoshop Generative Fill is a daily-use feature that has no equivalent in Midjourney. If you already pay for Creative Cloud, Firefly is essentially free.
