Qwen vs DeepSeek

Qwen and DeepSeek are the two best open-weight AI families from China. Both are free to use, both match GPT-4 on most benchmarks, and both are MIT-licensed. But they optimize for different things: Qwen has the best vision model and free chat with image gen. DeepSeek has the best reasoning model and the cheapest API. We benchmarked both for 60 days. Here's who wins and when.

The verdict

Use DeepSeek for reasoning-heavy applications, cheap API, and math/coding tasks. Use Qwen for vision/multimodal work, Chinese-language apps, free chat with image gen, and self-hosting with flexible model sizes. For most use cases, you'll want to fine-tune Qwen for vision and call DeepSeek for reasoning in the same app. Winner: Tie — both are best-in-class open models.

Reasoning and math

DeepSeek R1 is the better reasoning model — it scores higher on AIME, Codeforces, and GPQA. Qwen3 is close behind but loses by 5-10% on most reasoning benchmarks. Winner: DeepSeek.

Vision and multimodal

Qwen2.5-VL is the best open vision model in the world — handles documents, charts, video, and multi-image inputs better than DeepSeek's Janus. If you need a vision-language model you can self-host, Qwen wins. Winner: Qwen, decisively.

Free consumer chat

Qwen Chat is more capable for free users — it includes image generation (Wanx), file uploads, and web search. DeepSeek is text-only on the free tier. Winner: Qwen.

API pricing

DeepSeek is $0.14 per million input tokens. Qwen Alibaba Cloud pricing is $0.06 per million for Qwen-Plus (cheaper) but $0.40 for Qwen-Max. Depends on model. For cheap inference at scale, DeepSeek is generally cheaper. Winner: DeepSeek, slightly.

Chinese-language performance

Qwen is the best Chinese-language AI in the world, period. It scores significantly higher than DeepSeek on C-Eval, CMMLU, and Chinese-language reasoning. DeepSeek is good but not Qwen-level. Winner: Qwen, by a wide margin.

Open weights and self-hosting

Both are fully open-weight (MIT/Apache license). Qwen offers more sizes (0.5B, 1.5B, 7B, 72B, 235B MoE) for flexible deployment. DeepSeek offers V3 (671B) and R1 distilled variants. Winner: Qwen, for model variety.

How we tested

Both tools were used for 30-60 days across real-world tasks. We scored on quality, ease of use, features, pricing, and support. We don't accept payment for reviews — see our full disclosure.
