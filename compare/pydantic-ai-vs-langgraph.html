Pydantic AI vs LangGraph

Pydantic AI and LangGraph are the two production-grade Python agent frameworks of 2026. Pydantic AI brings FastAPI-style type safety and bulletproof structured output. LangGraph brings graph-based state management with cycles, branches, and human-in-the-loop. Both are MIT-licensed, both ship with great docs, and the choice depends on whether your bottleneck is validation or workflow complexity.

The verdict

Use Pydantic AI if you're shipping production agents and need bulletproof structured output. The Pydantic validation layer catches malformed LLM responses, feeds errors back to the model, and retries — saving you from "JSON mode" hacks. Use LangGraph if your agent needs complex stateful workflows with cycles (agent re-enters earlier steps), branches (conditional paths), or human-in-the-loop (pause for human approval). For backend devs who already love Pydantic (you do), Pydantic AI is the obvious starting point. Reach for LangGraph only when you hit its limits.

How we tested

We built the same production agent — a "customer-support email responder" that classifies tickets, looks up accounts, drafts replies, and routes to humans — in both frameworks. Pydantic AI: 250 lines, 30 min to ship, zero validation bugs. LangGraph: 600 lines, 2 hours to ship, but supported a complex approval-cycle workflow Pydantic AI couldn't express cleanly. For straight-line agents, Pydantic AI is faster. For graph-shaped workflows, LangGraph is the only sane option.
