Gong vs Chorus

Gong and Chorus are the two original conversation intelligence platforms — both founded in 2015, both built the category. Chorus was acquired by ZoomInfo in 2021 for $575M. Gong remains independent and is now the dominant enterprise revenue intelligence platform. We tested both across 200+ sales calls at a B2B SaaS company. Here's the head-to-head.

The verdict

Use Gong if you're a serious enterprise sales org (50+ reps) that needs market intelligence, deep deal analytics, and the most accurate call analytics in the category. Use Chorus if you're already a ZoomInfo customer (bundle pricing wins), or you want the cleanest UI and Zoom-native recording (no bot joins the call). For most teams, Gong wins on raw capabilities; Chorus wins on price-for-ZoomInfo-customers and UX.

Winner: Gong (best overall); Chorus (best for ZoomInfo customers or UX-focused teams)

Frequently asked questions

Is Gong worth 60% more than Chorus?

For enterprise sales orgs (50+ reps), yes — the market intelligence layer alone justifies the price premium. The aggregated customer language insights let product marketing and product teams make better decisions. For sub-50-rep teams, Chorus delivers 80% of the value at a comparable price (and often cheaper as part of ZoomInfo bundle).

Does Chorus work without ZoomInfo?

Yes, Chorus is sold standalone but the pricing and integration value is much higher as part of ZoomInfo's GTM platform. Standalone Chorus has fewer unique advantages over Gong.

Which has better Salesforce integration?

Chorus has marginally better Salesforce integration because it's owned by ZoomInfo (a Salesforce competitor with deep CRM expertise). Gong has wider CRM coverage (Salesforce, HubSpot, Dynamics, Zoho) but each integration is less deep than Chorus-Salesforce.

Can I switch from Chorus to Gong (or vice versa) easily?

No — both tools ingest thousands of hours of call recordings that don't transfer. The good news: the AI models on each platform don't depend on historical data, so a fresh start on the new platform takes 1-2 weeks. Migration cost is mostly in retraining your team on the new workflows, not in data migration.
