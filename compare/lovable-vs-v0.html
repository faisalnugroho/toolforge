Lovable vs v0

Lovable and v0 are the two AI app builders that took the indie hacker world by storm in 2025-2026. Both can take a prompt and ship a working web app. Both produce clean React + Tailwind code. Both have a 'wow' moment when you see your first generated app. But they serve different stages of the build: Lovable is full-stack (backend + auth + database), v0 is UI-first (components and pages you paste into your Next.js project).

The verdict

Pick Lovable if you're a non-engineer founder who wants to ship a full-stack MVP from a prompt. Backend, auth, database, and Stripe are all built in. Pick v0 if you're a designer or frontend dev who wants to generate clean React components and pages for your Next.js project. v0 produces more polished UI per prompt; Lovable produces more complete apps.

Who should pick Lovable?

Non-technical founders, indie hackers, designers with light coding skills. Lovable's full-stack nature means you can ship a SaaS MVP in a weekend. The Supabase integration gives you auth + database + storage for free. The downside: complex logic still requires you to know what you're doing.

Who should pick v0?

Frontend devs, UI/UX designers, design engineers. v0 produces the cleanest, most polished UI in the AI generator category — better than Bolt.new or Lovable for pure visual quality. The components paste cleanly into a Next.js project. The downside: no backend, no auth, no full-stack. You'll need to wire that up yourself.
