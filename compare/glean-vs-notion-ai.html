Glean vs Notion AI

Glean and Notion AI both claim to be 'AI search for your work' — but they're not even in the same category. Glean searches across every app in your stack (Slack, Drive, Salesforce, GitHub, etc.). Notion AI searches only inside your Notion workspace. We tested both on a 200-person company's real workflows. Here's who wins for whom.

✓ Glean pros

  • Best-in-class federated search across 100+ enterprise apps
  • Respects every app's permission model — secure by default
  • Glean Assistant turns search into action (drafts emails, creates tickets)
  • Enterprise-proven: Databricks, Grammarly, Plaid, Sony

✗ Glean cons

  • Enterprise pricing ($15/user/mo) — small teams priced out
  • Requires security review (4-8 weeks to deploy)
  • Quality depends on connector coverage of your stack

✓ Notion AI pros

  • Trivial to set up — just toggle Notion AI in your workspace
  • Great for Q&A over your Notion docs, wikis, and databases
  • Cheap add-on ($10/user/mo) for teams already on Notion
  • Native to Notion — UI is consistent with the rest of Notion

✗ Notion AI cons

  • Only searches Notion — useless for cross-app queries
  • Can't search Slack, Drive, Salesforce, etc.
  • Limited action capabilities vs Glean Assistant

The verdict

For companies with 100+ employees using 5+ apps: Glean wins. Federated search across 100+ apps is what you need. Notion AI is irrelevant because your knowledge isn't all in Notion. For small teams that live entirely in Notion: Notion AI wins. It's $5 cheaper per user, instant to set up, and works great if all your knowledge is already in Notion. The decision is simple: where does your knowledge live? If it's all in Notion, use Notion AI. If it's spread across many apps, use Glean.
