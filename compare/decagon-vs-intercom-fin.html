Decagon vs Intercom Fin

Decagon and Intercom Fin are the two leading AI customer support agents in 2026. They take similar approaches (autonomous resolution, not deflection), but they differ on pricing model, customization, and platform. Decagon is the enterprise option. Fin is the default for the millions of companies already on Intercom. We tested both on real support tickets.

✓ Decagon pros

  • 60-80% autonomous resolution — saves real headcount
  • Deep customization for complex enterprise workflows
  • Native to Zendesk, Intercom, Salesforce — no rip-and-replace
  • Used by Hertz, Eventbrite, Bilt — proven at scale

✗ Decagon cons

  • Enterprise pricing ($1K+/mo) — startups and SMBs priced out
  • Requires clean knowledge base and API access
  • Long onboarding (4-8 weeks typical)

✓ Intercom Fin pros

  • $0.99 per resolution — perfect for variable volume
  • Native to Intercom — zero integration work
  • Fast to set up (1-2 weeks if you have a clean Intercom Articles KB)
  • Multi-language support (40+ languages) is best-in-class

✗ Intercom Fin cons

  • Intercom only — useless if you're on Zendesk or Salesforce
  • Lower resolution rate than Decagon (40-60% vs 60-80%)
  • Per-resolution pricing can get expensive at scale

The verdict

For enterprises with 10K+ tickets/month, complex workflows, and a multi-helpdesk stack: Decagon wins. The customization and resolution rate justify the enterprise price. For companies already on Intercom, especially growing SaaS startups: Fin wins. The native integration, pay-per-resolution pricing, and fast setup are perfect for that use case. For everyone else (not on Intercom, not enterprise), look at Zendesk AI (if you're on Zendesk) or Sierra (Bret Taylor's competitor to both).
