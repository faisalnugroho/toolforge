GPT-OSS vs Llama 4

The two heaviest open-weights LLMs of 2025-2026. GPT-OSS is OpenAI's first foray into open weights — a 120B MoE with Apache 2.0. Llama 4 is Meta's flagship — a 405B dense model with the Llama Community License. We ran identical prompts to see which one wins on quality, speed, and licensing.

The verdict

GPT-OSS wins on license (true Apache 2.0 vs Llama's "700M users" clause), hardware footprint (single H100 vs multi-GPU cluster), and the fine-tune ecosystem. Llama 4 wins on raw benchmark quality, multimodal capability (vision + audio in-box), and multilingual coverage. For most builders in 2026, GPT-OSS is the practical default; Llama 4 is the choice when you need frontier-class quality and have the GPUs to run it.

Winner: GPT-OSS for shipping; Llama 4 for benchmarks

Where GPT-OSS pulls ahead

  • License clarity — Apache 2.0 with no asterisks. Llama's "700M MAU = ask Meta first" clause trips up enterprise legal teams.
  • Hardware cost — 120B MoE runs on a single H100 or a 4090 (quantized). Llama 4 405B needs an 8-GPU node minimum.
  • Function calling — native OpenAI-format tool use drops into existing stacks with zero prompt engineering.
  • Fine-tune ecosystem — Hugging Face is flooded with GPT-OSS LoRAs for coding, agents, roleplay.
  • Inference speed — ~30 tok/s on H100 vs ~12 tok/s for Llama 4 405B on equivalent hardware.

Where Llama 4 pulls ahead

  • Raw quality — beats GPT-OSS by 3-5 points on MMLU, GPQA, and HumanEval.
  • Multimodal — vision and audio in-box. GPT-OSS is text-only.
  • Multilingual — strong on 20+ languages; GPT-OSS is English-first.
  • Context window — 256K vs 128K. Matters for full-codebase tasks.
  • Safety tuning — less aggressive refusal. GPT-OSS inherits OpenAI's conservative defaults.
