The verdict

Synthesia is the safer pick for most teams: more avatars (230+), broader language coverage (140+), better lip-sync, and the proven reliability of the category leader. It's what you want for compliance training, onboarding, and anything that needs to look polished in dozens of locales. Hour One wins on workflow — its template-first builder is faster for marketing teams who want to spin up branded social clips and product demos without touching a timeline. If your use case is "many languages, many regions" pick Synthesia; if it's "many videos, same brand, fast turnaround" pick Hour One.

Winner: Synthesia for scale & languages; Hour One for template-driven marketing video

How we tested

We wrote one 2-minute product-explainer script and produced a finished video in each platform. We judged avatar realism, lip-sync accuracy, render time, language switching, and how much manual cleanup the output needed.

Where Synthesia pulls ahead

  • 230+ avatars — the largest library, with diverse ages, ethnicities, and attire.
  • 140+ languages — translate once, render in dozens of locales without re-recording.
  • Custom avatars — film yourself and create a digital twin (Enterprise plan).
  • Better lip-sync — mouth movements track the script more convincingly in our tests.
  • Lower entry price — $22/mo Starter vs Hour One's $25/mo Lite.

Where Hour One pulls ahead

  • Template-first workflow — start from a branded layout and swap content in minutes.
  • Reals feature — auto-generate video variations for A/B testing ad creative.
  • Faster render times — our 2-minute clip rendered in ~3 minutes vs ~8 in Synthesia.
  • Brand kit integration — logos, colors, and fonts applied consistently across templates.
  • Social-first aspect ratios — built-in 9:16 and 1:1 exports for Reels, TikTok, Shorts.
