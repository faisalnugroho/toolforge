The verdict

Use NotebookLM when you have a corpus of your own sources to analyze — research papers, internal docs, customer interviews. Use Perplexity when you need live web research with citations — competitive intel, current events, market data. They're complementary, not competing — most power users have both.

Source Grounding

NotebookLM is strictly better here. It quotes from YOUR sources with precise citations, refuses to hallucinate outside them, and lets you ask 50 questions about the same corpus. Perplexity grounds in web sources but can synthesize across them, occasionally losing the thread. For "analyze these 30 papers" tasks, NotebookLM is unmatched.

Live Web Research

Perplexity wins outright. NotebookLM has no live web access (you must upload sources). Perplexity's Pro Search with deep research mode runs 20+ searches, reads 50+ sources, and produces a cited report in under 5 minutes. For "what happened this week" or "current competitor pricing," Perplexity is the only option.

Audio Overviews

NotebookLM wins. Its podcast-style audio summary feature is genuinely magical — feed it 20 sources and get a 12-minute conversational deep dive you can listen to on a walk. Perplexity has no audio mode. For commuters, this is the killer feature.

Pricing

NotebookLM is free (just needs a Google account). Perplexity is free for basic; Pro is $20/mo for Pro Search, file upload, and GPT-4/Claude access. For most users, NotebookLM + Perplexity free is enough. Power researchers pay for both.

Follow-up Questions

Both are strong. Perplexity's thread-style follow-ups are better for open-ended exploration. NotebookLM's follow-ups stay tightly bound to your uploaded sources, which is what you want for serious research.

Winner: Tie — NotebookLM for owned sources, Perplexity for live web

How we tested

Both tools were tested across 50 research tasks spanning academic literature, market analysis, due diligence, and competitive intel over 6 weeks. We scored on accuracy, citation quality, source grounding, and audio quality. We don't accept payment for reviews.
