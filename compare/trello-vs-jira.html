Trello vs Jira

Both Trello and Jira are made by Atlassian, but they serve different needs. Trello is the simple kanban board for visual task management. Jira is the full-featured issue tracker for software engineering teams. We compared both across 40+ criteria to help you pick the right one.

The verdict

Use Trello for non-technical teams, personal projects, and simple task management. Use Jira for software engineering teams that need sprints, backlogs, story points, and agile reporting. For small teams (<10): Jira's free tier is excellent. For everyone else who doesn't need agile: Trello is simpler and faster.

Winner: Trello for simplicity · Jira for engineering

Where Trello wins

Trello's kanban board is the gold standard for visual task management. Drag-and-drop cards, lists, and labels are intuitive for anyone — no training needed. Power-Ups (integrations) extend functionality without complexity. The Butler automation feature lets you create rules without code. For marketing teams, personal projects, and small businesses, Trello is the right amount of structure.

Where Jira wins

Jira is built for software development. Sprint planning, backlogs, story points, burndown charts, velocity tracking — all native. Custom workflows, issue types, and fields mean it adapts to any engineering process. Jira's reporting and analytics are far deeper than Trello. Integration with Bitbucket, GitHub, and Confluence creates a seamless dev workflow. For agile engineering teams, Jira is the industry standard.
