The verdict

Use Notion if you want a flexible, beautiful workspace that handles docs, project management, and databases in one place. Use Confluence if your engineering team already uses Jira and you need structured documentation with enterprise permissions and audit trails. For startups: Notion. For enterprise engineering: Confluence.

Winner: Notion for flexibility · Confluence for enterprise

Where Notion wins

Notion's block-based editor is more flexible — pages can be databases, kanban boards, calendars, or wikis. The AI integration (Notion AI) is more mature and useful for drafting and summarizing. Templates and community are larger. Pricing is straightforward and the free tier is generous for small teams.

Where Confluence wins

Confluence's deep Jira integration is unbeatable for engineering teams — link tickets, embed roadmaps, and track sprint docs automatically. Enterprise features like granular permissions, audit logs, and compliance certifications are superior. Page templates for technical documentation (RFCs, runbooks, architecture decisions) are built-in and polished.

How we tested

Both tools were used for 30+ days across real team workflows — documentation, project tracking, meeting notes, and knowledge bases. We scored on flexibility, collaboration, AI features, integrations, pricing, and enterprise readiness. See our full disclosure.
