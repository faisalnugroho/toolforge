ElevenLabs vs Suno

ElevenLabs and Suno are both leaders in AI audio — but they solve different problems. ElevenLabs makes the most realistic AI voices. Suno makes full songs from text prompts. We tested both across 100+ generations to help you decide.

Where ElevenLabs wins

  • Voice quality: ElevenLabs produces the most realistic AI voices available. Indistinguishable from human narration in many cases.
  • Voice cloning: Clone any voice from a 1-minute sample. Suno doesn't offer voice cloning.
  • Multi-language: Supports 30+ languages with natural-sounding pronunciation. Suno's lyrics are primarily English.
  • Dubbing: Automatically dub videos into other languages while preserving the original voice.
  • API access: Developer-friendly API for integrating TTS into apps. Suno's API is more limited.

Where Suno wins

  • Full songs: Suno generates complete songs with vocals, instruments, and structure (verse-chorus-bridge). ElevenLabs only does speech.
  • Music genres: Pop, rock, jazz, electronic, hip-hop — Suno handles them all with impressive quality.
  • Lyrics generation: Write your own lyrics or let Suno generate them. Full creative control over content.
  • Speed: Generate a full 2-minute song in under 30 seconds. ElevenLabs focuses on short-form audio.
  • Cost for music: At $10/mo for 500 songs, Suno is far cheaper than hiring musicians or licensing stock music.

The verdict

These tools aren't really competitors — they're complementary. Use ElevenLabs for voiceovers, narration, audiobooks, and dubbing. Use Suno for songs, jingles, and background music. If you create audio content (podcasts, videos, games), you'll want both. Total cost: $15/mo.

Winner: Different tools — use both
