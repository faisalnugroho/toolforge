Granola vs Otter.ai

Granola and Otter are both meeting AI tools, but they take opposite approaches. Granola is for people who want to write notes and have AI polish them. Otter is for people who want a full transcript and AI summary with no writing.

The fundamental difference

Granola's premise: you take rough notes during the meeting, and the AI uses your context plus the (optional) audio recording to expand them into a polished, structured document after the call. The result reads like you spent 30 minutes on it.

Otter's premise: Otter joins your meeting, transcribes everything, identifies speakers, and writes the summary for you. You don't take notes at all. The result is comprehensive but often feels like a wall of text.

Quality of output

Granola wins on output quality. Because you wrote the bones, the notes reflect what you actually thought was important. Otter's notes include every 'um' and tangent because it doesn't know what's noise.

Search and retrieval

Otter wins on search. If you need to find a specific moment from a 6-month-old meeting, Otter's full transcript + click-to-jump is unbeatable. Granola's polished output is great for sharing but less searchable.

Privacy

Granola wins on privacy. Granola's audio is processed and discarded. Otter stores transcripts and audio indefinitely on its servers (unless you pay for Enterprise). If your meetings contain sensitive info, Granola is the safer call.

Pricing

Both have usable free tiers. Otter's free is 300 min/mo, Granola's is 25 meetings/mo. Granola's paid ($14/mo) is cheaper than Otter Pro ($16.99/mo).

The verdict

Pick Granola if you want notes that sound like you and you care about privacy. Pick Otter if you need full transcripts, search across 6 months of meetings, or real-time notes in the meeting. Power users often use both — Otter for the searchable archive, Granola for the shareable output.

FAQ

Can I use both?

Yes. Many users run Otter as the searchable archive and Granola for the meeting's output. They don't conflict.

Which is better for sales calls?

Otter. The full transcript lets you find exact quotes from prospects, and the CRM integrations (HubSpot, Salesforce) are deeper.

Which is better for 1:1s?

Granola. The output reads like a clean recap, not a transcript. 1:1s rarely need verbatim.
