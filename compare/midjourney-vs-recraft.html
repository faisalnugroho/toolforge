Midjourney vs Recraft

Midjourney is the artistic, painterly AI image generator. Recraft is the design-focused tool with vector output, brand kits, and typography. The choice depends on whether you want art or design.

Output type

Midjourney is raster only. PNGs and JPEGs of stunning, painterly images. Recraft outputs vectors and real text. SVGs, vectors, and crisp typography — the kind of thing you can drop into a Figma file and ship to a developer.

Aesthetic vs. utility

Midjourney wins on raw aesthetic. The default output looks like fine art. Recraft wins on utility. Recraft's output is more like a designer's work — clean, on-brand, and ready to use.

Brand consistency

Recraft wins decisively. Recraft has a brand kit feature where you upload your brand's colors, fonts, and styles, and it generates images in your style. Midjourney has --sref but it's much less precise.

Text in images

Recraft wins on text. Recraft can render real, readable text in images. Midjourney still mangles text most of the time.

Community and ecosystem

Midjourney wins on community. 20M+ users, a huge Discord with feedback, prompts, and inspiration. Recraft is smaller but the community is more design-focused.

Pricing

Both around $10-12/mo. Recraft has a generous free tier. Midjourney's free tier was removed in 2024.

The verdict

Pick Midjourney if you want beautiful, artistic images and don't need them to fit a brand system. Pick Recraft if you do design work and need on-brand, vector, typography-aware output. Many design teams use both — Midjourney for hero images, Recraft for everything else.

How we tested

Both tools were used for 30-60 days across real-world tasks. We scored on quality, ease of use, features, pricing, and support. We don't accept payment for reviews — see our full disclosure.

FAQ

Which is better for marketing graphics?

Recraft. The brand kit and typography make it production-ready out of the box.

Which is better for book covers?

Midjourney. The painterly quality is hard to beat for one-off art pieces.

Can I use both?

Yes. Many designers use Midjourney for inspiration and concept art, then Recraft for final design assets.
