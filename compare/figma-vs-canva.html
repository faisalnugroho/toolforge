Figma vs Canva

Figma and Canva are the two dominant design tools in 2026 — but they serve different users. Figma is the professional's tool (UI/UX, product design, design systems). Canva is for everyone else (social media, presentations, marketing materials). Both added major AI features in 2025-2026. Here's which one to pick.

The verdict

Use Figma if you're a designer building interfaces, design systems, or product UI. Use Canva if you're a marketer, founder, or non-designer who needs to ship social posts, decks, and marketing materials fast. Different tools for different jobs — both are excellent at what they do.

Winner: Tie — different audiences, both are category leaders

Core differences

Figma is built for designing screens — vector editing, components, auto-layout, design tokens, dev mode. The free tier is genuinely powerful for personal projects. Canva is built for creating assets — social posts, presentations, posters, marketing materials. Massive template library (250K+) and dead-simple editor that anyone can use.

AI features in 2026

Figma AI: First Draft generates full UI mockups from text prompts; AI-powered auto-layout suggestions; smart component naming; text-to-image for placeholders. Canva AI: Magic Design (text-to-template), Magic Write (copy generation), Magic Edit (image manipulation), Beat Sync for video. Canva's AI is broader; Figma's AI is more focused on the design workflow.

Collaboration

Both are real-time collaborative. Figma pioneered multiplayer design — multiple cursors, comments on components, dev handoff. Canva has similar collaboration plus brand kit management for marketing teams. Figma's collaboration is built for designers working on product teams; Canva's is built for marketing teams and stakeholders.

Learning curve

Canva: 15 minutes to productive. Figma: 5-10 hours to productive, 50+ hours to master. If you're not a designer by trade, Canva is the obvious choice. If you're designing a product UI or maintaining a design system, Figma's complexity pays off.

Pricing comparison

Both have generous free tiers. Figma free: 3 design files, unlimited viewers. Canva free: 250K+ templates, 5GB storage, basic editor. Figma Pro ($15/mo): unlimited files, components, dev mode. Canva Pro ($13/mo): 100M+ premium assets, brand kit, background remover. Figma Org ($45/mo/user): design systems, SSO. Canva Teams ($200/yr for 5 users): brand management, approvals.
