Superhuman vs Spark

Superhuman and Spark are both AI-powered email clients, but they take opposite approaches. Superhuman optimizes for individual speed — keyboard shortcuts, AI triage, send later — to make solo email users 2x faster. Spark optimizes for teams — shared inboxes, delegation, AI drafts — to make team email collaborative. We tested both for 30 days across 500+ emails.

Superhuman: pros & cons

✓ Works

  • Fastest email client ever built
  • Keyboard shortcuts for every action
  • AI triage that actually works (Split Inbox)
  • Beautiful design and animations
  • Read receipts and send later (built-in)

✗ Doesn't

  • $30/mo is the most expensive email client
  • No team features (deliberately solo)
  • No shared inboxes or delegation
  • iOS/Mac only (no Windows/Linux)
  • Limited integrations

Spark: pros & cons

✓ Works

  • Free tier is actually useful
  • Team collaboration features (shared inbox)
  • AI compose and reply drafts
  • Works on Mac, Windows, iOS, Android
  • Smart notifications (only pings for important mail)

✗ Doesn't

  • Not as fast as Superhuman
  • UI is busier
  • AI less sophisticated than Superhuman's
  • Limited customization
  • Some advanced features paywalled

The verdict

Use Superhuman if you process 100+ emails/day solo and need the fastest possible workflow — it's worth the $30/mo for founders, execs, and power users. Use Spark if you're on a team, need shared inboxes, or want a great free AI email client. For most people, Spark's free tier is the right starting point. Upgrade to Superhuman only when email becomes your biggest time sink.

How we tested

Both apps installed on real email accounts (Gmail + custom domain). Used for 30+ days across 500+ emails. Measured time-to-inbox-zero, AI feature usefulness, and overall satisfaction. Tested team features with shared inbox in Spark. We don't accept payment for reviews.
