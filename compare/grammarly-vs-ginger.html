Grammarly vs Ginger

Grammarly and Ginger are two of the oldest AI writing assistants. Both check grammar, spelling, and style — but they've diverged significantly. Grammarly has evolved into a full AI writing platform; Ginger has stayed focused on language learners and translation. We tested both across 500+ writing samples to see which catches more errors.

The verdict

Use Grammarly if you're a native English speaker who wants the best overall writing assistant with generative AI. Use Ginger if English is your second language and you need built-in translation, rephrasing, and a personal trainer. Grammarly is the better tool overall; Ginger is better value for ESL users.

Winner: Grammarly overall · Ginger for ESL

Where Grammarly wins

Grammarly's tone detection, clarity suggestions, and generative AI (GrammarlyGO) are significantly more advanced than Ginger's. The browser extension works everywhere — Gmail, Google Docs, LinkedIn, Twitter. Grammarly's plagiarism checker is more comprehensive. The brand voice and style guide features (Business plan) are enterprise-grade. For native English writers, Grammarly catches more subtle issues.

Where Ginger wins

Ginger's built-in translation (60+ languages) and rephrasing tool are designed for non-native speakers. The personal trainer feature adapts to your common mistakes and creates practice exercises — genuinely useful for language learning. Ginger is also significantly cheaper ($7.49 vs $12/mo). For ESL users who need translation alongside grammar checking, Ginger is the better value.

How we tested

Both tools were tested on 500+ writing samples including emails, essays, blog posts, and non-native English text. We measured error detection rate, false positives, suggestion quality, and user experience. See our full disclosure.
