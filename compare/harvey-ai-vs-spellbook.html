Harvey AI vs Spellbook

Harvey AI and Spellbook are the two most-funded legal AI startups in 2026 — and they're aimed at different jobs. Harvey (backed by Sequoia at $3B valuation) is the Swiss-army knife for biglaw: research, drafting, due diligence. Spellbook is the contract specialist: it lives inside Word and helps transactional lawyers draft and review agreements. Here's who wins for your practice.

✓ What Harvey AI does better

  • GPT-4 class reasoning on complex legal questions
  • Handles litigation, M&A, compliance — full range
  • Used by 40%+ of AmLaw 100 firms
  • Strong data privacy and SOC 2 compliance

✗ Where Harvey AI falls short

  • $100/mo is steep for solo practitioners
  • No native Word plugin — works via web app
  • Sometimes hallucinates on obscure case citations
  • Waitlist for new accounts

✓ What Spellbook does better

  • Lives inside Microsoft Word — no context switching
  • Trained specifically on contract language patterns
  • Cheaper than hiring a junior associate
  • Real-time redline suggestions on contract drafts

✗ Where Spellbook falls short

  • Limited to contract work — not a research tool
  • Less useful for litigation or compliance teams
  • Word-only (no Google Docs support)
  • Newer, smaller community than Harvey

The verdict

For litigators and biglaw doing research, memos, and complex analysis, Harvey AI is the clear winner — it's GPT-4 tuned for law and handles novel questions. For transactional lawyers drafting contracts all day, Spellbook is purpose-built for that workflow and lives where you work (Word). Both are worth $100/mo if you're billing $300+/hour.

Winner: Depends on practice area (litigation: Harvey · contracts: Spellbook)
