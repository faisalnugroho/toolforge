Slack vs Notion

The verdict

This isn't an either/or. Most successful teams use both: Slack for real-time communication and quick decisions, Notion for documentation and long-form knowledge. If budget forces a choice: Slack for communication-heavy teams (sales, support), Notion for documentation-heavy teams (engineering, design).

Winner: Use both — they complement, not compete

Where Slack wins

Real-time messaging is Slack's core. Channels, threads, huddles, and DMs are faster than any doc tool for quick back-and-forth. Slack AI summarizes threads, answers questions about your workspace, and finds information across channels. Integration ecosystem (2,600+ apps) is unmatched. For synchronous team communication, nothing beats it.

Where Notion wins

Notion is a knowledge management system, not a chat tool. Pages, databases, wikis, and project boards live in one place. Notion AI can draft docs, generate summaries, and answer questions about your workspace content. For asynchronous work, onboarding docs, and institutional memory, Notion is superior. Searchable, structured, and permanent — unlike Slack messages that scroll away.
