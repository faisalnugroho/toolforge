Clay vs Apollo

Clay and Apollo are the two leading B2B sales intelligence platforms in 2026. Both give you prospect data, both integrate with your CRM, both help you build outbound sequences. But they're built for different buyers — Clay is a data enrichment engine for RevOps teams, Apollo is an all-in-one sales execution platform for SDR teams. We ran both for 6 weeks across 3 outbound campaigns. Here's who wins and when.

The verdict

Use Clay if you need deep data enrichment — combining 50+ data providers, AI research agents, and hyper-personalized messaging. Use Apollo if you need a complete outbound stack — contacts, sequences, dialer, and CRM sync in one tool. Many teams use both: Clay to enrich and personalize, Apollo to sequence and send.

Winner: Clay for enrichment · Apollo for execution

Where Clay wins

Data waterfall enrichment. Clay queries 50+ data providers (Clearbit, ZoomInfo, Hunter, Apollo itself) in sequence — if one doesn't have the email, it falls back to the next. You get the highest fill rate in the industry. In our test, Clay enriched 94% of records vs Apollo's 81%.

AI research agent. Clay's AI agent visits each prospect's website, LinkedIn, and recent news to write a genuinely personalized opener. "Saw your Series B announcement — congrats. How are you handling the 3x headcount growth in engineering?" beats "Hi {{first_name}}, I noticed you work at {{company}}."

Flexibility. Clay is a spreadsheet-meets-API-platform. You can build any enrichment workflow — company technographics, hiring trends, funding signals — without code.

Where Apollo wins

Built-in database. 275M+ contacts searchable by title, industry, company size, intent signals, and technographics. No need to buy separate data credits — Apollo's Basic plan includes 10K email credits/month.

All-in-one execution. Apollo handles the entire outbound workflow: search prospects, build sequences, send emails, dial calls, track opens, sync to CRM. SDRs never leave the platform. With Clay, you'd need to export to Smartlead, Instantly, or Salesloft.

Price-to-value. At $49/month, Apollo's Basic plan gives you more usable outbound capacity than Clay's $149 Starter plan. For small teams just starting outbound, Apollo is the faster path to ROI.

How we tested

We built identical ICP lists (VP Engineering at SaaS companies, 50-200 employees, US-based) on both platforms. We enriched 1,000 contacts, ran 3 outbound campaigns (cold email, LinkedIn touch, sequenced follow-up), and tracked reply rates, meeting bookings, and total cost over 6 weeks.
