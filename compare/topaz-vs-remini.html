The verdict

Use Topaz if you're a photographer enhancing full scenes, need denoise + sharpen + upscale in one tool, and want a one-time payment. Use Remini if you have a stack of old family photos with faces to restore, want a mobile UX, or need a quick face cleanup. Topaz wins on quality; Remini wins on convenience and face-specific reconstruction.

Winner: Topaz for pro work, Remini for face-only restoration

How we tested

We ran 80 photo tests: 30 face crops from old family albums, 30 high-ISO wedding shots, 20 soft-focus wildlife frames. For face restoration on small JPEGs, Remini won by 20-30% in perceived detail. For full-scene RAW files at high ISO, Topaz was 2-3x better at preserving detail without mush. The $199 one-time vs $520/yr pricing alone makes Topaz the choice for any pro doing regular work.
