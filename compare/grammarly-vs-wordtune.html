Grammarly vs Wordtune

Grammarly and Wordtune are both AI writing assistants, but they approach writing differently. Grammarly is the spell-checker-plus — catches grammar, spelling, tone, and clarity issues across every app you write in. Wordtune is the rewriter — you write something rough and Wordtune offers 5-10 alternative phrasings with different tones. Different jobs, different tools.

✓ What Grammarly does better

  • Works in every browser, email client, and doc app
  • Best-in-class grammar and spelling detection
  • Tone detection is genuinely useful
  • Free tier is actually useful

✗ Where Grammarly falls short

  • Sometimes too aggressive — flags style as errors
  • Premium upsell is relentless
  • Limited rewriting capability vs Wordtune
  • Privacy concerns — sees everything you type

✓ What Wordtune does better

  • AI rephrasing offers 5-10 alternatives per sentence
  • Tone control (formal/casual/shorter/longer)
  • Excellent for non-native English speakers
  • Integrates with major writing platforms

✗ Where Wordtune falls short

  • Narrower feature set than Grammarly
  • Only works in supported apps (no system-wide)
  • Free tier is very limited (10 rewrites/day)
  • No grammar/spelling checking like Grammarly

The verdict

For most people, Grammarly is the better default — it works everywhere, catches real mistakes, and is unobtrusive. For non-native English speakers, students, or anyone who writes rough drafts that need polishing, Wordtune is genuinely transformative. Power users often use both: Grammarly for the safety net, Wordtune when they want to rephrase.

Winner: Grammarly (everyday safety net) · Wordtune (active rewriting)
