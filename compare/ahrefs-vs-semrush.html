Ahrefs vs Semrush

Ahrefs and Semrush are the two biggest names in SEO tooling, and the choice between them has gotten harder. Ahrefs is the SEO-first tool — best backlink crawler, clean UI, beloved by technical SEOs. Semrush is the full marketing suite — SEO plus PPC, social, content, and competitive research in one platform. $130-140/mo each.

✓ What Ahrefs does better

  • Largest fresh backlink database (crawled constantly)
  • Cleanest UI of any SEO tool
  • Content Gap tool is best-in-class
  • Free tools (keyword generator, backlink checker) are genuinely useful

✗ Where Ahrefs falls short

  • No PPC or social media tools
  • Steeper learning curve for non-SEOs
  • Limited rank-tracking volume on lower tiers
  • No content writing assistant built in

✓ What Semrush does better

  • All-in-one: SEO + PPC + social + content
  • Stronger on competitive PPC research
  • Better agency reporting and client dashboards
  • Larger database of keywords in some markets

✗ Where Semrush falls short

  • UI feels cluttered with features
  • Slower data refresh on backlinks
  • Higher entry price ($140 vs $129)
  • Overkill if you only do SEO

The verdict

If you only care about SEO (organic search rankings, backlinks, content gaps), Ahrefs is the better tool — the data is faster to crawl and the UI is more focused. If you run paid ads, do social media, or need a single platform for your whole marketing team, Semrush is the better investment. Solo SEO consultants usually pick Ahrefs; agencies pick Semrush.

Winner: Ahrefs (pure SEO) · Semrush (full marketing suite)
