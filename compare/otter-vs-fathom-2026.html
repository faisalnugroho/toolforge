Where they differ in 2026

Free tier: This is Fathom's killer feature. The free plan gives you unlimited recordings on the personal tier (with usage caps on the AI summary), while Otter's free plan caps at 300 minutes/month and 30 minutes per conversation. For a solo user on many calls, Fathom's free is meaningfully more generous.

Transcription accuracy: Otter still wins on raw accuracy, especially on overlapping speakers and strong accents. Fathom is very good and has closed the gap with its 2026 model upgrade, but Otter's live captions are ~1–2 seconds faster and slightly cleaner.

Summaries & clips: Fathom shines here. Its 1-click highlight clips (with auto-formatted shareable links) are the best in the market for sharing a 30-second moment with a teammate. Otter's summaries are more text-heavy and less shareable.

CRM & integrations: Fathom pushes calls into HubSpot, Salesforce, Close, and Pipedrive natively — built for revenue workflows. Otter leans calendar + Slack.

Privacy: Fathom never trains on your calls (written policy, free tier included) and is SOC 2 Type II. Otter added a no-training toggle but defaults to opt-out — check your settings.

The verdict

Pick Fathom if you take a lot of customer or sales calls and want a genuinely useful free plan with shareable clips. Pick Otter.ai if your priority is the most accurate live transcript for lectures, interviews, or accessibility use cases.

Winner: Fathom for sales · Otter for accuracy

How we tested

Both notetakers joined the same 35 calls on Zoom and Google Meet over 5 weeks. We measured free-tier limits, transcription word-error rate, summary quality, clip sharing, and CRM sync. We don't accept payment for reviews — see our full disclosure.
