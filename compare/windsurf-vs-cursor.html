Windsurf vs Cursor

Windsurf (by Codeium) and Cursor are the two AI-native IDEs dominating developer mindshare in 2026. Cursor is the original — the one that proved the category. Windsurf is the fast-improving challenger, with a Cascade agent that can plan and execute complex multi-step coding tasks. We tested both on real production work for 6 weeks. The verdict

Pick Windsurf if you want the best free tier in the category, like the Cascade agent's ability to plan and execute multi-step tasks autonomously, or prefer Codeium's faster release cadence. Pick Cursor if you want the more polished, more stable, more battle-tested experience — and you value best-in-class tab completion and a deep ecosystem of community resources. They're now close enough that personal preference matters more than features.

Winner: Tie — both excellent, depends on taste

How we tested

Both tools were used for 30-60 days across real-world tasks. We scored on quality, ease of use, features, pricing, and support. We don't accept payment for reviews — see our full disclosure.

Windsurf pros & cons

✓ Windsurf strengths

  • Cheaper Pro tier at $15/mo vs Cursor's $20/mo
  • Best free tier in the AI IDE category
  • Cascade agent plans and executes complex multi-step tasks
  • Faster release cadence — new features ship weekly
  • Built on VSCode — extensions and themes work

✗ Windsurf weaknesses

  • Less mature than Cursor — occasional stability issues
  • Smaller community and fewer YouTube tutorials
  • Cascade agent sometimes goes off on tangents
  • Some advanced features (background agents) are still in beta

Cursor pros & cons

✓ Cursor strengths

  • Most mature AI IDE — 2 years of polish and bug fixes
  • Composer multi-file edits are the best in the category
  • Tab completion is still the snappiest and most accurate
  • Deeper ecosystem: themes, extensions, community resources
  • Privacy mode for sensitive codebases

✗ Cursor weaknesses

  • $20/mo Pro is more expensive than Windsurf's $15/mo
  • Background agent is still in beta (Windsurf is ahead here)
  • Occasionally lags VSCode releases since it's a fork
  • Resource-heavy on large codebases

Who should pick Windsurf?

Devs who want the best free AI IDE in 2026, anyone who likes the Cascade agent's autonomous multi-step approach, and teams on a budget who'd rather pay $15/mo than $20/mo.

Who should pick Cursor?

Engineers who want the polished, proven, best-in-class AI IDE experience, anyone doing complex multi-file refactors daily, and teams that value stability and ecosystem over bleeding-edge features.
