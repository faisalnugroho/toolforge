GPT-4 vs Claude

GPT-4 (and its o1/o3 reasoning variants) and Claude (Opus 4.5, Sonnet 4) are the two flagship AI models that 90% of professionals are choosing between in 2026. Both are excellent, both are at the frontier, but they have meaningfully different strengths: GPT-4 wins on multimodality, voice, and ecosystem; Claude wins on long-form reasoning, writing quality, and code review. We tested both on 50+ real tasks over 6 weeks. Here's the honest breakdown.

The verdict

Pick GPT-4 if you want the best multimodal experience (DALL-E image gen, voice mode, real-time vision), the broadest app/integration ecosystem, or ChatGPT's polished consumer UI. Pick Claude if you need the smartest long-form analysis, the most 'human' writing, the best code review, or 200K-token context for whole-document analysis. For most power users in 2026, the answer is: have a $20/mo subscription to each.

Winner: Tie — different strengths, both at the frontier

How we tested

Both tools were used for 30-60 days across real-world tasks. We scored on quality, ease of use, features, pricing, and support. We don't accept payment for reviews — see our full disclosure.

GPT-4 pros & cons

✓ GPT-4 strengths

  • Best multimodal experience — image gen (DALL-E 3), voice, real-time vision
  • Largest app/integration ecosystem (3M+ GPTs, 1000+ plugins)
  • Polished consumer product — ChatGPT is the most-used AI product in the world
  • o1 and o3 reasoning models are best-in-class for hard math/logic
  • Free tier is actually useful (GPT-4o mini)

✗ GPT-4 weaknesses

  • 200K context exists but is gated to enterprise plans
  • Writing can feel generic — 'ChatGPT voice' is recognizable
  • Hallucination rate is higher than Claude on factual tasks
  • OpenAI's safety/training is more restrictive than competitors

Claude pros & cons

✓ Claude strengths

  • Best-in-class long-form writing — most 'human' prose of any model
  • 200K context on Pro tier (1M on enterprise) — fit whole books
  • Superior code review and refactoring suggestions
  • Constitutional AI training feels less restrictive and more honest
  • Artifacts and Projects are thoughtful UX additions

✗ Claude weaknesses

  • No native image generation (relies on third-party for vision)
  • Voice mode exists but is less polished than ChatGPT's
  • Smaller app ecosystem — fewer third-party integrations
  • Free tier is more limited (5 messages/day on Opus)

Who should pick GPT-4?

Consumers who want a polished all-purpose AI, anyone who needs the best image generation and voice mode, teams building on top of the OpenAI API, and users who want the broadest app ecosystem (GPTs, plugins).

Who should pick Claude?

Writers, analysts, lawyers, and researchers who need the highest-quality long-form output, developers who want the best code review, anyone working with very long documents (legal, research, books), and teams that value Anthropic's thoughtful safety approach.
