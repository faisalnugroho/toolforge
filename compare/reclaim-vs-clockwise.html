Reclaim vs Clockwise

Reclaim and Clockwise are the two leading AI calendar tools in 2026. Reclaim is the habit-and-task scheduler — it finds time for your recurring work (focus blocks, exercise, 1-on-1s) automatically. Clockwise is the meeting coordinator — it moves flexible meetings to create longer Focus Time for your team. Both claim to give you "your time back" via smart scheduling.

✓ What Reclaim does better

  • Auto-schedules recurring tasks and habits
  • Integrates with Todoist, Linear, Asana, ClickUp
  • Smart 1-on-1 scheduling finds mutual times
  • Free tier is usable for solo work

✗ Where Reclaim falls short

  • Free tier limited to 3 calendar integrations
  • Habit tracking can feel intrusive
  • Less polished for team-wide scheduling
  • No Slack status sync (unlike Clockwise)

✓ What Clockwise does better

  • Creates the longest Focus Time blocks of any tool
  • Team-wide analytics on meeting time
  • Integrates with Slack and Asana for status sync
  • Lower price ($8/mo entry)

✗ Where Clockwise falls short

  • Limited task-management features
  • Less powerful for individual habit scheduling
  • No Todoist/Linear/Asana integration
  • Stats dashboard only on paid tier

The verdict

For individual knowledge workers who want to defend focus time and auto-schedule habits, Reclaim is the better fit — it integrates with task managers (Todoist, Linear, Asana). For teams that spend 60%+ of time in meetings and need to coordinate schedules across the company, Clockwise wins. They're not mutually exclusive — many users run both.

Winner: Reclaim (individual + tasks) · Clockwise (team coordination)
