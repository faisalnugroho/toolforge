The comparison

📸 Photorealism

Flux produces significantly more photorealistic images out of the box. Skin textures, lighting, and reflections look natural without extensive prompting. Stable Diffusion 3.5 and SDXL can match Flux with the right LoRAs and settings, but it requires more effort.

Winner: Flux — better defaults, less tweaking needed.

🔤 Text Rendering

Flux handles text in images dramatically better. It can render signs, logos, and labels with readable text. Stable Diffusion still struggles with text — garbled letters are common even with careful prompting.

Winner: Flux — text rendering is a generational leap.

🎨 Customization & Ecosystem

Stable Diffusion has a massive ecosystem — thousands of LoRAs, ControlNets, IP-Adapter, and deep ComfyUI/A1111 integration. You can fine-tune every aspect of the output. Flux's ecosystem is growing but can't match years of community development.

Winner: Stable Diffusion — the ecosystem is unmatched.

⚡ Speed

Flux models are generally faster, especially Flux Schnell which generates images in 1-4 steps. Stable Diffusion requires 20-30 steps for comparable quality. On equivalent hardware, Flux is 3-5x faster.

Winner: Flux — significantly faster generation.

The verdict

Use Flux for photorealistic images, text-heavy designs, and when you need fast results with minimal tweaking. Use Stable Diffusion when you need deep customization, specific art styles via LoRAs, or are already invested in the ComfyUI ecosystem. For new users starting in 2026, Flux is the better first choice.

Overall winner: Flux for quality, Stable Diffusion for customization
