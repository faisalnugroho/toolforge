How electricians actually use AI

1. Quoting and estimating

The old way: drive to the site, walk the job, scribble notes, go back to the truck, build a spreadsheet, email the GC. Two hours minimum per quote. The AI way: record a voice walkthrough with Otter.ai during the site visit — it transcribes everything. Feed the transcript to ChatGPT with your rate sheet and it drafts a line-item quote in 30 seconds. You review, adjust, and send. Quote time drops from 2 hours to 20 minutes.

2. NEC code compliance lookups

Every electrician has spent 45 minutes flipping through the NEC handbook to confirm a conduit fill calculation or ampacity derating rule. ChatGPT (with its code-trained knowledge) can answer most NEC questions in seconds — "What's the max number of 12 AWG THHN conductors in a 3/4" EMT?" — and cite the article. Always verify against the actual codebook, but for quick field checks, it saves enormous time.

3. Blueprint markup and crew coordination

Fieldwire turns your iPad into a digital blueprint tool. Upload PDFs, mark up circuits, assign tasks to specific crew members, and track completion in real time. The AI features auto-detect sheets, suggest task groupings, and flag conflicting markups. No more "is this the latest revision?" confusion on a 40-sheet commercial project.

4. Dispatching and scheduling

Service Fusion uses AI to optimize dispatching — it considers drive time, job priority, technician skills, and traffic patterns to assign the right electrician to the right job. For a 3-truck operation, this saves 5-8 hours of dispatching labor per week and reduces windshield time by 15%.

How we picked

Every tool on this list was actively used by at least 3 licensed electricians running their own contracting business. We tested each for 2+ weeks on real jobs — residential service calls, commercial TIs, and new construction. We scored on time saved, accuracy, ease of use on a phone/table, and price-to-value. We removed anything that required more setup time than it saved.

The verdict

Start with ChatGPT Plus ($20/mo) — it pays for itself the first time you use it to decode a confusing spec sheet or draft a change order. Add Housecall Pro if you're solo ($49/mo) or Service Fusion if you have crews ($145/mo) for scheduling and quoting. Bring in Fieldwire for any commercial job with blueprints. The total stack costs less than one hour of billable time per month.
