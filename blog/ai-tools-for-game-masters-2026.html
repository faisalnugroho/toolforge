Top picks, explained

ChatGPT

Generate a scheming merchant's voice, three plot hooks from a rumor, or a year of in-world calendar events in seconds. Keep the dice and the decisions yours.

Midjourney

Show players what the Goblin King looks like or sketch a tavern they walk into. Immersive without commissioning an artist.

Suno

A 90-second tavern theme or an urgent combat loop generated at the right tempo keeps the table in the moment.

ElevenLabs

Give the haunted ghost a real voice mid-session. Bookmark a few clips so you're not generating live under pressure.

Notion

A searchable wiki for NPCs, factions, and loose threads — and session notes so you remember what the party promised last week.

Canva

Printable handouts, wanted posters, and party certificates for milestone sessions.

How to choose

Prep-time is the enemy of consistent games. Spend AI on the parts you'd skip, not the parts you love.

  • ChatGPT + Notion handle prep and continuity for free.
  • Midjourney is the one paid tool worth it if you love showing art.
  • Suno + ElevenLabs are atmosphere — add once you want immersion.
  • Canva only if you print physical handouts.

FAQ

Does using AI make me a lazy GM?

Only if you outsource the judgment. Use it for raw material — names, images, loops — and you still direct the story. Most GMs report prepping 3x faster.

Can players tell it's AI art?

Sometimes. Frame it as 'mood reference' and most tables love the boost. Don't claim it's a commissioned piece.

What's the cheapest high-impact combo?

ChatGPT + Notion + Suno covers prep, continuity, and atmosphere at $0.

Verdict

ChatGPT and Notion alone will save you hours of prep a week. Add Midjourney, Suno, and ElevenLabs when you want your table to see and hear the world you're building.
