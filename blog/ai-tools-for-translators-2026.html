The AI translation workflow

Professional translators in 2026 don't use AI to replace themselves — they use it to remove the boring 60% of their work. Here's the workflow that the top earners use:

Step 1: Machine Translation First Pass

Run the source text through DeepL Pro for a high-quality first draft. DeepL's neural engine is 20-30% more accurate than Google Translate for European languages, and it preserves formatting, making it easy to paste back into your CAT tool.

Step 2: Translation Memory + Glossary

Use OmegaT, Smartcat, or Trados to leverage your translation memory. If you've translated similar content before, your CAT tool pre-fills matches. AI-enhanced TM engines (like Smartcat's) can even suggest fuzzy matches with 70%+ similarity.

Step 3: ChatGPT for Context

When you hit a tricky idiom, cultural reference, or ambiguous phrasing, paste the context into ChatGPT. Ask: "How would a native speaker translate this idiom? What's the cultural equivalent?" GPT-5 excels at explaining why a translation works, not just what it should be.

Step 4: DeepL Write for Polish

Run your final translation through DeepL Write. It catches awkward phrasing, tone inconsistencies, and grammar errors that slip through — especially helpful when translating into a non-native language.

Step 5: QA with QA Distiller or Xbench

Run automated QA checks for consistency, missing numbers, formatting errors, and terminology deviations. This catches 90% of mistakes that human reviewers miss.

The honest verdict

If you're a professional translator and you're not using DeepL Pro ($9/mo) + ChatGPT Plus ($20/mo), you're leaving 50% of your income on the table. These two tools alone can double your daily word output while maintaining quality. Add a free CAT tool like OmegaT for translation memory, and your total stack costs $29/mo — which pays for itself in the first hour of billed work.

The tools that aren't worth it: Dedicated "AI translation" startups that promise one-click translation. They're just wrappers around DeepL or GPT APIs with a markup. Stick with the original tools.
