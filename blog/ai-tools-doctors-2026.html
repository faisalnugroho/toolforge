HIPAA: what you must check

Before using any AI tool on patient data, verify three things:

  1. Business Associate Agreement (BAA). The vendor must sign one. Without it, you are violating HIPAA by sending PHI to a non-covered entity. The four big ambient scribes (Abridge, Suki, DeepScribe, Nuance) all offer BAAs.
  2. No training on your data. The free and Plus tiers of ChatGPT, Claude, and Gemini are not HIPAA-compliant — full stop. Their enterprise tiers do offer BAAs but with caveats. Read the fine print.
  3. Audit logs. You need to know who accessed which patient record and when. Enterprise ambient scribes and EHR-integrated tools do this; consumer products don't.

How we picked

Every tool on this list either (1) has a BAA on the entry-level plan, (2) is in use at one of the 10 US News Honor Roll hospitals, or (3) has cleared an FDA pathway for clinical AI. We removed anything where patient data flows to a vendor that won't sign a BAA. We also removed the AI tools that are technically clever but operationally a nightmare (looking at you, fine-tuned LLaMA on a hospital server).

The verdict

For primary care & high-volume clinics: Abridge (ambient scribe) + Consensus (research). The combo pays for itself if it saves you 30 minutes/day of charting — and it will.

For specialists: DeepScribe (specialty-tuned scribe) + UpToDate. Cardiology, ortho, and OB/GYN-specific models are noticeably better than general scribes.

For hospitalists & residents: Dragon Medical One (EHR dictation) + Suki (note generation). The Dragon-Suki combo is the most-used in academic medical centers.

For researchers & academic medicine: Consensus + Elicit. The literature review tools have cut 20-hour literature reviews down to 2 hours at our test institutions.
