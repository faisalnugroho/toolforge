What actually differs

On the surface all four serve foundation models through an API. The differences show up in three places that matter to your P&L and your audit log: model choice, safety controls, and where the data lives. Vertex AI wins on catalog breadth and grounding; Bedrock wins on guardrails and AWS integration; Azure OpenAI wins on OpenAI quality under Microsoft compliance; watsonx wins on governance and hybrid deployment.

If you want to avoid lock-in

A pragmatic pattern in 2026 is to put a gateway like Portkey in front of whichever cloud you choose. One OpenAI-compatible endpoint, automatic fallbacks between providers, and traces on every call mean a future migration is a config change, not a rewrite. For open-model experimentation, Replicate keeps you off any single vendor's roadmap.

The bottom line

Default to the cloud you already run on. The models are increasingly comparable; the real differentiators are guardrails, grounding, and governance — exactly the things that are painful to retrofit later.
