Best AI Tools for Meetings in 2026 (Ranked & Tested)

The best AI meeting assistants in 2026 — Otter, Sembly, Fireflies, Granola, Read AI, tl;dv, Avoma. Real transcription accuracy, real pricing, and a clear buying guide for teams and individuals.

✍️ Published June 2026 ⏱️ 10 min read 🔄 Updated June 17, 2026

The meeting AI category exploded between 2023 and 2026. What started as "transcribe your Zoom call" became a $3B+ market of AI assistants that join, summarize, extract action items, push notes to CRM, and even coach reps in real time. We tested 8 platforms across 50+ real meetings in English, Spanish, and Mandarin. Here's the ranking, the tradeoffs, and the picking guide.

The shortlist

If you only have 60 seconds: Granola is the best notes app for individuals. Otter is the cheapest with the best raw transcription accuracy. Sembly is the strongest "all-rounder" for teams. Fireflies is the pick for sales teams that need CRM sync. tl;dv has the best free tier. Read AI is the underdog with the best meeting analytics. Avoma is the all-in-one for mid-market teams.

How we tested

We measured six things across 50+ meetings: transcription accuracy (word error rate on English, Spanish, and Mandarin audio), speaker identification accuracy, action-item extraction quality, search quality across meeting history, integration depth (calendar, CRM, docs), and price. We ran each platform in parallel on the same calls where possible, and recorded three meetings per platform across different industries (sales, design review, customer support).

The full ranking

1. Granola — Best notes app for individuals & small teams

The "AI notepad that doesn't suck" — write your own notes, and Granola's AI expands them into structured summaries, decisions, and action items. By 2026, Granola has 200K+ users, mostly founders, designers, and PMs who want AI to amplify their own note-taking rather than replace it. $14/mo. Weakness: no auto-join — you have to click "record" yourself. Weakness #2: team collaboration is basic.

2. Otter — Best for raw transcription accuracy

Still the gold standard for clean English transcription (~96% WER). $16.99/mo Pro. Joins Zoom/Meet/Teams, transcribes live, generates summaries. The brand most people recognize. Weakness: limited multilingual (English-first). Weakness #2: action-item extraction is weaker than Sembly or Fireflies.

3. Sembly — Best all-rounder for teams

The most balanced: 92% transcription, best decision/action-item extraction ("Sembly Moments"), 40+ languages including code-switching, $10/mo Pro, team meeting library with shared search. Best pick for teams running 5+ meetings/week across multiple languages. We reviewed Sembly in detail here.

4. Fireflies — Best for sales teams

Deep CRM integration (Salesforce, HubSpot, Pipedrive), conversation intelligence features (sentiment, talk-time, objection tracking). $18/mo Business. The sales-team default by 2026. Weakness: not as good for non-sales meetings; pricing jumps fast at the team tier.

5. tl;dv — Best free tier

Generous free tier: unlimited recordings, 10 AI summaries/month, basic search. $20/mo Pro. Best for individual users and small teams who don't want to pay for a meeting AI yet. Weakness: AI summaries are weaker than Granola or Sembly.

6. Read AI — Best meeting analytics

The "underdog that's catching up fast" — Read AI provides meeting analytics (engagement, sentiment, talk-time balance) plus standard transcription/summary. Free tier. $19.99/mo Pro. Best for managers who want analytics on team meetings. Weakness: smaller ecosystem than Otter/Fireflies.

7. Avoma — Best all-in-one for mid-market

Full meeting lifecycle: scheduling, recording, transcription, summary, CRM sync, conversation intelligence. $59/mo per user is steep, but it replaces 3-4 separate tools. Best for mid-market sales orgs. Weakness: too expensive for individuals and small teams.

The picking guide

Pick Granola if you want AI to amplify your own note-taking (founders, designers, PMs).

Pick Otter if you just need the most accurate English transcription at the lowest price.

Pick Sembly if you run 5+ meetings/week across multiple languages or need decision/action-item extraction.

Pick Fireflies if you're a sales team that needs CRM integration and conversation intelligence.

Pick tl;dv if you want to try a meeting AI without paying.

Pick Read AI if you're a manager who wants meeting analytics on your team.

Pick Avoma if you're a mid-market team that wants one tool for the full meeting lifecycle.

The hidden cost: meeting fatigue

Meeting AI solves documentation, but it doesn't solve too many meetings. The best teams in 2026 use meeting AI to compress meetings (skip the parts you don't need, search transcripts later) AND cut their meeting count by 30-40%. Pair your meeting AI with a calendar tool like Clockwise or Motion to reclaim focus time.

FAQs

Do I need to notify participants that AI is recording? Laws vary by state/country. Most platforms include a disclosure script. California requires explicit consent. Best practice: always disclose.

Can I use multiple meeting AIs at once? Yes, but they'll all transcribe the same audio — redundant and wasteful. Pick one primary and use others for occasional specific needs.

How accurate are AI meeting transcriptions in 2026? 90-96% on clean English audio. Lower (75-90%) on noisy environments, accents, or technical jargon.

What's the best free meeting AI in 2026? tl;dv (unlimited recordings, 10 AI summaries/mo) or Granola (unlimited AI expansions on your own notes).
