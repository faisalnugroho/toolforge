How serious angels use AI in 2026

The pre-AI workflow: 6 hours/week scrolling Twitter, reading 200+ cold emails, manually screening pitch decks in a 5-tab Excel spreadsheet, scheduling 3 intro calls with startups that turn out to be wrong-stage, and then doing real diligence. Total: 15 hours per week to look at 30 deals.

The post-AI workflow: 30 mins Perplexity scanning the week's news for sector signals, 1 hour Hebbia or Claude Projects doing first-pass deck analysis, 1 hour scheduling, 6 hours actual diligence. Total: 8.5 hours per week to look at 100 deals.

That's 2x more deals in half the time — the edge every active angel needs in 2026's competitive market.

The diligence playbook

Step 1: Deck triage (15 min/deck). Upload to Claude Sonnet with this prompt: "Analyze this pitch deck for: (1) claim-consistency issues, (2) market-size realism, (3) red flags from financial profile, (4) strengths worth deeper diligence. Output a one-page memo." Catches 80% of the dealbreaker patterns humans miss.

Step 2: Market signal scan (20 min/deal). Perplexity Pro with Pro Search queries LinkedIn, Crunchbase, and the web for: recent competitor funding, hiring patterns (traction signal), and customer reviews (product quality signal). Surfaces the 'is this real' questions worth asking founders.

Step 3: Founder background check (10 min). ChatGPT Search with the founder's name + previous companies + Twitter/LinkedIn history. You'll find the 1-in-20 founder who's been quietly working on this for 6 years vs. the 19-in-20 who started 6 weeks ago.

Step 4: Reference call prep (15 min/call). Granola or Otter join your reference calls. Get auto-generated summaries and a list of the founder's most-avoided questions. (The questions they skipped = the questions to dig into.)

Step 5: Portfolio monitoring (1 hour/month per company). Hebbia or ChatGPT Projects reads the monthly investor update emails and synthesizes: who's on track, who's slipping, what themes are emerging across the portfolio. The output is a 1-page board-prep doc.

The stack at a glance

Total monthly cost: ~$120. Total time saved: 6-8 hours per week. The math: if your average angel check is $25K and your hit rate improves 10% because you're seeing 2x more deals, the stack pays for itself on the first good investment it surfaces.

Active angels in 2026 don't have time to read 200 decks in detail. They use AI to read all 200, then spend their human attention on the 8-10 that pass. This is the new normal — and the early movers are already winning it.

When NOT to use AI

AI is great at pattern detection and signal extraction. AI is bad at: gut feel for founder charisma, team dynamic read, and the 'this is a 100x founder' moment. Always keep the founder meeting human. Always close your own deals. AI is the screening layer, not the judge.
