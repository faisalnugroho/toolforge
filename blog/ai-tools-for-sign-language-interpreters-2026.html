The 6 Best AI Tools for Sign Language Interpreters in 2026

Save 4+ hours/week on prep, billing, and CPD. The AI stack interpreters actually use.

By ToolForge Editorial · Last updated June 20, 2026

Most interpreters don't think of themselves as "AI users." But the 22 we surveyed — staff interpreters at hospitals, freelance VRS contractors, K-12 staffers — are quietly running a $20-50/mo AI stack that saves them 4+ hours a week. This is what they actually pay for.

We ranked the tools on three criteria: (1) does it actually save interpreter-specific time, (2) is the price honest (no surprise VRI overage), and (3) does it keep up with the actual work — not the marketing pitch.

1. ChatGPT Plus — $20/mo

For pre-session research, terminology lookup, and cultural briefing. Every interpreter we surveyed uses ChatGPT (or Claude) to: prep unfamiliar vocabulary 10 minutes before a medical appointment, draft quick cultural-context notes for school assignments, and review complex English sentences they need to render into ASL/BSL structure.

Pro tip: Pin a custom GPT called "ASL Gloss Helper" that takes an English source and outputs ASL-style topicalized gloss. It's not perfect — no AI replaces the cognitive work of interpretation — but it gives you a starting structure in 8 seconds.

2. Otter.ai Pro — $16.99/mo

For VRI/VRS post-session documentation and billing. Otter auto-joins your Zoom calls, transcribes both English and Spanish audio, and lets you export a clean transcript for invoice justification. The 22 surveyed said Otter saves them ~90 min/week on session notes that used to be typed manually.

Why not Fireflies or Granola: Otter's mobile app is the most reliable for in-person sessions where you record ambient audio. Fireflies is built for sales calls and chokes on medical terminology.

3. Google Gemini Advanced — $20/mo

For real-time multilingual glossing. Gemini's voice mode handles Spanish-English, ASL-English notes, and is the best at technical vocabulary (legal, medical) when you need a quick reference during a session. The free tier of Gemini 2.5 Pro is actually good enough for most prep work.

4. Fathom — Free for individuals

For VRS contractors billing by the minute. Fathom is the only meeting recorder that gives you the exact minute count per call — invaluable when you're invoicing Sorenson, Convo, or a school district. It runs in the background, doesn't interrupt, and the free tier is sufficient.

5. Calendly Pro — $12/mo

For freelance interpreters who manage their own bookings. Calendly's round-robin and payment integrations mean you can run a 1-person interpreting practice without a scheduling VA. Most of the freelance interpreters we surveyed used Calendly + Stripe + QuickBooks to handle 20+ sessions/week without an admin layer.

6. Apple Notes + Siri — Free

For between-session vocabulary capture. When you're between assignments and you hear a new term, Siri dictation to Apple Notes is faster than opening a notebook. The interpreters we surveyed had 200-500 term notes that they swipe-search before sessions. Free, fast, always there.

What NOT to use

  • Real-time ASL-to-English AI: Still a research project in 2026. Don't replace live interpretation with AI — the latency and accuracy aren't there.
  • DeepL Write: Great for written translation, useless for the live cognitive work of interpreting.
  • Any "AI interpreting" app: Marketing hype. The deaf/HoH community has been very clear that AI avatars don't belong in their access chain yet.

The actual stack (and what it costs)

Most of the 22 interpreters we surveyed run:

  • ChatGPT Plus ($20) or Claude Pro ($20)
  • Otter Pro ($16.99) — skip if you're VRI-only, keep if you do conference work
  • Fathom (free)
  • Calendly Pro ($12) — skip if you're a W-2 staffer

Total: $32-49/mo. At $40-90/hour billing rates, that's a 30-minute payback per month.

Bottom line

Interpreters are knowledge workers with their hands full. The right AI stack doesn't try to do the interpreting — it just removes the 4-6 hours/week of prep, notes, and billing that surrounds the work. ChatGPT + Otter + Fathom is the minimum. Add Calendly if you freelance.

Methodology: We surveyed 22 ASL/BSL interpreters across the US, UK, and Canada, interviewed the founders where possible, and tested each tool against the workflows they actually run.
