Why these tools (and not the obvious picks)

We deliberately excluded tools that sound 'AI for contractors' but are really just rebranded CRMs. ServiceTitan and Jobber made the list because they actually have AI features (AI dispatch, AI call summaries, AI pricebook) that save time, not just AI stickers on a SaaS dashboard. ChatGPT made the list because every HVAC owner we spoke to who tries it ends up using it daily for emails, SOPs, and marketing. The rest are best-in-class for a specific pain point.

How to actually deploy this stack in a week

You don't need to deploy all 8 at once. Here's a realistic rollout:

  1. Day 1: Sign up for ChatGPT Plus ($20/mo). Use it to write 5 customer follow-up emails and one Google Ads headline. You'll know by the end of the day whether it's worth keeping.
  2. Day 2: Turn on Otter.ai for your office phones. Let it run for a week. Read the AI summaries. You'll be shocked at how many 'oh, I forgot to mention' items it catches.
  3. Day 3-7: Audit your current field service software. If you're on ServiceTitan, turn on AI Dispatch and AI Call Summary. If you're on Jobber, turn on AI scheduling. Both have AI features buried in settings — most shops don't use them.
  4. Week 2: Add Levity to auto-tag inbound service requests. Watch your dispatcher's time free up. Add Fathom for free vendor and team meeting notes.
  5. Week 3: Use Canva + Gamma to refresh your service brochure and create 3 social media post templates. Most HVAC companies' marketing looks like 2014. This is the cheapest 13 + $10 you can spend to fix that.

The ROI math: if the AI stack saves your dispatcher 10 hours/week (at $25/hr loaded = $13K/year) and helps you book 2 extra jobs/month (at $500 avg ticket = $12K/year), the stack pays for itself 10x over. None of this requires replacing your existing CRM — it layers on top.

Common mistakes to avoid

  • Buying AI tools that don't integrate with your CRM. If it doesn't sync to ServiceTitan/Jobber/Housecall Pro, you'll end up with two systems that don't talk to each other. Check the integration list before signing up.
  • Using AI to send cold messages to homeowners. TCPA, state laws, and carrier filters will burn you. Use AI to respond to inbound interest, not generate outbound spam.
  • Letting AI auto-reply to service requests without human review. A wrong diagnosis or missed urgency flag can cost you the job and the relationship. AI drafts, humans approve.
  • Ignoring training data. Your AI is only as smart as what you feed it. Spend 30 minutes giving ChatGPT context on your service area, pricing, common repairs, and brand voice. The quality jump is massive.
  • Forgetting customer consent for call recording. Otter and Fathom are great, but you must disclose that calls are recorded. Most states are one-party consent; check yours.
