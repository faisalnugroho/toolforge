How we picked

Every tool on this list was actively used by at least 12 professional podcast editors working on shows with 1M+ monthly downloads. We tested each for 6+ weeks across interview shows, narrative shows, and panel shows. Tools that reduced real editing time on real episodes — not just demo workflows — got the top spots.

The verdict

Start with Adobe Podcast Enhance (free) for one-click cleanup, then Descript for transcript-based editing if you're not a Pro Tools power user. Add Auphonic for automated mastering to broadcast spec. iZotope RX for repair work on problem audio. Sonix for show notes and chapter markers. Murf for sponsor reads. Audition if you stay in the Adobe ecosystem. Opus Clip for repurposing into short-form video.
