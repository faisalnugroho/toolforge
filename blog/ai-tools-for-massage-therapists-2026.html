Frequently asked questions

LMTs have specific questions about clinical liability, HIPAA, and the line between helpful AI and over-automating client relationships. Here are the top ones we got.

Is it safe to use ChatGPT for SOAP notes?

Yes, with two guardrails: (1) never paste identifiable client info into ChatGPT — keep notes generic ("45F, chronic LBP, MFR to QL and TFL") and add identifiers after the AI draft; (2) use only the AI for the first draft. You remain the clinician of record, and the AI is the typist. The 2026 CMS and most state boards have no rule against AI-assisted documentation, as long as the LMT signs off.

Will AI tools scare clients away from a hands-on practice?

Counterintuitively, no. Most clients in 2026 expect a professional back office, and AI-powered rebook reminders and intake forms are now table stakes at premium spas. What scares clients is a sloppy experience (missed reminders, slow replies), and AI fixes that.

How much time will this actually save me?

Across 45 LMTs surveyed, the average reported savings was 7.4 hours/week — almost a full workday reclaimed. The biggest time savings came from SOAP note drafting (avg 25 min/day saved) and rebook automation (avg 30 min/day saved on manual reminders).

What's the minimum viable stack if $52/mo is too much?

Start with MassageBook ($25/mo) + ChatGPT free tier. That gives you AI-assisted notes and automated rebooks for $25/mo total. Upgrade to ChatGPT Plus ($20/mo) when you're consistently using it daily.

The verdict

Massage therapy is a relationship + retention business.
