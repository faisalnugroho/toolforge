The workflow

1. Idea & lyrics: Claude for brainstorming, lyrics drafts, and chord progression theory. "Write a verse about losing someone in 4/4 time, A minor." Don't sleep on it for hooks.

2. Reference breakdown: Moises for stem separation — drop in any track and pull out just the vocals, or just the drums. Sample flips, cover prep, learning sessions.

3. Demo & ideation: Suno or Udio for fast song sketches. Generate 20 variations of an idea in an hour. Use them as reference tracks for your session musicians.

4. Recording session: Krisp running on your DAW's input — removes HVAC, room echo, breath noise in real time. Cuts editing time in half.

5. Vocals: ElevenLabs for harmonies, doubles, and ad-libs that match your lead vocal's timbre. Use ethically — disclose AI vocals when shipping to streaming.

6. Editing: Descript for podcast-style audio (interviews, dialogue, narrated content). Edit audio by editing the transcript. Revolutionary.

7. Session notes: Granola records every producer call and gives you a clean summary with action items. "Re-record chorus, second take. Add high-pass filter on bass." Done.

The honest take

AI music tools won't write a hit song for you — but they will kill 80% of the boring admin that makes producing feel like a second job. The best producers we know use AI for the early ideation and post-production polish, then spend the actual craft time on arrangement, performance, and emotion. That's the right ratio.

The verdict

The full stack runs about $137/month — less than one studio session. Start with Suno + ElevenLabs + Krisp for the core loop. Add the rest as your release calendar demands. For bedroom producers especially, this is the moment.
