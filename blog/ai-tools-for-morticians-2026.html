Top picks, explained

ChatGPT

Turn a family's scattered memories into a dignified first draft of an obituary or eulogy — then you refine it together. Never input a full name + birth date into a public model without the family's understanding.

Canva

Tasteful, print-ready memorial cards, order-of-service programs, and tribute boards without a print shop markup.

ElevenLabs

With family consent, clone a late loved one's voice to read a letter or poem at a service. Profound — and ethically sensitive. Always get explicit permission.

Descript

Edit a photo-and-clip tribute like a document; add music and captions for the reception screen.

Notion

One calm place for each arrangement's checklist, vendor contacts, and family preferences — so nothing slips during a busy week.

Grammarly

Keep every email to a grieving family clear, warm, and error-free.

How to choose

In this field, tone is everything. Use AI for drafts and layout; always keep a human hand on the final words.

  • ChatGPT + Grammarly for writing; you stay the editor.
  • Canva for every printed tribute piece.
  • Descript for memorial videos when families want them.
  • Notion to keep arrangements from overwhelming your week.

FAQ

Is it appropriate to use AI for an obituary?

As a drafting aid, yes — families are often too grieved to write. The final words should always be shaped by you and approved by the family.

Voice cloning — when is that okay?

Only with explicit, documented family consent, and handled with the gravity it deserves. If in doubt, don't.

What about privacy?

Treat any identifying details as sensitive. Prefer local or BAA-covered tools for anything that names the deceased alongside dates.

Verdict

AI won't soften a loss, but it can lift the logistical weight. ChatGPT + Canva + Grammarly cover most firms; add Descript and Notion as your volume and tribute services grow.
