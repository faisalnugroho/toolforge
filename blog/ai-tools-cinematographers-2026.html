How we picked

Every tool was actively used by at least 5 working cinematographers shooting features, episodic, or commercial work. We tested each for 30+ days on real prep cycles, scored on look-development speed, on-set utility, output quality, and price-to-value. We removed anything that produced "AI-looking" footage (a death sentence for cinematographers), anything that disrupted workflow more than it helped, or anything that wasn't worth the subscription cost.

The verdict

Start with Midjourney for look dev — mood boards that used to take a day of frame grabs and Photoshop now take an hour, and the output is genuinely cinematic. Add Runway for camera move testing and previz — you can show a director "this is what a 50mm push-in on a Sigma Cine feels like" without burning 90 minutes on set. Perplexity replaces 30 minutes of forum-spelunking with cited gear research. The first two alone reclaim 2-3 prep days per project.
