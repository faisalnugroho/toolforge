Planning and prep

ChatGPT and Claude both have free tiers that handle lesson planning, differentiation by ability, and first-draft rubrics. Perplexity is the safest for student-facing research because every claim links to a source — teach kids to check the link, not trust the answer.

Feedback and integrity

Grammarly’s free tier gives students real writing feedback inside Docs. QuillBot helps summarize long readings. And Copyleaks’ free check lets you have an honest conversation about AI use instead of playing detective. PDF AI and ChatDoc turn dense handouts into something a struggling reader can actually query.

The verdict

Every tool above is free to start — total cost: $0. Use AI to offload prep and grading, and reinvest that time into the human part of teaching. Upgrade to paid tiers only when a free limit actually blocks you.
