1. Cursor — Ship code 10x faster

If you're a technical founder, Cursor is non-negotiable. It's a VSCode fork with GPT-4 and Claude built in — you write code by describing what you want in English. The Composer feature edits 5-10 files at once, so you can scaffold a whole feature ("add Stripe checkout with subscription tiers") in one prompt. At $20/mo, it pays for itself in the first hour.

Founder tip: Use Cursor's @codebase feature to ask questions about your entire repo. "Where do we handle authentication?" — it'll find it across all files instantly.

2. Notion AI — Your company brain

Every startup needs a single source of truth. Notion AI adds a layer of intelligence on top — it can draft docs, summarize meeting notes, and answer questions about your company wiki. "What's our refund policy?" — Notion AI searches your docs and answers. Replace Confluence, Google Docs, and a project management tool with one $10/user/mo subscription.

3. Perplexity Pro — Research at startup speed

Founders spend 30% of their time researching — market sizes, competitor features, regulations. Perplexity Pro gives you cited, real-time answers in seconds. "What's the TAM for AI-powered legal tools in the EU?" — you get a structured answer with sources. At $20/mo, it replaces a $5K/mo research analyst.

4. Lovable — MVPs without a developer

Non-technical founder? Lovable (and its competitor v0) can build a working web app from a text prompt. "Build a waitlist page with email capture and a referral system" — it generates the code, deploys it, and gives you a live URL. Perfect for validating ideas before you hire or learn to code yourself.

5. Gamma — Pitch decks that raise money

Investors decide in 3 slides. Gamma generates beautiful, structured pitch decks from a text outline — "AI-powered logistics platform, Series Seed, $2M raise" — and you get a polished deck in 10 minutes. Edit, export to PDF, and send. The free tier is enough to build your first deck.

6. Make.com — Automate the boring stuff

Every startup has manual workflows — onboarding emails, CRM updates, data syncs. Make.com (the visual automation tool, formerly Integromat) lets you build automations without code. Connect Stripe → Notion → Slack: when a customer pays, create a project doc, notify the team. At $9/mo, it's cheaper than an intern and works 24/7.

The total stack cost

Cursor ($20) + Notion AI ($10) + Perplexity Pro ($20) + Lovable ($25) + Gamma ($10) + Make ($9) = $94/mo total. That's less than one part-time hire — and it replaces a developer, a researcher, a designer, a project manager, and an automation engineer.

What we'd skip

Don't pay for Jasper ($49/mo) if you have ChatGPT or Claude — the writing quality gap has closed. Skip dedicated SEO tools (Ahrefs at $99/mo) until you have organic traffic to optimize. And hold off on Zapier ($29/mo) — Make.com does the same thing for $9/mo with a better visual editor.
