The Free-Tier-Only AI Stack for 2026 ($0/month, full productivity)

A complete AI productivity stack using only free tiers. Every tool tested with its actual free limits in 2026. Real workarounds when the limits bite.

TF
ToolForge Editorial
✍️ Published June 2026 ⏱️ 6 min read 🔄 Updated June 21, 2026

You can run a real AI productivity stack for $0/month in 2026. Not "barely usable free tiers" — actually enough for a solo founder, freelancer, or small team to operate. Here are the 12 tools, what they actually give you for free, and where the limits bite.

The full stack (12 tools, $0/month)

Writing & thinking: ChatGPT Free + Claude Free

Both give you GPT-5-mini and Claude 3.5 Haiku for free, with rate limits. The trick in 2026: use them in alternation. When ChatGPT hits its rate limit, switch to Claude. Same workflow, double the capacity. Real limit: 10-20 messages/hour on each. Real workaround: sign up for both, use them as failover. Total cost: $0.

Research: Perplexity Free

5 Pro searches/day with full source citations. That's enough for most research workflows if you batch your queries. When you hit the limit, Perplexity's standard search (free, unlimited) still cites sources — just less depth. Total cost: $0.

Meeting notes: Otter Free

300 minutes/month free. That's ~6 hours of meetings — enough for most solo workers. Pro is $16.99/mo but you only need it if you have 5+ hours/week of meetings. Total cost: $0 for light users, $17 for meeting-heavy.

Code: Codeium + Continue.dev

Codeium gives you unlimited autocomplete in any IDE (VSCode, JetBrains, Vim) for free — no Copilot tax. Pair it with Continue.dev (free, open source) for in-IDE chat with any model. Beats the GitHub Copilot $10/mo for most workflows. Total cost: $0.

Image generation: Leonardo.ai Free + Bing Image Creator

Leonardo gives 150 tokens/day free (≈30 images). Bing Image Creator uses DALL-E 3 for free with a Microsoft account — unlimited, slower, slightly lower quality. Use Leonardo for serious work, Bing for throwaway iterations. Total cost: $0.

Voice generation: ElevenLabs Free

10,000 characters/month free — that's ~10 minutes of audio. Enough for product demos, internal training videos, YouTube shorts. For longer work, upgrade to Starter at $5/mo (100k chars). Total cost: $0 for light use.

Video generation: Genmo Free + Pika Free

Genmo gives 100 credits/month free (≈12 clips). Pika gives a smaller free tier with watermark. Combine both for 15-20 short video clips/month, no watermark on Genmo. Total cost: $0.

Transcription: Whisper.cpp (local) or Otter Free

Whisper runs entirely on your laptop — no rate limits, no upload, fully private. Slower than Otter but unlimited. Total cost: $0 + your electricity bill.

Automation: Make Free

1,000 operations/month free — enough for 5-10 active workflows. Better than Zapier's free tier (100 tasks/mo). Total cost: $0.

Docs & knowledge: Notion Free + Obsidian

Notion free for collaborative docs, Obsidian (free) for personal knowledge management. Both have AI features in their paid tiers but the free versions are excellent on their own. Total cost: $0.

Email: Superhuman Free (waitlist) or Gmail with AI

Superhuman's free tier opened up via waitlist in 2025 — limited but real. If you can't get in, Gmail's built-in Gemini features handle 70% of what Superhuman does. Total cost: $0.

Project management: Linear Free + Trello Free

Linear free for product teams (up to 10 members, unlimited issues). Trello free for everything else. Total cost: $0.

Where the limits bite (and workarounds)

Rate limits on LLMs: The biggest bottleneck. ChatGPT free gives you 10-20 messages/hour on GPT-5-mini. Workaround: alternate with Claude free (also 10-20/hr). When both hit limits, use Perplexity's standard search for factual queries. Never run out of options, but you do have to switch tools.

Image gen token limits: Leonardo's 150 tokens/day feels restrictive if you're doing brand work. Workaround: do rough iterations on Bing Image Creator (unlimited), only use Leonardo tokens for final-quality renders. Token conservation = roughly 5-10 final images/day.

Otter's 300 min/month: Runs out fast if you have regular meetings. Workaround: record only the meetings where decisions are made. Skip status meetings. Or batch up — record two weeks, skip two weeks. Most people don't need every meeting transcribed.

Make's 1,000 ops/month: One Salesforce sync = 5 ops. One Slack post = 1 op. Workaround: use the free tier for the most valuable automations only. Manually do the rest. Don't try to automate everything — pick the 3-5 workflows that save the most time.

When to start paying

The free stack works until you hit one of these walls:

  • You need longer than 10-min voice clips → Pay $5/mo for ElevenLabs Starter.
  • You need more than 5 meetings/week transcribed → Pay $17/mo for Otter Pro.
  • You need unlimited LLM access for serious work → Pay $20/mo for ChatGPT Plus or Claude Pro.
  • You need 4K video or longer video clips → Pay $12/mo for Runway.
  • You need more than 1k Make ops/month → Pay $9/mo for Make Core.

The verdict

The honest 2026 answer: you don't need to pay for AI tools to be productive. The free tiers have caught up — most were unusable in 2023, all of them are usable in 2026 if you understand the limits. Start free. Pay only when you hit a specific wall that costs you more time/money than the subscription costs. Most solo workers never need to pay for more than one or two tools.
