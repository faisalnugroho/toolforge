How security companies actually use AI

1. Incident reporting (the big time sink)

Every incident — a trespasser, a noise complaint, a door left open — requires a written report. The old way: guard scribbles notes on a clipboard, types it up at end of shift, supervisor reviews. 30-45 minutes per incident. The AI way: guard opens Otter.ai on their phone, describes what happened conversationally ("At 11:42 PM I observed a male approximately 6 feet tall in a dark hoodie near the loading dock on the north side..."). Otter transcribes and ChatGPT structures it into a clean incident report with timestamp, location, description, and action taken. Total time: 5 minutes.

2. Video surveillance review

A client reports a theft from their warehouse. There are 8 cameras, 72 hours of footage. The old way: a guard watches at 4x speed for 6+ hours. The AI way: BriefCam indexes all footage and lets you search "red truck, between 10 PM and 2 AM, loading dock camera" — it surfaces the 3 minutes of relevant footage in seconds. For security companies offering investigative services, this turns a billable 8-hour job into a 1-hour job while delivering better results.

3. Shift scheduling and coverage

Security runs 24/7, which means constant scheduling headaches — call-outs, overtime, post coverage. When I Work's AI scheduler considers guard certifications (armed vs. unarmed), post requirements, labor laws, and guard availability preferences to auto-generate schedules. One 50-guard company we talked to cut their scheduling time from 6 hours/week to 45 minutes.

4. Client reporting and proposals

Security companies live and die by client renewals. Monthly reports — incident summaries, patrol verification, response times — used to take a supervisor a full day to compile. ChatGPT ingests the raw incident data and generates a client-ready executive summary in minutes. The same tool drafts compelling proposals for new contracts, incorporating the specific site's risk profile and requirements.

How we picked

Every tool on this list was actively used by at least 3 security companies with 10+ guards. We tested each for 2+ weeks in real operations — patrol shifts, incident scenarios, client reporting cycles. We scored on time saved, accuracy of AI output, ease of use for non-technical guards, and price-to-value. We removed anything that required guards to type more than they already do.

The verdict

Start with Otter.ai (free tier, $17/mo Pro) — it's the single biggest time-saver for incident reporting. Add ChatGPT Plus ($20/mo) for structuring reports and drafting proposals. For companies with 10+ guards, Trackforce Valiant ($15/guard/mo) is the operational backbone — tour tracking, scheduling, and reporting in one. BriefCam is a premium add-on for companies that want to offer video investigation services (it's expensive but differentiating). The voice-first stack (Otter + ChatGPT) costs $37/month total and saves 10+ hours per guard per week.
