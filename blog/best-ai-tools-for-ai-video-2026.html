What we found

Runway and Kling led on raw visual quality and motion coherence. Veo edged both on photorealism but sits behind enterprise gates. For talking-head and corporate video, Synthesia and Heygen are in a league of their own — no camera needed. Pika and Kaiber shine for stylized short-form social content.

Picking by use case

Ads & film: Runway or Kling. Corporate/training: Synthesia or Heygen. Social clips: Pika, Kaiber, Genmo. Maximum realism: Veo (if you can get access).

Watch the clock

Most tools bill by credits, and a 10-second 4K clip burns several. Budget accordingly, and always keep a human in the loop for factual claims in generated video.

FAQ

Which AI video tool is best for beginners?

Pika and Kaiber have the gentlest learning curve and fast results. Synthesia is best if you just need a talking-head spokesperson from a script.

Can I use AI-generated video commercially?

Yes on paid tiers, but check each platform’s license for trainable models and likeness rights. Avoid generating real people without consent.

What does AI video cost?

Entry tiers start around $8–$12/mo (Pika, Runway, Kling). Enterprise photoreal models like Veo are custom-priced.
