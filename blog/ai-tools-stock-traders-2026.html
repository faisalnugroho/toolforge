How AI helps (and where it hurts)

Where AI genuinely adds an edge

  • Earnings summarization. "NVDA Q3 2026 earnings in 10 bullet points, focus on guidance and data center revenue" — Perplexity or Claude does this in 20 seconds vs 20 minutes of skimming transcripts.
  • Backtest scripting. ChatGPT Advanced Data Analysis turns "test a 20/50 EMA crossover on SPY from 2015" into runnable Python in 60 seconds. You still need to know what you're testing.
  • News sentiment at scale. AlphaSense reads every broker note and surfaces sentiment shifts across an entire sector in minutes. Doing this manually would take a team.
  • Pattern recognition. TrendSpider's automated trendline and pattern detection genuinely catches things your eye misses — especially on lower timeframes.
  • Research depth. "Compare NVDA vs AMD vs INTC data center strategy, last 24 months" — Claude + Perplexity does in 5 minutes what used to take a junior analyst a week.

Where AI is a liability

  • "Predict the market" services. 99% are scams. Don't trust any AI that claims to forecast prices.
  • Trading signals with no transparency. If you can't see the logic, you're not trading — you're gambling.
  • Real-time execution. AI latency + brokerage latency + market latency = slippage. Don't use AI to place trades; use it to inform trades.
  • Hallucinated financial data. LLMs invent numbers confidently. Always verify from primary sources (10-Ks, transcripts, official filings).

Stack recommendations by trader type

For swing traders (hold days to weeks): TradingView (charting) + Perplexity (news) + ChatGPT (backtest scripting). Total: ~$53/mo. This stack gives you 80% of what a $2K/mo Bloomberg gives you.

For day traders: TrendSpider (automated patterns) + Perplexity (real-time news) + a direct broker feed (Interactive Brokers, TradeStation). Add TradingView for visualization. Total: ~$60/mo.

For long-term investors: AlphaSense (research depth) + Claude (thesis stress-testing) + a quarterly review with AI summaries. Total: ~$50/mo.

For prop desk / serious professional: Bloomberg Terminal ($2K+/mo) + AlphaSense Enterprise ($12K+/yr) + custom Python ML on top. The terminal is still the deepest data set. Period.

Compliance note

Most retail AI tools are fine for personal use. If you're a registered investment advisor (RIA) or work at a fund, check with compliance before using AI on client portfolios. Several tools (especially the free tiers of ChatGPT and Claude) reserve the right to use inputs for training — a no-go for material non-public information.
