The tier-1 deflection stack

The biggest ROI in support AI is deflecting tier-1 tickets before a human sees them. The math: average support salary is $28/hour fully loaded. If your AI deflects 1,000 tier-1 tickets/month, that's $1,400 in saved labor. The cost of the AI tools to do it? Less than $300/mo combined. ROI: 5x minimum.

The standard stack: Intercom Fin (or Ada, Forethought) as the front-line AI agent, trained on your knowledge base. Zapier or Make to push resolved tickets to your CRM, billing, and Slack. Notion AI to keep the knowledge base current — every resolved ticket becomes a draft article, reviewed and published weekly.

The human-augmentation stack

For tickets that reach a human agent, AI makes them 2-3x faster. The stack: ChatGPT Team with a custom GPT trained on your support macros, product docs, and tone of voice. Agents paste the customer's question, get a 5-second draft response, edit, send. Fireflies.ai transcribes support calls for QA, sentiment analysis, and pattern detection. ElevenLabs handles phone support IVR for common questions — natural voice, no robotic tree menus.

The video support trend (2026)

The newest AI support play: video answers. Tools like D-ID and Synthesia turn FAQ text into short AI avatar videos. Embed them in your help center. CSAT jumps 15-20% on video answers vs. text — customers prefer watching a 30-second explainer over reading 500 words. Worth testing for any B2C support org.

What to skip in 2026

Two categories to be skeptical of: (1) AI phone agents that try to handle complex support calls — they work for simple Q&A, fail on edge cases, and frustrate customers. Stick with human phone agents for anything beyond "what's my balance". (2) AI ticketing tools that auto-route — they're better than rules-based routing but still 70% accurate. Human triage is still worth it for high-stakes tickets.
