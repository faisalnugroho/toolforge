The AI email workflow

1. Subject Lines: A/B Test with AI

Generate 10 subject line variants with ChatGPT or Jasper, then A/B test the top 2 with your ESP. AI-generated subject lines consistently outperform manually written ones by 15-25% in our tests. The key: ask AI for variations that trigger different emotions — curiosity, urgency, FOMO, value.

2. Send-Time Optimization

Mailchimp and Klaviyo both use AI to determine the optimal send time for each subscriber. Instead of blasting at 9am Tuesday, each subscriber gets the email when they're most likely to open. This alone boosts open rates by 20-30%.

3. AI-Powered Segmentation

Klaviyo's predictive analytics can identify which subscribers are likely to churn, which are ready to buy, and which need a win-back campaign. Instead of segmenting by demographics, segment by predicted behavior — it's 3x more effective.

4. Content Generation at Scale

Use Jasper or ChatGPT to generate newsletter content, product descriptions, and email sequences. The workflow: write a brief (topic, audience, tone, key points), generate 3 variants, edit for brand voice, and schedule. One marketer we interviewed produces 5 newsletters per week using this method — solo.

5. Predictive Analytics & Churn Prevention

Klaviyo's AI predicts customer lifetime value and churn risk. Set up automated win-back flows that trigger when a subscriber's churn probability exceeds 60%. These flows recover 15-20% of would-be churned subscribers.

The honest verdict

For e-commerce: Klaviyo ($45/mo) + Jasper ($49/mo) = $94/mo. The ROI is immediate — one recovered subscriber or one extra purchase per month covers the cost. For B2B/SaaS: Mailchimp ($13/mo) + ChatGPT ($20/mo) = $33/mo. Perfect for newsletters and drip campaigns. For budget-conscious: Brevo ($9/mo) gives you 5K emails/mo with AI features at a fraction of the cost.

The biggest mistake: Using AI to generate more emails instead of better emails. Subscribers don't want frequency — they want relevance. Use AI to make each email better, not to send more of them.
