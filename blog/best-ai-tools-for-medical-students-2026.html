How medical students actually use these

1. Active recall with Anki + AI. Feed your lecture notes into ChatGPT and ask it to generate 20 Anki cards in cloze-deletion format. Paste directly into Anki. This turns 3 hours of manual card creation into 5 minutes.

2. Clinical reasoning practice. Use ChatGPT to generate patient vignettes with lab values, symptoms, and history. Practice your differential diagnosis, then ask the AI to critique your approach. It's like having an attending on call 24/7.

3. Literature reviews without the pain. Perplexity searches PubMed and primary sources with citations. Ask "what's the evidence for metformin in PCOS?" and get a structured answer with real studies — not blog posts.

4. Lecture capture that's actually useful. Otter.ai transcribes every lecture. Search "Krebs cycle" and jump to the exact moment your professor explained it. Combined with Notion AI summaries, you never lose a lecture again.

What NOT to use AI for in med school

Never use AI for actual clinical decisions during rotations. Always verify drug interactions, dosages, and contraindications against UpToDate, Lexicomp, or your institution's clinical guidelines. AI models hallucinate — and in medicine, hallucinations can hurt patients. Use AI for learning, not treating.

The verdict

Start with ChatGPT Plus ($20/mo) — it's the single highest-ROI tool for a med student. Add Anki (free) for spaced repetition. Use Perplexity for evidence-based research. The rest is optional but helpful. Total cost: $20-40/mo, which is less than one textbook.
