Why sommeliers need AI in 2026

Time savings

The average sommelier writes 20-40 tasting notes per week. AI drafts them in seconds; you spend 60 seconds editing instead of 15 minutes writing from scratch.

Consistency

AI descriptions hit the same structure (appearance, nose, palate, pairing) every time. New hires sound like 20-year veterans.

Language support

AI translates tasting notes into 30+ languages. A French Chateau's note is now accessible to your Mandarin-speaking guests in seconds.

Pairing logic

Modern pairing AIs understand why Pinot Noir works with mushroom risotto (umami + earth) -- not just "red wine with red meat" rules.

How to choose

For a restaurant or wine bar, you want three things: a label-ID tool (Vivino), a note generator (TastingNotesGPT or Wine Folly), and a pairing engine (Pairwise or Cellar.ai). Total cost: under $100/mo. The ROI is immediate -- your team writes notes 10x faster and your guests get better recommendations.

The verdict

AI won't replace a great sommelier's palate -- but it will replace the 5 hours/week you spend on mechanical writing and research. Start with TastingNotesGPT (free trial) and Vivino Pro. Add the others as you scale.
