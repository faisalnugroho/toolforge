How we picked

We surveyed 150+ lawyers across BigLaw (AmLaw 200), boutique firms, and solo practices. We scored each tool on: time saved per week, accuracy of legal output, ease of integration with existing workflows, and "would you recommend this to a colleague?" We removed anything that required extensive training or IT support — every tool here works out of the box.

The recommended stack

Solo practitioners: Claude Pro ($20/mo) for drafting + Casetext ($89/mo) for research. Total: $109/mo, saves 10+ hours/week.

Small firms (2-20 lawyers): Harvey AI (enterprise) for research + Spellbook ($150/mo) for contract drafting. Total: ~$500/mo per lawyer.

BigLaw: Harvey AI enterprise license. The ROI is immediate — one saved billable hour per day pays for the entire license.

Important caveat

⚠️ Never cite AI-generated case law without verification. AI models can hallucinate legal citations. Always verify every case citation, statute reference, and legal argument in primary sources before filing or advising clients. Use AI as a research accelerator, not a replacement for legal judgment.
