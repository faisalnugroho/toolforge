How to Use AI for Tax Season 2026 (Cut Prep Time by 60%)

Tax season doesn't have to mean 70-hour weeks. Here's the exact AI playbook accountants, bookkeepers, and organized solo filers use to move faster — while keeping the human accountable for every number.

TF
ToolForge Editorial
✍️ Published June 2026 ⏱️ 4 min read 🔄 Updated June 16, 2026

Rule one: AI prepares, you sign. The IRS still wants a human's name on the return. Use these tools to kill the busywork — categorization, document extraction, draft responses to notices — and keep your judgment for the decisions that matter.

Step 1 — Auto-categorize the shoebox

Drop a client's bank and card exports into ChatGPT or Claude and ask for a Schedule C-ready category map. For recurring clients, save the mapping as a custom instruction so every month looks the same. Most firms reclaim 8-12 hours per return here alone.

Step 2 — Extract from PDFs and photos

1099s, K-1s, and crumpled receipts are no longer a data-entry afternoon. AssemblyAI handles audio (client calls), while multimodal models in ChatGPT pull line items straight off a phone photo of a receipt. Always eyeball the totals before they hit the workbook.

Step 3 — Draft notice responses

An IRS CP2000 lands in the client's inbox. Paste it into Claude, attach last year's return, and ask for a point-by-point response draft. You edit, you sign, you send. What used to be a billable scramble becomes a 20-minute task.

Step 4 — Research with citations

For anything gray-area, Perplexity and You.com return sourced answers you can actually cite to a partner or client — not a hallucinated statute section.

The 60% number, realistically

AI won't do the judgment. But it removes the extraction, categorization, and first-draft grind that eats most of a junior's week. Firms that adopt this stack report 40-60% faster turnaround on straightforward 1040s and Schedule Cs — with fewer transposition errors because the model doesn't get tired at 11pm.
