How to actually deploy this stack in a week

Most electricians don't need all 8. Here's the realistic rollout by role:

  1. Solo electricians: Start with ChatGPT Plus + Fathom (free) + Canva Pro. That's $33/month and replaces 3-5 hours/week of admin.
  2. Small shops (2-10 electricians): Add Jobber for scheduling and invoicing ($69/mo). Use its AI quote follow-up to win back 20-30% of unsigned estimates.
  3. Mid-market ECs (10-50 electricians): ServiceTitan is the standard. Pay for the AI add-on ($50-100/mo more) for AI dispatch and AI call summary. Add Otter for office phone recording.
  4. Large commercial ECs: Layer Procore for project management on top of ServiceTitan. Use Gamma for proposal generation. Murf AI for safety training videos.
  5. All: Spend 30 minutes teaching ChatGPT your service area, common service types (panel upgrades, EV charger installs, generator installs), and pricing structure. The quality of every response improves 3x.

What it costs (and what it saves)

The full stack above ranges from $33/month (solo) to $400+/month (mid-market with ServiceTitan). For most electrical contractors, that's less than 0.5% of revenue. The ROI math: AI-drafted estimates cut estimating time from 60-90 minutes to 5-15 minutes. If you do 10 estimates/week, that's 10-15 hours back per week, worth $400-$1,000 in your time. Plus AI quote follow-ups typically recover 15-30% of unsigned estimates. The stack pays for itself in week 1.

Common mistakes to avoid

  • Trusting AI for code interpretation without verification. ChatGPT is great for explaining NEC concepts, but always verify against the actual 2023/2026 NEC text. Use it as a tutor, not an authority.
  • Skipping customer consent for call recording. Two-party consent states (CA, FL, IL, MA, PA, WA, etc.) require all-party consent. Check your state laws before turning on Otter or Fathom.
  • Using AI to generate fake reviews or testimonials. FTC and Google will penalize you. Use AI to respond to real reviews, not fabricate them.
  • Letting AI auto-send estimates to customers. Estimates need human review for accuracy, scope, and price. AI drafts, you review and send.
  • Not training ChatGPT on your business. Generic ChatGPT responses are OK. ChatGPT that knows your service area, pricing, and specialties is great. Spend 30 minutes upfront.
