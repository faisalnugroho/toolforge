How we picked

Every tool on this list was recommended by at least 3 licensed therapists currently in private practice. We prioritized HIPAA compliance (BAA available), integration with existing EHRs (SimplePractice, TherapyNotes, Jane), and tools that don't add screen time to actual sessions. We excluded anything that involves sharing identifiable client data with consumer AI without proper agreements.

What to look for

The single most important thing: HIPAA compliance with a signed BAA. Consumer AI tools (free ChatGPT, free Otter, free Notion) are not HIPAA-compliant and should never see client data. Every tool on this list has a BAA available on a paid plan.

Second priority: workflow integration. The best tools disappear into your existing process. If a new tool means learning a separate app for every session, it won't stick. The winners here are the ones that quietly capture info while you do the work.

The documentation stack we recommend

Step 1: Capture — Use Fathom Health (HIPAA-compliant) or paid Otter Business to transcribe sessions. Both offer a BAA. Skip the free tiers — they aren't HIPAA-compliant.

Step 2: Draft — Paste the transcript into a HIPAA-compliant AI summarizer (or your EHR's built-in AI, if you have one) to generate a first-draft SOAP or DAP note. Always edit before signing — these are not perfect.

Step 3: Store — Keep notes in your EHR (SimplePractice, TherapyNotes, Jane). Use Notion only for de-identified resources and treatment plan templates.

Step 4: Bill — Use Headway or your EHR's built-in billing. Don't let insurance claims eat your weekend.

What to avoid

Free ChatGPT, free Claude.ai, free Gemini, free Perplexity — none of these are HIPAA-compliant. Pasting client session notes, names, or identifiers into them is a HIPAA violation, even for a 'quick edit.' The fines start at $50K per incident.

Also avoid consumer transcription apps that don't offer a BAA: Apple Dictation, Google Recorder, free Otter, free Notta. They are great for personal use and dangerous for clinical work.

The verdict

Start with Fathom Health (or your EHR's built-in transcription) for session capture, and Cal.ai for scheduling email automation. That's it for week one. The ROI on documentation tools compounds fast — most therapists in our survey cut their weekly admin from 6 hours to 2.5 hours within 30 days, freeing up an entire evening for actual life.
