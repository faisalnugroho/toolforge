Top picks, explained

ChatGPT

Draft WPS-style documentation, safety briefs, and client-facing explanations of what a job involves — then have a supervisor review. Huge time-saver for compliance paperwork.

Notion

One workspace for quotes, before/after photos, and repeat-client history. Beats a pile of paper receipts in the truck.

Otter

Dictate a job walkthrough on site; Otter turns it into searchable notes you can drop into a quote later.

Canva

Clean, branded quote sheets and yard signs without a graphic designer or a fax machine.

Adobe Express

Quickly polish project photos for your truck signage, Google Business profile, and word-of-mouth referrals.

Descript

Record a 60-second explainer of a repair and edit it like a doc — great for quoting complex jobs remotely.

How to choose

Welding is the product; everything else is overhead. Spend on tools that cut overhead, not on shiny demos.

  • Start with Notion (jobs + quotes) and Otter (capture site visits). That pair alone saves hours a week.
  • Use ChatGPT for any document you'd otherwise dread writing.
  • Add Canva / Adobe Express only when you need branded client-facing materials.
  • Skip video tools unless you already post to social for leads.

FAQ

Is AI going to replace welders?

No. It replaces the paperwork and quoting, not the torch. Shops using AI just bid faster and lose fewer leads to slow follow-up.

I'm not technical — can I use these?

Yes. Otter and Canva are tap-and-talk simple. Notion takes an afternoon to set up one good template.

What's the single highest-ROI tool?

Otter for capturing site visits, because that's where job details (and missed details) actually happen.

Verdict

You don't need a robot welder to benefit from AI. Notion + Otter + ChatGPT handles the admin that steals your evenings — and that's where the real money is.
