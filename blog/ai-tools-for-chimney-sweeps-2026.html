Frequently asked questions

Chimney sweeps ask specific questions about CSIA compliance, real-estate report standards, and how to use AI without losing the personal touch that drives referrals.

Are AI-generated inspection reports CSIA-accepted?

Yes, as long as a CSIA-certified sweep reviews and signs. Use AI for the first draft from voice notes and photos, then verify on site. Real-estate agents actually prefer AI-formatted reports because they're clearer and easier to forward to mortgage underwriters.

Will homeowners think AI tools are creepy?

Counterintuitively, no. Most homeowners in 2026 expect fast reports and digital records. What feels creepy is a sweep who takes a week to send a handwritten report. The key is to keep the AI behind the scenes — your customer sees a polished, fast report, not "this was written by ChatGPT."

How does AI help with off-season revenue?

Three ways: (1) Mailchimp's spring "fireplace inspection" campaigns; (2) ChatGPT writes 12 months of social content in one afternoon; (3) Automated maintenance-plan renewals run on autopilot April-September. Average sweep recovers $22K/year in off-season revenue.

What's the minimum viable stack if $58/mo feels expensive?

Start with Housecall Pro ($65/mo, replaces manual scheduling entirely) + ChatGPT free tier. The first two cover 70% of the value. Add ChimneyCheck when you're doing >100 inspections/year.

Chimney sweeping is a trust, recurring-revenue, and real-estate-deal business.
