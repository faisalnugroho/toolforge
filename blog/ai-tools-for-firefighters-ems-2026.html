How we picked

We talked to chiefs, training officers, and EMS coordinators at 18 departments — from 30-person volunteer combos to metro departments with 4,000+ responders. We focused on tools that: (1) work on government procurement budgets (most departments have $0 discretionary), (2) have a real track record of NFIRS/HIPAA compliance, (3) reduce — not add — documentation burden, and (4) don't require a year-long IT project. We excluded: anything that needs >10 hours of training, anything that doesn't generate audit-ready documentation, and anything priced like enterprise SaaS unless the ROI is obvious.

The verdict

Start free: ChatGPT free tier for SOG and post-incident report drafting and Canva free for prevention materials. Add ImageTrend Elite or ESO if your department still uses paper PCRs (the AI auto-fill alone justifies the cost). Veoci is the right answer for inspections/hydrants/occupancy workflows. ArcGIS only for departments with a GIS analyst on staff — it's overkill for volunteer departments.

Quick reference

Volunteer/small department: ChatGPT free + Canva free + Otter.ai = $0-20/mo

Career department (50-200): ChatGPT Plus + ImageTrend + Otter.ai = $50-200/mo per station

Mid-size metro (500+): All above + Veoci + ArcGIS online = $500-2K/mo

Large metro/regional (2K+): Enterprise tier all of the above + custom dispatch AI = $10K+/mo

Procurement notes

  • Cybersecurity review first. Any AI tool that touches patient or operational data needs to clear CJIS, HIPAA, or your state-specific requirements.
  • Use enterprise plans. Consumer ChatGPT should never see patient identifiers or operational security info. Use ChatGPT Enterprise with proper DPAs.
  • ISO/SOG documentation. AI can draft training content, but you must review against current NFPA standards and your local SOGs.
  • Union considerations. Some IAFF/IAFC locals have specific positions on AI for performance evaluation. Check before rolling out at scale.
