What AI can't replace (yet)

I'm not going to pretend AI replaces everything. Here's what I still do myself or hire for:

  • Relationship building: AI can't take a client to lunch, read the room, or build genuine trust. Networking still needs a human.
  • Complex negotiations: Contract terms, pricing discussions, and sensitive conversations need human judgment and emotional intelligence.
  • Creative judgment: AI drafts well, but the final call on what to publish, what to cut, and what's "good" is still mine.
  • Surprise problems: When things go wrong (server down, legal issue, PR crisis), you need a human who can think on their feet.
  • Physical tasks: Mail, packages, in-person errands. I use TaskRabbit for these.

Should you replace your assistant?

Honestly? It depends. If your assistant does mostly digital admin work — email, scheduling, research, drafting — yes, AI can handle 80-90% of it. If they do physical tasks, relationship management, or complex operations, you'll still need a human.

The best approach might be a hybrid: use AI tools for the repetitive 80%, and hire a part-time human (10-15 hrs/week) for the 20% that needs human touch. That gets you the best of both worlds at ~$500/mo instead of $4,000/mo.
