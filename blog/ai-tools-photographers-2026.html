Where the time savings come from

The average wedding photographer shoots 3,000-5,000 photos. Culling takes 2-4 hours. Editing takes 10-20 hours. Delivery takes 2 hours. Total: 14-26 hours. With AI: culling 30 min (Imagen/Aftershoot), editing 3-5 hours (AI batch), delivery 30 min. Total: 4-6 hours. That's 10-20 hours saved per wedding, which means 2-3 more weddings per year at $5K each = $10-15K more revenue.

The photographer's stack

Wedding/portrait: Imagen or Aftershoot for culling, Lightroom for editing, Pixieset or SmugMug for delivery. Commercial/product: Topaz Photo AI for upscale/denoise, Photoroom for backgrounds, Adobe Express for social. Fine art: Capture One for editing, Topaz for quality. The stack varies by genre, but every working photographer in 2026 uses at least 2-3 of these.
