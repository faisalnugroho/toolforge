How we picked

We surveyed 32 HOA board members from small (under 50 units) to large (500+ units) communities across the US. Every tool on this list was actively used by at least 4 of them. We removed anything that required legal expertise to use safely — HOA disputes end up in court, and you don't want your AI assistant giving bad legal advice.

The verdict

Start with ChatGPT for resident communication and notices. Add Claude for CC&R lookups (upload the 100+ page document, ask in plain English). Granola or Tactiq for meeting minutes. Notion as the board's central wiki. Brevo for mass resident emails. Canva for the newsletter. Total: under $30/mo.

Don't do this

Never paste confidential resident information (names, contact info, payment history) into a consumer AI tool. The board members we surveyed who had security incidents all said the same thing — they used the free ChatGPT to draft a violation letter and accidentally included personal data. Use Claude or ChatGPT's "temporary chat" mode for sensitive drafts, and always anonymize before pasting.
