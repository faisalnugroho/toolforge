The compliance reality (read this first)

The ABA's Formal Opinion 512 (July 2024) said lawyers must understand the benefits and risks of generative AI, supervise its output, and protect client confidentiality. Translation: you can't outsource judgment to a chatbot, and you can't paste client secrets into free ChatGPT.

Two non-negotiables for legal AI in 2026:

  1. Use a zero-retention tier. ChatGPT Team, ChatGPT Enterprise, Claude for Work, or Harvey all commit to not training on your data. The free and Plus tiers of consumer products do not.
  2. Verify every citation. AI still hallucinates. Always check case citations, statutes, and quotations against the primary source. Tools that ground their answers in verified databases (Lexis+ AI, Westlaw Precision) are safer for legal research.

How we picked

Every tool on this list was vetted against three criteria: (1) it ships with a real legal-specific product, not a generic chatbot, (2) it offers a no-training-on-your-data contract, and (3) at least 3 law firms we spoke to use it in production. We also weighted price-to-value heavily — most solo and small-firm lawyers can't afford a $100K/year Harvey deployment.

The verdict

Big law & in-house: Harvey AI plus Westlaw or Lexis+ AI. Budget is not the constraint; quality is.

Solo & small firm: Clause (contracts) + ChatGPT Team (drafting) + Perplexity Pro (research) + Otter (depositions). Total: ~$80/month. Replaces $5K/month in associate time.

Plaintiff-side & litigation: Lexis+ AI or Westlaw Precision (case law research) + Fireflies (client intake) + ChatGPT (drafts). The case-law tools pay for themselves if you avoid even one bad citation.
