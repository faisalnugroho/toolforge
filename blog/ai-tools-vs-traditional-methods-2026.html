AI wins clearly

Meeting notes. Otter and Fireflies beat a human scribe on cost and accuracy for capture (not judgment). First drafts. ChatGPT and Claude outpace a blank page every time. Transcript editing. Descript’s text-based editing beats timeline trimming for talking-head video. Voiceover. ElevenLabs beats hiring a narrator for volume work. Training video. HeyGen and Synthesia beat booking a studio for routine explainers.

Traditional still wins

Final creative judgment. AI can suggest, but a human owns taste. High-stakes writing (contracts, medical, legal) needs a professional’s sign-off. Trust and nuance — a real narrator’s inflection still beats synthetic voice for brand-defining spots. And anything requiring accountability: someone has to own the output, and that someone isn’t the model.

The verdict

Use AI to win the speed and volume game, and keep humans on judgment, taste, and accountability. The teams that thrive in 2026 treat AI as a junior partner, not a replacement.
