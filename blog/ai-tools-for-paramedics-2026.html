How we picked

Every tool was tested in the field by working paramedics. We prioritized tools that work on phone/tablet, work offline, and respect the realities of EMS — noisy environments, gloved hands, seconds matter. Tools that auto-populate patient care reports (PCRs) got top marks.

The verdict

For documentation, Abridge or Suki (both enterprise) are the only tools that meaningfully cut PCR time. For individual medics, ChatGPT ($20/mo Plus) is the best bang-for-buck — drug calcs, protocol lookup, voice mode in the cab. Wolfram Alpha ($8/mo) is the unsung hero for any medics running peds calls.
