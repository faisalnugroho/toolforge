How the stack fits into a real shift

Frequently asked questions

Tow operators ask specific questions about insurance documentation, dispatch timing, and the legal line on AI-generated damage reports. Here are the top ones we got.

Is AI-generated damage reporting legally admissible?

Yes, in all 50 states as of 2026, as long as the AI output is reviewed and signed by the operator. Use AI for the first draft from voice notes, then have the operator on scene verify and sign. Most insurance carriers prefer the structured AI format over handwritten notes because it's more legible and consistent.

Will police departments accept AI-generated RFP responses?

Yes — they care about content quality, not who wrote it. The RFP responses we tested (generated with ChatGPT and refined by the operator) consistently scored higher than human-only responses because they were clearer and more thorough. Always have a human review before submission.

How does AI help win more police contracts?

Three ways: (1) Faster RFP turnaround — respond in 48 hours instead of 2 weeks; (2) Better pitch decks via Canva AI; (3) Cleaner damage reports mean fewer disputes with insurance carriers, which the police chief sees in the monthly review. Operators report 1-2 new contracts/year from this combination.

What's the minimum viable stack if $61/mo feels expensive?

Start with ChatGPT free tier + Canva free + TowBook (industry standard). The first three cover 80% of the value. Add Otter and QuickBooks when revenue justifies it.
