How we judged them

We recorded a 3-minute sample, cloned on each platform, then had 20 people blind-guess which was the real voice. ElevenLabs and Respeecher fooled the room most often. For live use — voice agents, real-time dubbing — Rime AI and Cartesia won on latency. Pricing ranged from $5/mo (ElevenLabs free-ish tier) to enterprise-only (Respeecher, Resemble).

Which one should you pick?

Creators & YouTubers: ElevenLabs — best realism, easiest UI. Live voice agents: Rime AI or Cartesia for sub-second latency. Film & games: Respeecher for uncanny fidelity. Big teams: Resemble or Murf with workflow controls.

Stay safe

Every tool here now requires consent and most watermark output. Clone only voices you own or are authorized to use. Responsible use keeps the whole category alive.

FAQ

Is AI voice cloning legal?

Yes when you have consent. Most platforms require proof of permission and watermark synthetic audio. Cloning someone without authorization can violate voice-identity and deepfake laws.

How much audio do I need?

Typically 1–5 minutes of clean speech. More audio means a more accurate clone. Real-time platforms like Rime can clone faster but still want a few minutes.

What is the cheapest option?

ElevenLabs and PlayHT both have low-cost tiers (~$5–$31/mo). Rime AI is pay-as-you-go per character, great if you only clone occasionally.
