How we picked

We surveyed 100+ product designers at companies like Linear, Stripe, and Vercel. Every tool on this list is used weekly by at least 5 working designers. We tested each for 2+ weeks on real client and product work, scoring on output quality, time saved, integration with existing workflows, and price-to-value.

The workflow that 10x's your output

1. Research phase. Use Perplexity to research users, competitors, and design patterns. Ask it to summarize 5 competing products' onboarding flows. Output: a research brief in Notion.

2. Structure phase. Feed the brief into Relume. It generates a sitemap and wireframe structure in minutes — not hours. Export to Figma.

3. Visual exploration. Use Midjourney to generate mood boards and style references. "Clean SaaS dashboard, dark mode, Linear-inspired, minimal" gets you 4 variants to react to.

4. High-fidelity design. Figma AI suggests component layouts and generates variants. You refine — the AI handles the 80% grunt work.

5. Handoff. v0 generates production React/Tailwind components from your designs. Engineers get working code, not just a Figma file.

The verdict

If you design digital products, Figma AI + v0 is the non-negotiable core. Add Midjourney for visual exploration and Relume if you do web work. Total: $30-60/mo, which pays for itself in your first sprint.
