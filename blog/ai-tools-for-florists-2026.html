Top picks, explained

Canva

Canva's AI magic tools resize a single design across Instagram, Facebook, and print. Florists use it for seasonal menus, sale cards, and polished story templates without hiring a designer.

ChatGPT

Draft empathetic copy for sympathy arrangements, punchy captions for birthday bouquets, and SEO-friendly Google Business descriptions. Keep the human touch — let it do the typing.

Midjourney

Mock up new arrangements before cutting a single stem. Great for pitching wedding concepts and testing color palettes with clients remotely.

ElevenLabs

Turn a written promo into a warm voiceover for Instagram Reels and TikTok without booking a narrator.

CapCut

Auto-captions, trendy templates, and one-tap enhancements make a 30-second bouquet build look cinematic on a phone.

Grammarly

Catch typos in wedding proposals and condolence notes — the moments where a stray error really stings.

How to choose

Pick tools by the job, not the hype. Most shops only need three: a design tool, a writing tool, and a video tool.

  • Design: Canva covers 90% of shops. Add Midjourney only if you pitch custom wedding work.
  • Writing: ChatGPT for speed, Grammarly as a safety net on anything client-facing.
  • Video: CapCut is free and more than enough for Reels and TikTok.
  • Voice: ElevenLabs only if you post video regularly.

FAQ

Do I need to pay for any of these?

No. Canva, ChatGPT, CapCut, ElevenLabs, and Grammarly all have free tiers that cover a solo florist's needs. Midjourney is the only paid tool worth considering, and only for custom-event pitching.

Will AI make my shop feel impersonal?

Only if you copy-paste. Use AI for drafts and first passes, then add your own voice — especially on sympathy and wedding work where tone matters most.

What's the fastest win?

Set up 5 reusable Canva templates (one per occasion) and let ChatGPT write the captions. You'll post consistently without thinking about it.

Verdict

You don't need a marketing degree to fill the calendar in 2026. Canva + ChatGPT + CapCut gets most florists 80% of the way; add Midjourney only when you're pitching bespoke events.
