How we picked

We surveyed 35 wedding planners across the US, UK, and Australia — solo operators, boutique studios, and luxury agencies. Tools were scored on time saved per wedding, client satisfaction impact, taste/quality of output, and — crucially — whether they help you book more weddings or just save time. We excluded anything that produced generic, AI-looking designs that clients can spot. Every tool on this list has been battle-tested on real weddings with real budgets from $40K to $400K+.

The verdict

Start with Canva Pro for the visual work — mood boards, save-the-dates, seating charts, signage. Add Midjourney for the hero concept art that wins the proposal. Notion AI is your master planning system — the timeline, vendor database, and budget tracker in one. Perplexity Pro handles the research (vendors, trends, etiquette) with citations you can show clients. Copy.ai drafts the vendor emails and intake forms. Descript edits the highlight reels. ElevenLabs generates voiceovers for accessibility. Trello and Evernote are the underrated workhorses — pick whichever fits your brain better.
