Two different jobs

Most "transcription" buyers are actually doing one of two things. If you record for publication — podcasts, interviews, video — you want output quality and an editor that lets you fix mistakes. That points to Trint for journalism and Descript for audio/video production. If you capture live meetings, you want real-time notes and search, which is Otter and Fireflies territory.

When accuracy is non-negotiable

For courts, universities, and compliance, a raw AI transcript is a liability. Verbit adds human review to hit the accuracy and captioning standards those environments require, and Rev offers a human+AI path when you need a certified result fast.

The bottom line

Don't pay enterprise rates for meeting notes, and don't trust a $10 tool with a court filing. Match the tool to the job: Trint/Descript for production, Otter/Fireflies for live capture, Verbit/Rev for compliance.
