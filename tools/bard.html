What Google Bard does best

Google’s experimental conversational AI. The precursor to Gemini — still pulling huge legacy search volume. We tested it across real workflows to find where it shines and where it stumbles.

Who it's for: AI Assistant users who want a faster, AI-assisted workflow without a steep learning curve.

Key features

Conversational Q&AMultimodal chat

Bard answered questions, drafted text, and brainstormed ideas in a clean chat UI backed by Google’s LaMDA/PaLM models.

Google Workspace tie-inDocs, Gmail, Sheets

Deep integration with Google’s productivity suite let users draft and refine inside the apps they already used.

Web-grounded answersReal-time info

Unlike early ChatGPT, Bard pulled live web results, giving fresher answers on current events.

Multilingual support40+ languages

Launched globally with broad language coverage, a key advantage in non-English markets.

The honest take

✓ What works

  • Free and instantly available to anyone with a Google account
  • Strong multilingual and non-English performance
  • Live web grounding for current events
  • Tight Workspace integration

✗ What doesn't

  • Now superseded by Gemini — Bard branding is retired
  • Inconsistent reasoning vs Claude/ChatGPT at the time
  • No API of its own; folded into Gemini

Verdict

Google Bard earns a spot in a modern AI stack if you value ai assistant speed and quality. Free is reasonable for what it delivers — and the free tier is enough to decide if it fits.
