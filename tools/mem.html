The notes app that thinks for you

Mem reimagines note-taking for the AI era. No folders. No tags. No organization system to maintain. You write notes, Mem's AI (powered by GPT-4) automatically tags, links to related notes, surfaces relevant information when you need it, and even drafts responses based on your past thinking. The 'Mem X' tier adds ChatGPT-style features that understand the full context of your knowledge base.

Who it's for: Knowledge workers, writers, researchers, and anyone drowning in notes who never finds anything. Especially good for people who've tried Notion, Obsidian, Roam, and want something that requires less maintenance.

Key features

🤖 Auto-Organization

Write a note. Mem's AI tags it, links it to related notes, and adds it to a timeline. No manual filing. No 'I'll organize this later' procrastination.

💬 Mem X Chat

Ask questions about everything you've ever written. 'What did I think about X?' Mem searches, synthesizes, and answers based on YOUR notes — not generic AI.

🔗 Smart Connections

Mem surfaces related notes you forgot you wrote. Reading a note about AI? It shows your notes on related topics from months ago. Serendipitous discovery.

📱 Cross-Platform

Native macOS, Windows, iOS, Android apps. Web clipper for saving articles. Email-to-note for capturing thoughts on the go.

The honest take

✓ What works

  • Genuinely magical — feels like having a second brain that actually works
  • No organization tax — just write, AI handles the rest
  • Mem X Chat answers questions based on your own knowledge
  • Beautiful, minimal interface that gets out of your way
  • Faster than Notion for capturing and finding thoughts

✗ What doesn't

  • No folders means you can't 'force' a structure even if you want to
  • Free tier is very limited (must pay for AI features)
  • Smaller ecosystem than Notion (fewer integrations)
  • Search is AI-dependent — sometimes misses obvious keyword matches

Verdict

The right pick for knowledge workers who value capture and discovery over manual organization. If you've ever said 'I wrote that down somewhere but can't find it,' Mem solves that. If you need strict folder structures, stick with Notion.
