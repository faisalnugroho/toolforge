What Hootsuite does best

Hootsuite is one of the tools our readers ask about most in the social media space. We put it through two weeks of real workflows — here's the honest breakdown of where it shines and where it falls short.

Who it's for: The social media command center for teams that post everywhere.

Key features

Unified Inbox All DMs in one place

Pull mentions, comments, and DMs from every connected network into a single triage inbox your whole team can work.

Bulk Scheduler Plan weeks ahead

Upload a CSV of posts or use the planner to queue content across profiles with optimal-time suggestions.

Analytics Prove ROI

Customizable dashboards track reach, engagement, and conversions — then export board-ready PDF reports.

App Directory Extend it

Connect Canva, Salesforce, Adobe, and 150+ apps to fit Hootsuite into your existing stack.

The honest take

✓ What works

  • Most mature scheduler on the market
  • Best-in-class analytics exports
  • Solid team permissions and approvals
  • OwlyGPT AI caption helper built in
  • Wide network support

✗ What doesn't

  • Pricing is steep for solo creators
  • Interface feels dated vs Buffer
  • Add-on costs stack up fast
  • Learning curve for new teams

Verdict

Hootsuite earns its place in the social media stack if you want a tool that's genuinely better than the free default for serious work. We'd start on the trial,prove it on one real workflow, then upgrade only if it pays for itself.
