Boards, now with brains

Trello is the classic visual Kanban board, and in 2026 Atlassian Intelligence adds AI card summaries, draft content, and smart suggestions — plus Butler automation and a deep Power-Up marketplace.

Who it's for: Small teams and individuals who want visual, low-learning-curve task management with light AI help.

Key features

Boards Visual Kanban

Drag-and-drop cards across lists — the simplest way to see work move.

Atlassian Intelligence AI on cards

Summarize threads, draft card content, and suggest next steps with AI.

Butler No-code automation

Automate moves, due dates, and rules without writing code.

Power-Ups Extend

Add calendars, integrations, and views from a large marketplace.

The honest take

✓ What works

  • Dead-simple to learn
  • AI summaries save real time
  • Butler automation is powerful
  • Free tier is generous
  • Great with Jira for scaling

✗ What doesn't

  • Less depth than Asana/ClickUp
  • AI features need a paid plan
  • Big projects get cluttered
  • Power-Ups add cost

Verdict

Trello remains the friendliest way to get a team onto boards, and Atlassian Intelligence finally makes the AI useful for planning and summaries. Choose Asana or ClickUp if you need heavy project structure; pick Trello for speed and clarity.
