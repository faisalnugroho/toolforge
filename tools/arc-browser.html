The browser that finally fixes tabs, plus an AI that browses for you

Arc is the most radical rethink of what a browser can be since Chrome. Vertical tabs, spaces for projects, easels for visual work, and a built-in AI agent (Arc Max) that can summarize pages, rename tabs automatically, and execute multi-step browsing tasks. In 2026 Arc is what Chrome would be if Google actually cared about user experience.

Who it's for: Knowledge workers drowning in tabs, researchers juggling 50+ open URLs, designers and writers who want a browser that does not feel like it was built in 2008.

Key features

Tabs Vertical tabs + spaces

Vertical sidebar with auto-archiving after 14 days. Spaces group tabs by project. The end of "I have 87 tabs open" shame.

Arc Max Built-in AI

Renames tab groups automatically, summarizes any page in 5 bullets, drafts emails from a URL, generates cover images.

Browse for Me AI agent

Arc can browse 3-5 pages for you, extract the answer, and present it as a clean report. The first browser with a real Browse agent.

Easels Visual canvas

Whiteboard + screenshot annotation + notes on the side of any page. Perfect for design research.

Boosts CSS customization

Override any website's CSS. Hide ads, change fonts, recolor. No extension needed.

Sync Cross-device

Sync tabs, spaces, and easels across Mac, Windows, iOS, Android. End-to-end encrypted.

The honest take

✓ What works

  • Tabs finally make sense — spaces + auto-archive kills clutter
  • Arc Max AI is genuinely useful (renames 50 tabs in 2 seconds)
  • Browse for Me is the closest thing to a personal research assistant
  • Beautiful design that other browsers now copy
  • Boosts let you hack any site without extensions

✗ What doesn't

  • No Linux build (Mac/Win only for desktop)
  • Sync has been flaky in past releases
  • Resource-heavy — 1.5GB RAM minimum for normal use
  • The Browser Company announced Dia (successor) — Arc maintenance slowing

Verdict

Arc is the best browser for people who use their browser all day. The tab organization alone is worth switching for. The AI features are a real productivity multiplier, not a gimmick. If you live in Chrome and feel overwhelmed, give Arc a week.
