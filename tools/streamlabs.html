The all-in-one streaming toolkit. Capture, overlay, alert, and monetize — built for creators.

Streamlabs turned OBS — powerful but intimidating — into a creator-friendly streaming suite. Since 2014 it's been the on-ramp for millions of Twitch and YouTube streamers, bundling overlays, alert boxes, donation widgets, and mobile capture. In 2026, with Streamlabs Ultra adding cross-platform multistream, it's the all-in-one toolkit for creators who'd rather stream than configure.

Who it's for: Twitch, YouTube, and TikTok streamers who want a polished broadcast without a production crew. Gamers, creators, and influencers who need overlays, alerts, and donation widgets that 'just work' with OBS.

Key features

Studio Streamlabs Desktop

A fork of OBS with a friendlier UI, built-in overlays, and one-click setup. Stream to Twitch, YouTube, and TikTok simultaneously.

Alerts Alert box & widgets

Follower, sub, and donation alerts with custom sounds and animations. Tip jar, countdown, and chat box widgets drop into any scene.

Mobile Stream from phone

Streamlabs Mobile lets you go live from iOS/Android with the same overlays. Built for IRL and TikTok Live creators.

Ultimate Streamlabs Ultra

The $19/mo bundle: cross-platform multistream, premium overlays, and a 12% fee waiver on tips. One subscription replaces three tools.

The honest take

✓ What works

  • The easiest path to a professional-looking stream (overlays + alerts in minutes)
  • Huge free theme and overlay marketplace
  • Mobile streaming is genuinely good for IRL creators
  • Ultra bundle consolidates multistream + overlays + lower fees
  • Massive creator community and tutorial ecosystem

✗ What doesn't

  • Free tier takes a 12% cut of tips (Ultra removes it)
  • Heavier on resources than plain OBS
  • Some 'ultimate' features are gated behind the paid tier
  • Less customizable than raw OBS Studio for power users

Verdict

Streamlabs is the creator-friendly front-end for OBS — overlays, alerts, and monetization without a degree in broadcast engineering. Free gets you surprisingly far; Ultra ($19/mo) is worth it the moment you're multistreaming or taking tips. Power users who want total control will stay on vanilla OBS, but most streamers are happier here.
