The thinking tool that started a movement

Roam Research is the app that made bidirectional links and block-level references a household concept among knowledge workers. Every sentence in your notes is a discrete block you can link, reference, and embed anywhere else. The 2026 release added serious AI features — auto-linking, semantic search, and graph-wide Q&A — that turn a Zettelkasten into a queryable second brain.

Who it's for: Researchers, writers, founders, and lifelong learners who think in connections. Especially powerful if you're doing long-form research, writing a book, or building a personal knowledge base.

Key features

[[]] Bidirectional links

Type [[ to link any page. Roam builds the backlinks graph automatically — every connection surfaces serendipitously as you write.

Blocks Block-level references

Reference individual paragraphs, not just pages. Pull a quote from 2023 into today's daily note and it updates live — the killer feature for daily journaling workflows.

AI Roam AI (GPT-4o)

Ask a question across your entire graph. Roam AI pulls from every note, summarizes relevant blocks, and cites them inline. Like having a research assistant who's read everything you've ever saved.

Daily Daily notes workflow

One note per day, opened automatically. Capture fleeting thoughts, link to past notes, watch the graph grow over years — the foundation of every productive Roam workflow.

The honest take

✓ What works

  • Best bidirectional-linking UX in the business — feels instant
  • Block references change how you think about notes forever
  • Roam AI genuinely useful for graph-wide Q&A
  • Strong community, hundreds of power-user templates
  • Outliner-first approach is great for writers

✗ What doesn't

  • Outliner UI has a steep learning curve — takes 2-3 weeks to "click"
  • Free tier limited to 1,000 blocks — too small for real use
  • No native mobile apps that match desktop (mobile web only)
  • Pricing jumped from $15 to $165/yr — still cheap, but the optics hurt
  • Smaller plugin ecosystem than Obsidian or Logseq

Verdict

If you're serious about building a personal knowledge base and want the original bidirectional-linking experience, Roam still holds up. The Roam AI upgrade makes it more than a curiosity — it's now a daily research tool. For local-first privacy, pick Obsidian. For open-source and free, pick Logseq.
