Presentations that actually move

Prezi replaced the static slide deck with a single, zoomable canvas. Instead of flipping from slide 12 to 13, the camera pans, rotates, and dives into the part of your story that matters next. It is the closest thing to a TED-talk superpower for non-designers.

Who it's for: Speakers, teachers, founders, and sales teams who want decks that feel alive — not a wall of bullet points.

Key features

Zooming canvas Non-linear storytelling

Prezi’s signature: zoom, rotate, and pan across one canvas instead of advancing slides. Your audience follows a path, not a queue.

Prezi AI AI-generated decks

Type a topic and Prezi’s AI structures and animates a complete deck in seconds, then lets you restyle instantly.

Reusable templates Branded themes

Hundreds of professionally designed templates plus a brand kit so every deck matches your colors and fonts.

Present anywhere Live and recorded

Present live in the browser, record a voiceover walkthrough, or export straight to video.

The honest take

✓ What works

  • Truly unique visual style that stands out in a sea of slides
  • Generous free tier for casual users
  • Excellent for keynote-style and sales talks
  • AI builder cuts deck creation from hours to minutes

✗ What doesn't

  • The non-linear format can disorient some audiences
  • Real learning curve for the canvas metaphor
  • Less ideal for dense, text-heavy documents
  • Desktop app is occasionally buggy on macOS

Verdict

If your job involves standing in front of people, Prezi is worth a weekend of practice. The $15/mo Standard plan unlocks the features that actually matter. Pair it with Gamma or Beautiful.ai if you also need fast, templated decks.
