What Cal.com actually does

Cal.com is the open-source Calendly competitor. The cloud version is identical in UX to Calendly. The difference: you can self-host it on your own infrastructure, fork it, white-label it, or build custom integrations the closed-source alternative won't allow. Used by Vercel, Supabase, and dozens of unicorns for their internal scheduling.

Who it's for: Developers, privacy-first teams, agencies that want to white-label scheduling, and anyone allergic to vendor lock-in.

Key features

🔓 100% open source (AGPLv3)

Full code on GitHub. Self-host on Vercel, Railway, or your own server. No vendor lock-in, ever.

🎨 White-label bookings

Your domain, your colors, your logo. Embed booking flows directly into your product.

🔌 150+ integrations

Every calendar, every conferencing tool, every CRM. API + webhooks for everything else.

💰 Built-in payments

Take deposits via Stripe at booking time. No-shows drop 60% when payment is on the table.

The honest take

✓ What works

  • Truly open source — fork it, host it, modify it
  • Cloud pricing is comparable to Calendly but more generous
  • White-label and embeddable out of the box
  • Massive integration catalog
  • Active community (12k+ Discord members)

✗ What doesn't

  • Self-hosting requires DevOps chops (managed cloud is easier)
  • UI is slightly less polished than Calendly
  • Some advanced features (routing forms) require higher tier
  • Smaller brand recognition with non-technical buyers

Verdict

If you're a developer or technical founder, Cal.com is the clear winner — same scheduling UX as Calendly but open source and self-hostable. For non-technical users, the choice between Calendly and Cal.com cloud is mostly taste. Both are excellent.
