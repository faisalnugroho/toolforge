Key features

Cmd-K Keyboard-driven

Every action has a shortcut. Once you internalize the keyboard model, Linear is the fastest issue tracker you can use — faster than clicking through Jira by an order of magnitude.

Cycles Sprint planning

Built-in agile sprint cycles with auto-rollover of unfinished work. No setup — you get a real sprint workflow out of the box, not a config rabbit hole.

AI assist AI triage & summary

Linear's new AI features auto-summarize long threads, suggest assignees, and detect duplicate issues. Modest but useful — saves 10-15 min/day for a busy PM.

Projects Roadmap & goals

Roll issues up into projects, projects into initiatives, initiatives into goals. The roadmap view is gorgeous and finally makes quarterly planning feel like planning, not archaeology.

The honest take

✓ What works

  • Genuinely 10x faster than Jira for daily work — the keyboard-first design pays off
  • Opinionated workflow — you don't drown in configuration the way you do with Jira
  • Beautiful UI that engineers actually like opening (rare in this category)
  • Excellent GitHub/GitLab/Slack integrations that feel native, not bolted on
  • Free tier is generous (10 users) — startups can run on it forever

✗ What doesn't

  • Less customizable than Jira — if you have a weird process, you're stuck
  • Reporting & analytics are thin vs. dedicated PM analytics tools
  • No on-prem option — fully SaaS, no way to self-host
  • AI features are still behind competitors (no natural-language issue creation yet)

Verdict

If you're starting a new product org in 2026, use Linear. The default is right, the speed is unmatched, and the team will thank you. If you're already a year-deep into Jira with custom workflows, the migration is painful and probably not worth it.
