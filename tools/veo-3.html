The honest take

Google's third-gen video model -- best prompt fidelity, native audio, 4K output.

Who it is for: Brands and agencies who need on-brand cinematic video, technical demos with accurate UI, and motion graphics with precise text rendering.

Key features

4K 4K output

Up to 4K at 30fps. Sora 2 caps at 1080p on Plus -- Veo 3 wins on resolution.

Prompt Best prompt adherence

Follows long, detailed prompts more accurately than Sora 2 or Runway. Try: "a chef in a Tokyo kitchen, slow dolly, golden hour".

Audio Native audio

Generates dialogue, ambient, and sound effects alongside video. Comparable to Sora 2.

Text On-screen text

Best text-in-video of any model. Renders titles, labels, and signs accurately.

Style Style transfer

Drop in a reference image and Veo 3 maintains the visual style across the entire clip.

The honest take

✓ What works

  • 4K resolution -- only model that hits this
  • Prompt fidelity is the best in class -- what you ask is what you get
  • Best text rendering in video (titles, UI mockups, signs)
  • Available free with limits in Google AI Studio
  • Integrates with YouTube Shorts for one-click publish

✗ What does not

  • Smaller community than Sora 2 -- fewer prompt examples to copy
  • Vertex AI pricing is opaque ($0.50/clip of 8s)
  • Clips capped at 30s (Sora 2 does 60s)
  • Character consistency worse than Sora 2 across multi-shot scenes

Verdict

Veo 3 is the best choice when prompt fidelity and resolution matter more than clip length. If you are making 15-30s social ads with on-screen text (product names, prices, CTAs), Veo 3 beats everything. For longer narrative content, Sora 2 is still better.
