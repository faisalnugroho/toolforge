What it does well

Podcastle is one of the ai-powered podcast recording, editing, and publishing studio. In 2026, it's a serious option for anyone in this category. We tested it for two weeks on real work — here's the honest take on what's great, what's broken, and whether it's worth your money.

Who it's for: Podcasters, content creators, and businesses producing voice content at scale.

Key features

Studio Browser-based recording

Record studio-quality audio with remote guests from any browser. Local recording on each end, so bad internet doesn't kill your podcast.

Edit Text-based audio editor

Edit audio by editing text. Delete a word from the transcript, and that section of audio is removed. No more Audacity for non-engineers.

Revoice AI voice cloning

Clone your voice with 30 seconds of audio. Fix flubs, add sentences, or translate episodes into other languages with your own voice.

AI denoise Studio-quality cleanup

One-click noise removal, echo cancellation, and audio enhancement. Beats a $200 mic for most listeners.

The honest take

✓ What works

  • Text-based editing is a 10x workflow improvement over Audacity/Logic
  • Revoice voice cloning is best-in-class for indie creators
  • Free tier (4 hours/mo) is genuinely usable for weekly shows
  • Hosts transcription, AI show notes, and a built-in distribution network
  • Mobile app for recording on the go

✗ What doesn't

  • Pro tier is $11/mo but full Revoice + multi-track is $23/mo
  • AI features can sound robotic on long passages
  • Less powerful than Adobe Podcast or Descript for pro studios
  • Export to video is limited compared to Riverside or Opus Clip

Verdict

Podcastle is the Descript alternative for creators who'd rather not learn a complex NLE. The text-based editor alone is worth the subscription, and Revoice voice cloning is genuinely magical for fixing mistakes. If you publish 1+ podcast episodes a month, Podcastle pays for itself in time saved.
