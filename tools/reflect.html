The thinking person's notes app

Reflect is what Roam Research would have been if it shipped in 2024. Bidirectional links, daily notes, network graph, GPT-4 integrated as a first-class citizen — and end-to-end encrypted, so even Reflect can't read your notes. The feel is "writing in a tool that thinks."

Who it's for: Writers, researchers, founders, and anyone whose note-taking is actually thinking — not just collecting.

Key features

🔗Bidirectional links

Roam-style [[wikilinks]] done right. Backlinks panel, graph view, automatic discovery of related notes. The mental model that "your second brain is a network, not a folder" is finally polished.

🤖Native GPT-4

Highlight any text → "Ask AI." Summarize, translate, rewrite, generate counter-arguments. Not bolted on — feels like the editor understands you.

🔒End-to-end encrypted

Your notes are encrypted with a key only you have. Even a Reflect breach can't leak your journal. Rare for productivity SaaS.

⚡Speed

Native apps for Mac, iOS, and web. Loads in milliseconds. Sync is instant. The fastest note-taking app we've used.

The honest take

✓ What works

  • Best-in-class writing experience — minimal, fast, no clutter
  • AI integration is native, not an afterthought
  • End-to-end encryption — privacy isn't a marketing claim
  • Daily notes feature builds journaling habit gently
  • Mac/iOS apps feel native, not Electron

✗ What doesn't

  • No Android, no Windows native app (web only)
  • Limited collaboration features — this is a solo tool
  • No markdown export of the link structure (just text)
  • $10/mo for a notes app is steep if you don't use the AI features

Verdict

If you're a writer or thinker who has tried Obsidian, Roam, Notion, and walked away frustrated, give Reflect a serious trial. It's the most polished networked-notes app of 2026 — and the encryption story is genuinely rare.
