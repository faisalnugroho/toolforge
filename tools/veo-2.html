Google's answer to Sora

Veo 2 is Google DeepMind's second-generation video model, and in 2026 it's the closest credible competitor to OpenAI's Sora for cinematic, coherent text-to-video. It understands real-world physics, camera motion, and lens language — you can ask for 'a slow dolly shot at golden hour' and get something that looks filmed, not rendered. On-screen text is sharper than nearly any rival, which matters for ads and explainers.

Who it's for: Google Workspace and Gemini users, agencies producing short-form ads, and creators who need legible text and controlled camera moves inside generated clips.

Key features

🎥Cinematic control

Specify camera moves, lenses, and lighting. Veo 2 understands film grammar, not just 'make a video of X'.

🌍Physics realism

Models gravity, reflection, and material behavior so clips read as footage, not animation.

🔤Sharp on-screen text

Renders legible titles and labels — a real edge for explainer and ad content.

🔗Gemini integration

Prompt from Gemini and push to VideoFX; no separate model subscription required.

The honest take

✓ What works

  • Excellent physical realism and camera control
  • Best on-screen text of any video model
  • Native Gemini integration
  • Strong safety and watermarking (SynthID)

✗ What doesn't

  • Shorter clips than Sora
  • Access still gated behind Gemini Advanced / VideoFX waitlists
  • Less community and remix ecosystem than Runway
  • Noisy moderation on some innocent prompts

Verdict

The strongest Google video model yet and a real Sora rival for controlled, text-heavy, cinematic clips. If you live in the Google ecosystem it's the obvious choice; otherwise Runway's tooling and community still win for hands-on editing.
