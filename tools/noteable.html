Jupyter notebooks — but collaborative, deployable, and AI-native

Noteable is the data notebook platform built for teams. You get a real Jupyter-compatible environment in your browser (Python, SQL, R) with native Snowflake/BigQuery/Databricks connections, real-time multiplayer editing, and one-click sharing. The 2026 AI features add a copilot that explains queries in plain English, auto-fixes broken code, and turns a notebook into a parameterized data app. It's like Hex's cheaper, more Jupyter-pure cousin.

Who it's for: Data scientists, data analysts, data engineers, and academic teams who love Jupyter but need collaboration and easy sharing.

Key features

Collab Real-time multiplayer notebooks

Google Docs-style editing for Jupyter notebooks. See teammates' cursors, comment on cells, version history. The first real solution to 'how do I share my notebook'.

Polyglot Python + SQL + R in one notebook

Mix cells freely. Connect to Snowflake, BigQuery, Databricks, Postgres, and 20+ warehouses. Run Python on the result. Native dbt integration.

AI AI copilot

Highlight a query, ask 'explain this' or 'optimize this.' Generate Python from a comment, auto-fix errors, and turn notebooks into documented reports.

Deploy One-click share + schedule

Turn a notebook into a parameterized app stakeholders can run. Schedule it to run hourly/daily and email the report. No more 'run this every Monday' Slack pings.

The honest take

✓ What works

  • Real-time collab actually works — Google Docs for notebooks
  • Cheaper than Hex for solo analysts ($15 vs $20/mo)
  • Strong dbt and Snowflake integration — model-aware suggestions
  • Schedules + parameterized apps solve the 'manual Monday run' problem
  • Open-source, self-hostable for privacy-sensitive teams

✗ What doesn't

  • UI is less polished than Hex's 2026 redesign
  • AI copilot is useful but less magical than Hex's Magic AI
  • Smaller community and fewer templates than Hex or Observable
  • Mobile experience is essentially nonexistent

Verdict

Noteable is the best affordable collaborative data notebook in 2026. If you love Jupyter but need to share with your team without sending screenshots or .ipynb files, Noteable is the answer. Hex wins on UI and AI; Noteable wins on price, openness, and Jupyter-compatibility. For data teams of 3-10, it's a great choice.
