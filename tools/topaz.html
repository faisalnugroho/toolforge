The best-in-class AI image enhancer

Topaz Photo AI is the desktop app that pros reach for when Lightroom's denoise isn't enough. It bundles three AI models — Sharpen, Denoise, and Gigapixel (upscale) — and applies them automatically based on image analysis. The result: noisy ISO-12,800 wedding photos look like ISO-400. Soft 50mm f/1.4 shots get razor-sharp eyes. 12MP crops become 48MP print-ready files.

Who it's for: Wedding, event, sports, and wildlife photographers who shoot at high ISO and need clean results. Portrait retouchers who fix soft focus. Real estate shooters who need to crop and upscale. Less useful if you shoot at base ISO on a tripod or only post phone snapshots.

Key features

Auto Autopilot mode

Drop in a folder of 500 RAWs. Topaz analyzes each one, applies the right model (sharpen + denoise + upscale), exports. Batch processing at its best. Manual controls available for perfectionists.

Noise Best-in-class denoising

Recover detail from ISO 25,000+ shots that Lightroom would mush. Trained on millions of noisy/clean pairs. Detail preservation is the reason pros pay $199 instead of using the free Remini.

Upscale Gigapixel AI upscaler

Upscale 4x with realistic detail. Crop a 12MP frame from a 50MP file, upscale back to 12MP, and it looks like the original lens. Used by print shops to save client shots.

Plugin Photoshop & Lightroom plugin

Runs as a standalone app OR as a PS/LR plugin. Send a RAW from Lightroom to Topaz, process, send back. Or apply as a smart filter in Photoshop with layers.

The honest take

✓ What works

  • Industry-leading denoise and sharpening
  • One-time payment ($199), no subscription
  • Excellent RAW support (all major cameras)
  • Batch processing is fast on a decent GPU

✗ What doesn't

  • Requires a dedicated GPU (RTX 3060+ recommended)
  • $199 is steep for casual users — try free tools first
  • Processing 100 RAWs takes hours on weak hardware
  • Output files are huge (60MB+ for upscaled 4x)

Verdict

Topaz Photo AI is the gold standard for image enhancement in 2026. The one-time $199 price pays for itself if you shoot weddings, events, or wildlife even once. For casual phone photographers, Lightroom's built-in denoise is good enough — save your money. For everyone else, this is the tool. Pair it with Photoshop for a complete retouching workflow.
