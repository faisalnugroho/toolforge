What it does well

LiveKit is the open-source WebRTC platform that powers real-time voice agents, video conferencing, and live audio/video AI. Used by Character.AI, OpenAI Realtime, and Notion for low-latency streaming. Free self-hosted; managed cloud starts at $0.004/participant-minute. In 2026, it is a serious option for anyone in this category. We tested it for two weeks on real work — here is the honest take on what is great, what is broken, and whether it is worth your money.

Who it is for: Developers building voice AI agents, real-time transcription, video conferencing with AI features, or any low-latency audio/video application.

The honest take

✓ What works

  • Open-source under Apache 2.0 — no per-minute fees if you self-host
  • Native OpenAI Realtime API integration is the fastest path to a production voice agent
  • Sub-200ms latency is genuinely conversational — feels like a real human, not a phone tree
  • Used by OpenAI, Character.AI, and Notion — battle-tested at massive scale
  • Active developer community with extensive docs, examples, and Discord support

✗ What does not

  • Self-hosting WebRTC at scale requires real infrastructure expertise (STUN/TURN, NAT traversal, media servers)
  • Steep learning curve compared to managed voice AI platforms like Vapi or Retell
  • You are still on the hook for building the conversation logic, tool calling, and interruption handling
  • Cloud pricing is per-participant-minute — can get expensive for high-volume outbound calling
  • Documentation has improved but still assumes you know WebRTC fundamentals

Verdict

LiveKit is the right choice if you are a developer building a serious voice AI product in 2026 and you want control over every layer of the stack. The OpenAI Realtime integration alone is worth it — you can have a production voice agent in a weekend, not a quarter. If you are a non-developer or you want a no-code voice agent, use Vapi or Retell instead. But if you are comfortable with code and you need scale, customization, or self-hosting, LiveKit is the platform that OpenAI themselves use for their Realtime API demos. The open-source license means you can migrate to managed later without rewriting your application.
