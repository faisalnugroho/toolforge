See it on you before you buy it

TryOn AI is a virtual fitting room. Upload a full-body photo, paste a link to any clothing item (or upload a product image), and the AI renders that outfit on your body in seconds. It's built for online shoppers who want to see how clothes will look before ordering — and for fashion brands that want to offer virtual try-on on their product pages.

Who it's for: Online shoppers, fashion influencers, boutique owners, and e-commerce brands. Also useful for stylists and personal shoppers who want to show clients outfit options without ordering samples.

Key features

URL Any product link

Paste a link to any clothing product (Amazon, Zara, Shein, Shopify stores) and TryOn AI fetches the item and renders it on your photo. No manual image editing.

Full body Body-aware rendering

The AI maps clothing to your body shape, pose, and proportions. Dresses drape, pants fold, jackets sit on shoulders — it's not a flat overlay.

Batch Multiple outfits at once

Upload one photo and try on 10+ outfits in a single session. Compare looks side-by-side and save your favorites.

API For e-commerce

Brands can integrate the try-on API into their Shopify or custom store. Customers see "Try on me" buttons on product pages.

The honest take

✓ What works

  • URL-based product import — paste a link, get a try-on
  • Body-aware rendering is better than flat image overlays
  • Useful for reducing e-commerce returns (see it before you buy)
  • API available for Shopify and custom stores
  • Affordable at $15/mo for unlimited try-ons

✗ What doesn't

  • Rendering quality varies by fabric — silk and sheer materials look off
  • Requires a good full-body photo (not a selfie) for best results
  • Body shape accuracy is approximate — don't expect perfect fit prediction
  • API integration requires developer setup — not plug-and-play

Verdict

TryOn AI is the best virtual try-on tool for consumers in 2026. It won't replace ordering a size up and down, but it'll save you from buying things that clearly won't work. For e-commerce brands, the API is worth testing — virtual try-on reduces returns by 20–30% in our research.
