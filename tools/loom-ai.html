What Loom AI does best

Loom started as a simple screen recording tool. In 2024-2025, they shipped the AI features that made Loom actually competitive with Slack voice notes and Zoom calls: automatic titles, AI summaries, chapter markers, action item extraction, filler word removal, and AI-generated follow-up emails. For remote teams, Loom AI has become the async communication tool — faster than a meeting, more personal than Slack.

Who it's for: Remote teams, sales reps doing prospect outreach, engineering teams doing async standups, and anyone who sends 'let me just show you' messages. Especially powerful for distributed teams across time zones.

Key features

📹 HD screen + camera recording

Record your screen, webcam, or both. Up to 4K on Pro. One-click start, no setup.

🤖 AI summaries

Every Loom gets an auto-generated summary with key points and action items. Viewers who skip to the end still get the value.

📑 Auto chapters

AI breaks long recordings into chapters with timestamps. Great for 30-minute Looms.

✉️ Action item emails

AI extracts todos and offers to draft follow-up emails to attendees with the action items.

🗣️ Filler word removal

AI deletes 'um', 'uh', and long silences with one click. Makes you sound way more polished.

The honest take

✓ What works

  • Best-in-class screen recording UX (zero learning curve)
  • AI summaries actually save time — viewers can skim
  • Async video is 5-10x faster than meetings for status updates
  • Free tier is enough to test (25 videos, 5-min each)
  • Great integrations: Slack, Notion, Gmail, Salesforce, Jira

✗ What doesn't

  • Free tier 5-minute limit is brutal for any real use
  • Pro is $15/mo per user — adds up fast for teams
  • AI features only on paid plans
  • No real video editing (it's a recorder, not an editor)
  • Some users worry about screen recording privacy

Verdict

Loom AI is the async communication tool every remote team should've adopted in 2024. The AI summaries alone save hours per week for teams who send 5+ Looms daily. For sales teams and async-first companies, it's now table stakes. For everyone else, the free tier is enough to try it.
