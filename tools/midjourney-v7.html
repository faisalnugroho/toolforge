Still the bar for beautiful images

Midjourney v7 refined what v6 nailed: cleaner realism, better hands and text, and a personalization system that learns your taste so repeats are on-style. It remains the default for concept art, mood boards, and anything that needs to look designed rather than generated.

Who it's for: Artists, designers, and marketers who prize aesthetics above literal prompt accuracy. The go-to for hero images and brand visual worlds.

Key features

Personalize Taste tuning

Train a style profile from your rankings so output matches your eye.

Realism v7 engine

Sharper faces, cleaner text, fewer weird artifacts than prior versions.

Modes --oreflex etc

New flags for reflex-style consistency across a set.

Upscale Hi-res

2x and 4x upscalers for print-ready output.

The honest take

✓ What works

  • Best stylized output of any model
  • Personalization is a real differentiator
  • Huge, active community and prompts
  • Strong upscaling for print
  • Reliable, predictable results

✗ What doesn't

  • No free tier worth using
  • Web/Discord only, no native editor
  • Literal prompt control is weaker than SD
  • Can look samey without tuning

Verdict

Midjourney v7 is still the king of beautiful. If your job is to make things look good, it is the first tool to open. Pair it with an editor (or Playground) when you need to composite.
