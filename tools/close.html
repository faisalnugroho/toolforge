The CRM built for closing, not data entry. Built-in calling, SMS, and email that live where your deals are.

Close (formerly Close.io) was built on a contrarian thesis: sales software should help you close deals, not just track them. Launched in 2013, it became the CRM of choice for high-velocity outbound teams by baking calling, SMS, and email directly into the deal record. In 2026 it's the 'do less admin, make more calls' CRM — the anti-Salesforce for teams that live on the phone.

Who it's for: Outbound sales teams and SDRs who live on the phone and in email. Startups and SMBs running high-velocity outbound where speed-to-lead matters more than enterprise pipeline complexity. If your reps spend more time logging calls than making them, Close fixes that.

Key features

Dialer Built-in power dialer

No third-party dialer needed. Click-to-call, power dialing, and local-presence numbers built in. Calls, recordings, and notes attach to the lead automatically — zero manual logging.

SMS In-app texting

Send and receive SMS from the same inbox as calls and email. Sequenced outreach across voice + text + email without leaving the CRM.

Pipeline Visual pipeline

Drag-and-drop deal stages, customizable views, and a Kanban that actually reflects reality. Built for speed, not for impressing the RevOps team.

Automation Workflow + AI

Automated follow-ups, lead routing, and a recent AI assistant that drafts emails and summarizes threads. Keeps reps selling instead of clicking.

The honest take

✓ What works

  • Built-in calling/SMS means no per-seat dialer add-on (big savings vs Salesforce+ZoomInfo+Outreach)
  • Reps actually use it — the UI is fast and keyboard-driven
  • Calling data lives on the lead automatically (no 'did you log that call?')
  • Transparent, flat per-seat pricing (no per-contact or per-call nickel-and-diming)
  • Onboarding is quick — teams are live in days, not quarters

✗ What doesn't

  • Salesforce-grade customization and enterprise features aren't there (not built for 500-seat orgs)
  • Marketing automation is thin — pair with HubSpot or Marketo for heavy nurture
  • Reporting is good but not as deep as dedicated BI tools
  • No free tier — $99/seat/mo is a real commitment for tiny teams

Verdict

Close is the best CRM for outbound sales teams that value speed over enterprise bloat. The built-in dialer and SMS alone justify the $99/seat vs the Salesforce + separate dialer + separate sequencer stack most teams duct-tape together. If you're a 5-50 person outbound team, it's the obvious pick. If you're a 500-seat enterprise with complex CPQ, stay on Salesforce.
