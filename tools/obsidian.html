The notes app that finally respects your data

Obsidian is a local-first, plain-text markdown knowledge base. Your vault is just a folder of .md files on your disk — you own your data forever, can read it in 50 years with any text editor, and never lose access because a company shut down. The plugin ecosystem has 1,800+ community plugins, and the AI ones (Smart Connections, Copilot, Tars) are best-in-class for connecting your vault to GPT-4, Claude, or local LLMs via Ollama.

Who it's for: Researchers, writers, privacy-conscious professionals, and anyone who wants to own their notes for the next decade. Especially good if you do technical work and want to link code snippets, PDFs, and images in one graph.

Key features

.md Local plain-text files

Your vault is a folder on disk. Plain Markdown, future-proof, no lock-in. Sync via iCloud, Dropbox, Git, or Obsidian Sync ($8/mo).

[[]] Bidirectional links + graph view

Classic Roam-style backlinks, plus a beautiful force-directed graph visualization that shows how your ideas connect over time.

Plugins 1,800+ community plugins

From kanban boards to Zotero integration to calendars. The plugin store is the deepest in any note-taking app, period.

AI AI plugins (Smart Connections, Copilot)

Connect to GPT-4, Claude, Gemini, or local Ollama models. AI plugins index your vault semantically and answer questions across all your notes — without sending them to a third party if you self-host.

The honest take

✓ What works

  • Your data lives in plain .md files you fully own — forever
  • Plugin ecosystem is unmatched — 1,800+ and growing
  • Native desktop apps on Windows, Mac, Linux + mobile
  • AI plugins work with local models via Ollama for full privacy
  • Free for personal use, including commercial use of the local app

✗ What doesn't

  • Sync costs $8/mo (no free tier) — many users bolt on iCloud or Git instead
  • Real-time collaboration requires third-party plugins (no built-in like Notion)
  • Plugin quality varies wildly — the good ones are great, the bad ones break
  • Plugin API changes occasionally break your favorite plugin for a release cycle

Verdict

If you want to own your notes forever and care about AI flexibility, Obsidian is the obvious choice. The free desktop app + Ollama for local LLM inference is the closest you can get to a private ChatGPT over your entire second brain. For cloud-native real-time collab, pick Notion. For an outliner-only workflow, pick Roam.
