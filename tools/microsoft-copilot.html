What it does well

Microsoft Copilot is one of the microsoft's ai assistant baked into word, excel, outlook, teams, and windows. In 2026, it's a serious option for anyone in this category. We tested it for two weeks on real work — here's the honest take on what's great, what's broken, and whether it's worth your money.

Who it's for: Teams and operators in the Microsoft/Slack ecosystem who want AI woven into the apps they already use.

Key features

Word Doc drafting & editing

Tell Copilot 'write a project brief from these notes' and watch a 2-page doc materialize. It can also rewrite, summarize, and reformat existing documents.

Excel Formula-free analysis

Ask 'why did Q3 revenue drop?' in plain English. Copilot writes the formulas, builds the pivot table, and surfaces the answer with a chart.

Outlook Inbox triage on autopilot

Summarizes long email threads, drafts replies in your tone, and flags the messages that actually need you. Saves execs 30+ minutes/day.

Teams Meeting notes that write themselves

Joins your meetings, transcribes them, and produces a summary with action items and owners. Beats Otter on integration if you're already in the M365 ecosystem.

The honest take

✓ What works

  • Bundled with Microsoft 365 Copilot ($20/user/mo) — no new tool to learn
  • Massive context: sees your emails, files, calendar, meetings (with consent)
  • Strong Excel integration — natural-language formulas are genuinely magical
  • Enterprise-grade security and compliance (SOC 2, HIPAA, GDPR)
  • Free tier in Bing and Windows is genuinely useful

✗ What doesn't

  • Best features locked behind the $30/user/mo M365 Copilot license
  • Quality lags ChatGPT/Claude on raw reasoning — it's a 'good enough' model, not the best
  • Privacy concerns: opt-out for tenant data training is buried in admin settings
  • Inconsistent across apps — Copilot in Word is great, in PowerPoint it's still rough

Verdict

If your company is already on Microsoft 365, Copilot is the lowest-friction AI upgrade you can buy. It won't blow your mind with raw intelligence, but the fact that it sees your calendar, emails, and files (with permission) makes it incredibly practical. The $30/user/mo is steep for SMBs — start with the free Bing/Windows tier and the $20 Copilot Pro personal plan first.
