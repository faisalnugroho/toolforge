Open-weight video generation you can actually own

Moonvalley is a video model and companion app focused on giving creators and researchers control. Generate from text or an input image, run it from Discord, and — uniquely — fine-tune and self-host the weights, which appeals to teams that cannot ship to a black-box cloud API.

Who it's for: Researchers, indie filmmakers, and builders who want reproducible, self-hostable video generation rather than a locked SaaS.

Key features

Text→Video Text-to-video

Describe a scene and Moonvalley renders short clips with coherent motion and decent camera control.

Img→Video Image-to-video

Feed a still and animate it — pans, zooms, and object motion driven by a short prompt.

Discord Discord bot

Generate from Discord with slash commands, handy for communities and quick iteration.

Open Open & fine-tunable

Weights can be fine-tuned and run locally, so outputs are reproducible and privacy-friendly.

The honest take

✓ What works

  • One of the few fine-tunable / self-hostable video models
  • Discord workflow is fast for iteration
  • Free daily credits to experiment
  • Good for research and reproducible pipelines

✗ What doesn't

  • Output length and resolution still limited vs Runway/Sora
  • Self-hosting needs real GPU horsepower
  • UI is lighter than polished SaaS rivals
  • Motion coherence can drift on complex prompts

Verdict

Moonvalley is the pick when you need control and ownership over video generation — not just a pretty clip. If you want the slickest out-of-the-box results, Runway or Kling win; if you need to fine-tune and self-host, Moonvalley is the standout.
