From spreadsheet to shareable visual in minutes

Piktochart started as an infographic tool and has grown into a full visual-communication suite — infographics, presentations, reports, social posts, and short videos. Its 2025 AI assistant turns a prompt or a doc into a structured draft you can refine, which is why it remains a classroom and comms-team staple.

Who it's for: Marketers, educators, and internal-comms teams who need professional visuals without a designer.

Key features

AI visual generator

Describe a topic or paste a doc; Piktochart drafts a layout with charts and icons.

Infographics & reports

Templates for reports, one-pagers, and data stories that export to PNG/PDF/PPT.

Brand kit

Lock fonts, colors, and logos so every asset stays on-brand.

Video & social

Build animated explainers and resize for every channel from one editor.

The honest take

✓ What works

  • Fastest path from data to infographic for non-designers
  • Huge template library across use cases
  • Brand kit keeps teams consistent
  • Free tier is usable

✗ What doesn't

  • AI drafts still need a human edit pass
  • Less pixel-control than Figma for custom work
  • Video tool is basic vs dedicated editors
  • Pro needed to remove watermark

Verdict

If you publish reports or explainers regularly and don't have a designer on call, Piktochart is the pragmatic pick. It won't replace Figma for product UI, but it will replace the hours you'd waste fighting PowerPoint.
