The AI that lives where your work lives

Notion AI isn't trying to replace ChatGPT or Claude. It's the AI that lives inside the workspace you already use. You don't context-switch to a chat window — you highlight a paragraph, hit space, and ask for a summary, translation, or rewrite. You can generate entire pages from prompts, auto-fill databases, and extract action items from meeting notes. It's the 'glue AI' that makes your existing workflow faster.

Who it's for: Notion power users, knowledge workers, and teams that already have their docs, wikis, and projects in Notion. Also great for anyone who wants AI in-context instead of in a separate app.

Key features

✨ In-line AI

Highlight any text, hit space, and ask for a summary, translation, rewrite, or expansion. No copy-paste to ChatGPT, no context-switch.

📄 AI Pages

Generate entire Notion pages from a prompt. 'Create a project plan for launching a podcast' → full page with sections, tasks, and timelines.

🗄️ Database AI

Auto-fill properties in Notion databases. Have a list of 100 customer interviews? AI extracts sentiment, themes, and key quotes into database columns.

🔍 Q&A

Ask questions about everything in your Notion workspace. 'What did we decide about pricing in last week's meeting?' AI searches and synthesizes.

The honest take

✓ What works

  • In-context AI — no context-switching to a separate app
  • AI Pages and Database AI are genuinely useful, not gimmicks
  • $10/mo add-on is reasonable for what you get
  • Built into the workspace you already pay for
  • Privacy-focused — your data isn't used to train models

✗ What doesn't

  • Not a general-purpose AI — only useful inside Notion
  • If you don't use Notion, this isn't for you
  • Quality of long-form generation is below ChatGPT/Claude
  • Limited integrations with external tools (Notion-only)

Verdict

If you're already on Notion, the $10/mo AI add-on is a no-brainer. It pays for itself the first time you summarize a 30-page document with a single keystroke. If you don't use Notion, this won't change your mind — there are better general-purpose AI tools.
