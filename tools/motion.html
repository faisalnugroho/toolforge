The AI calendar that actually plans your day

Motion is what happens when AI meets time management. Connect your calendar, add a to-do list, and Motion automatically schedules every task into open time slots. Miss a deadline? Motion reshuffles. Add a meeting? Tasks rearrange. The AI handles the cognitive overhead of 'when do I work on this?' and lets you focus on 'how do I do this well?' Used by founders, consultants, and anyone who spends more time planning than doing.

Who it's for: Busy professionals, founders, consultants, and anyone with overflowing calendars and to-do lists. Especially valuable for people who waste 30+ minutes a day manually scheduling tasks.

Key features

🤖 Auto-Scheduling

Add tasks with deadlines and duration estimates. Motion finds open calendar slots and schedules them automatically. If something changes, the whole day reshuffles.

📅 Calendar Sync

Two-way sync with Google Calendar, Outlook, iCloud. Motion sees your meetings and schedules work around them. Works with team calendars too.

🎯 Priority Engine

AI ranks tasks by deadline, priority, and your work patterns. Surfaces 'what should I work on right now' instead of leaving you to decide.

👥 Team Projects

Coordinate tasks across team members, balancing workload and respecting everyone's calendars. Great for small teams that want shared visibility.

The honest take

✓ What works

  • Eliminates the 'what do I do next?' decision fatigue
  • Reshuffles intelligently when plans change (meetings, emergencies)
  • Visual calendar interface makes the day concrete
  • Used by founders and execs — credible productivity tool
  • Mobile app keeps everything in sync across devices

✗ What doesn't

  • No free tier — must commit to $34/mo to try
  • Learning curve to trust the AI with your schedule
  • Doesn't integrate with every calendar (limited Apple Calendar features)
  • Can feel over-controlling for people who like flexible schedules

Verdict

Worth it for anyone who loses 30+ minutes a day to manual scheduling. The $34/mo is steep for casual users, but for founders and consultants billing $200+/hr, it pays for itself in the first week. Try a 7-day refund window before committing.
