What it is

Phi-4 is the best small model of 2026.

Who it's for: Developers & researchers needing small but smart.

Key features

14B params Small but mighty

14 billion parameters that punch at 70B-model weight. Fits on a single consumer GPU with 4-bit quantization.

MIT Truly open

MIT-licensed weights on Hugging Face. No API gatekeeping, no rate limits, no data exfiltration. Self-host for free.

Math Top math scores

Scores 96.6% on the MATH benchmark — ahead of Llama 3.1 405B and within striking distance of GPT-4o.

128K Long context

128K-token context window handles entire codebases, papers, and transcripts without chunking.

The honest take

✓ What works

  • MIT-licensed — no usage restrictions, fine-tune commercially
  • Runs locally on a single 24GB GPU (or 16GB with 4-bit)
  • Best math/coding in its size class
  • 128K context window for long docs
  • 128K tokens/sec on RTX 4090 with vLLM

✗ What doesn't

  • Smaller ecosystem of fine-tunes than Llama or Mistral
  • No hosted API — you must self-host or pay a third-party
  • Multimodal vision requires a separate model
  • Microsoft releases it under the "research" tier — production support is community-driven

Verdict

Phi-4 is the best small model of 2026. If you need a smart, self-hostable AI that runs on a single GPU and you can fine-tune commercially, Phi-4 is the default. It won't replace GPT-5 for agentic work, but for cost-sensitive on-prem deployments it's the clear winner.
