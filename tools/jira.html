Where engineering work actually lives

Jira is the standard for software project management — sprints, epics, and issue tracking done right. In 2026 Atlassian Intelligence drafts acceptance criteria, summarises threads, and auto-triages backlog noise so teams spend less time in the tool.

Who it's for: Engineering and product teams, especially those already in the Atlassian ecosystem (Confluence, Bitbucket).

Key features

Atlassian Intelligence AI in your workflow

Draft ticket descriptions, summarise comment threads, and get backlog suggestions.

Boards Scrum & Kanban

Flexible boards for any agile process, with automation built in.

Roadmaps Planning at scale

Advanced roadmaps roll team plans up to portfolio view.

Integrations Dev-connected

Native links to Bitbucket, GitHub, and CI for traceability.

The honest take

✓ What works

  • Purpose-built for engineering workflows
  • Free tier for small teams
  • Strong automation and reporting
  • Deep Atlassian integration
  • AI reduces ticket busywork

✗ What doesn't

  • Overwhelming config for newcomers
  • UI can feel dense
  • Pricey as seats scale
  • Less intuitive for non-dev teams

Verdict

Jira is still the right call for software teams that live in agile. The AI features are real productivity wins, not gimmicks. If you are not an engineering team, Asana or Linear will feel lighter.
