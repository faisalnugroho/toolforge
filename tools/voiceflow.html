The canvas where AI agents get designed

Voiceflow is the visual conversation design platform that became the de facto standard for designing AI agents — voice and chat. It started in 2018 as the platform Amazon used to build Alexa Skills internally, and by 2026 it's used by 100,000+ teams including Amazon, BMW, USAA, JP Morgan, and thousands of smaller teams. The canvas lets product, design, and engineering collaborate on conversation flows visually, then deploys to any channel (voice, chat, web, mobile) with native LLM orchestration for AI responses. The "design once, deploy anywhere" approach has made Voiceflow the platform of choice for design-first AI agent teams.

Who it's for: Product designers, conversation designers, and product teams building AI agents who need to prototype, design, and collaborate visually before engineering handoff.

Key features

🎨Visual canvas

Figma-like canvas for designing conversation flows. Drag-and-drop blocks for intents, responses, branching, conditions, variables. Designers and PMs can build without code. Real-time multiplayer collaboration.

🤖LLM orchestration

Native integration with OpenAI, Anthropic, Google, Cohere, Mistral. Mix deterministic flows with LLM-powered responses. Knowledge base (RAG) support. Custom function calling.

🌐Deploy anywhere

One-click deploy to web chat, voice (Twilio, Telnyx), Alexa, Google Assistant, custom APIs, Slack, Microsoft Teams, WhatsApp. The widest channel coverage in the category.

🔧Developer handoff

Export conversation designs as documentation, share via URL, or import directly into Vapi/Retell/Bland via JSON. The de facto "design tool" for the entire AI agent ecosystem.

The honest take

✓ What works

  • Best-in-class visual canvas — designed for collaboration between PMs, designers, and engineers
  • Widest channel support — Alexa, web chat, voice, WhatsApp, Slack, Teams from one design
  • Strong LLM orchestration — mix flows with AI responses gracefully
  • Free tier is genuinely useful (2 prototypes, 1 knowledge base)
  • Mature SDK and API for production deployment

✗ What doesn't

  • Production voice call scaling less robust than Vapi/Retell — not the right choice if you need 10K+ concurrent calls
  • $40/mo Pro is more than Synthflow ($29) — steeper price for less voice-specific tooling
  • Self-hosted voice deployment requires more dev work than dedicated voice platforms
  • Telephony carrier integrations are less turnkey than Vapi/Retell

Verdict

For teams building AI agents where conversation design is a collaborative, multi-stakeholder process, Voiceflow is the best platform of 2026. For pure-code voice agent developers, Vapi or Retell AI are better fits. For non-developers needing a quick voice agent, Synthflow is more turnkey. Voiceflow's sweet spot is the design-led product team that needs to ship an AI agent that works on every channel.
