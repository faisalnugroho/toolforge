The foundation the open AI world is built on

Llama 4 isn't one model — it's a family. Scout gives you a staggering 10-million-token context for cheap retrieval. Maverick is the multimodal daily driver. Behemoth competes with closed frontiers. Because it's open-weight, every tool, quant, and fine-tune in the ecosystem supports it.

Who it's for: Builders who want the largest supported open ecosystem, massive context on a budget, and the freedom to fine-tune and self-host.

Key features

10M context Scout edition

Llama 4 Scout carries a 10-million-token context — the longest of any open model. Entire repos and document sets, natively.

Multimodal Text + vision

Maverick and Behemoth accept images. Document understanding, chart reading, and visual Q&A out of the box.

Ecosystem Universal support

Every inference server, agent framework, and quant tool supports Llama. The path of least resistance for open AI.

Fine-tunable Yours to adapt

License permits commercial fine-tuning. Spin up a domain-specific model on your own data with LoRA or full FT.

The honest take

✓ What works

  • 10M-token context (Scout) — unmatched for open models
  • Largest supported ecosystem of any model family
  • Multimodal across the family
  • Free to self-host and fine-tune commercially
  • Behemoth rivals closed frontier on benchmarks

✗ What doesn't

  • Behemoth needs serious hardware to self-host
  • Trails Opus 4.5 on subtle English creative writing
  • License has usage thresholds for very large apps
  • Quantized versions lose some benchmark quality

Verdict

Llama 4 is the safe, ubiquitous open-weight foundation in 2026. If you want maximum ecosystem support, a 10M-token context, and the freedom to self-host and fine-tune, start here. Reach for Qwen 3 Maxi if you need the absolute best open reasoning.
