Notion Calendar

What it does best

Notion Calendar (formerly Cron, acquired by Notion in 2023) is the calendar app Notion users have been waiting for. It's a Mac/iOS/Web calendar that syncs Google Calendar and Apple Calendar, has deep Notion database integration, and a UI that doesn't make you want to throw your laptop. Free, no upsells, no premium tier. It's what Google Calendar should be.

Who it's for: Notion power users, freelancers juggling multiple calendars, and anyone who wants Google Calendar to actually look good and integrate with their task system.

Key features

Notion sync Two-way database integration

Connect a Notion database (tasks, projects, CRM) and see it as a calendar. Time-block tasks, drag-and-drop, complete them from the calendar view. No more Notion-app-calendar-app switching.

Multi-calendar Overlay everything

Overlay personal, work, and shared calendars in one view. Color-code by source, filter by category, see where your time actually goes. The visualization Google Calendar should have shipped years ago.

Smart scheduling Time-zone aware

Schedule across time zones without mental math. Click a contact's time zone, drag a meeting block, Notion Calendar handles the conversion. Essential for remote teams.

Free forever No premium tier

Notion Calendar is free. No 'Pro' plan, no feature gates, no 'pay for advanced analytics.' Notion makes money on the workspace side; this is a loss-leader that drives Notion adoption.

The honest take

✓ What works

  • Completely free — no hidden tiers, no 'upgrade to remove watermark'
  • Notion database integration is genuinely game-changing for Notion users
  • Best calendar UI on Mac — keyboard shortcuts, mouse-only, mobile parity
  • Multi-calendar overlay is more usable than Google Calendar's own UI
  • Time-zone scheduling is the best of any calendar app

✗ What doesn't

  • No Windows or Android app (Mac/iOS/Web only) — dealbreaker for some teams
  • Doesn't sync with Outlook or iCloud natively (Google + Apple only)
  • Less powerful than Motion for AI-driven auto-scheduling
  • No natural-language input ('Lunch with Sarah tomorrow at 1' works, but limited)
  • Some features still feel Cron-era, not fully Notion-ified

Verdict

If you're on a Mac, use Notion, and use Google Calendar, this is the only calendar you should install. Free, well-designed, and the Notion sync alone is worth switching for. If you're on Windows or rely on Outlook, this isn't for you (yet).
