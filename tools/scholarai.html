What ScholarAI is built for

ScholarAI is the AI research assistant built for people who live in papers — grad students, scientists, analysts, and founders doing due diligence. Point it at a question and it returns answers pulled from 200M+ peer-reviewed papers and patents, each one cited.

Who it's for: Researchers, students, and knowledge workers who need verifiable answers from the literature fast.

Key features

Search Literature search

Ask a plain-English question and get answers cited from 200M+ peer-reviewed papers and patents across PubMed, arXiv, and more.

Summarize Paper summaries

Drop a PDF or DOI and get a structured summary with key findings, methods, and limitations in about 30 seconds.

Cite Auto-citations

Every claim links to its source. Export to BibTeX, RIS, or APA in one click — no more manual reference wrangling.

Ask Multi-paper Q&A

Compare methods across 10 papers at once: 'what dosage did Study X use versus Study Y?' and get a cited table.

The honest take

✓ What works

  • AI-native search across 200M+ papers and patents
  • Every answer is verifiable with a source link
  • Excellent for systematic reviews and lit scans
  • Chrome extension + API for labs and workflows
  • Free tier is genuinely usable

✗ What doesn't

  • Pro plan needed for heavy daily use
  • Not a replacement for reading full texts
  • Weaker on pre-2010 literature
  • Can occasionally mis-cite very obscure papers

Verdict

If your work touches academic literature, ScholarAI pays for itself in the first literature review. The citations are the killer feature — you stop trusting a chatbot's memory and start trusting a linked source. Pair it with Elicit for data extraction.
