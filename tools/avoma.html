The bottom line

Avoma positions itself as the all-in-one meeting platform for customer-facing teams — combining Gong-style conversation intelligence with Fathom-style meeting notes with a built-in scheduler. The 2026 differentiator is end-to-end workflow: book the meeting → record and transcribe → AI summarize → sync to CRM → forecast pipeline based on call content. Cheaper than Gong, more end-to-end than Fathom, more sales-aware than Fireflies.

Who it's for: Mid-market B2B sales and customer success teams (10-200 reps) that want one tool for meeting lifecycle.

Key features

📅 AI Scheduling

Smart scheduling with round-robin assignment, buffer times, and meeting routing rules. Connects to Salesforce, HubSpot, and Google/Outlook calendars.

🎙️ Meeting Recording & Notes

Auto-joins Zoom/Meet/Teams. AI generates structured notes with topics, action items, decisions, and follow-ups. Speaker ID and multi-language support.

💰 Revenue Intelligence

Tracks deal mentions across calls: budget, authority, need, timeline (BANT). Updates Salesforce opportunities with call summaries and BANT scores. The closest competitor to Gong's deal intelligence.

📊 Coaching & Scorecards

Pre-built and custom scorecards for sales calls. Tracks rep performance against frameworks (MEDDIC, SPIN, BANT). Manager dashboards show rep trends.

🔁 CRM Sync

Bi-directional Salesforce and HubSpot sync. Calls auto-logged with summary, transcript, and action items. Reduces rep CRM admin time by 30-40%.

The honest take

✓ What works

  • All-in-one approach is genuinely useful — eliminates the Fathom + HubSpot + separate scheduler stack
  • Pricing is 40-50% below Gong for similar conversation intelligence features
  • BANT/MEDDIC tracking is built-in (Gong requires custom setup)
  • Onboarding is faster than Gong — most teams are live in 1-2 weeks
  • Strong customer success use cases (not just sales) — meeting AI for CS teams is a 2026 strength

✗ What doesn't

  • Less brand recognition than Gong or Chorus — fewer peer references for enterprise buyers
  • Market intelligence layer is weaker than Gong's aggregated customer language insights
  • Limited integrations compared to Gong (no Apollo, no Outreach native sync)
  • Reporting dashboards are less customizable than Gong's
  • No standalone mobile app (Gong has full iOS/Android)

Verdict

Avoma is the right choice for mid-market B2B teams (10-200 reps) that want one tool to handle scheduling, meeting recording, conversation intelligence, and CRM sync. Cheaper than Gong with 80% of the functionality. The all-in-one approach is genuinely valuable for teams that don't want to stitch together Fathom + Gong + Calendly + Salesforce. Not as deep as Gong on pure conversation analytics, but better on operational workflow. If you're past 200 reps and need enterprise-grade market intelligence, Gong is still the answer.
