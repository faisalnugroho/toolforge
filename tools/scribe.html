What Scribe does best

The AI tool that turns any process into a step-by-step guide — automatically.

Who it's for: Operations teams, HR, IT, customer support, and any knowledge worker who writes the same SOP twice.

Key features

Auto-capture Automatic step recording

Open Scribe, hit record, do the task. Scribe watches your clicks and screenshots and turns it into a polished how-to with text, screenshots, and annotations. No manual screenshots.

AI write-ups GPT-generated descriptions

Each step gets a 1-2 sentence AI-generated explanation of what you did and why. Edits to match your voice are simple — click and rewrite.

Browser & desktop Works everywhere

Chrome extension records web flows. Desktop app captures native apps (Excel, SAP, Salesforce, your terminal). Mobile app for iOS workflows.

Sharing & SOPs Share as guide or embed

Export as PDF, Markdown, or share via link. Embed directly in Notion, Confluence, or your wiki. Build a full SOP library in an afternoon.

The honest take

✓ What works

  • The single fastest way to document any repeatable process
  • Free tier is genuinely useful — 10 Scribes/month is enough to start
  • Auto-capture is genuinely magical — no more 'let me screenshot that'
  • Scribe AI text is good enough to ship without editing most of the time
  • Integrations with Notion, Confluence, Zendesk, and most wikis are first-class

✗ What doesn't

  • Desktop app only works on Mac and Windows (no Linux)
  • Auto-capture misses some context — you'll still add intro/outro text manually
  • Pro tier ($12/user/mo) gets expensive fast for large teams (100+ seats)
  • AI-generated descriptions sometimes hallucinate steps that didn't happen

Verdict

If your team writes the same how-to guide more than twice — onboarding docs, SOPs, client onboarding steps, IT troubleshooting — Scribe pays for itself in week one. The free tier is generous enough to prove the value before you commit. For 10-person ops teams, it's a no-brainer.
