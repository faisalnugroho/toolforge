What it does well

Hailuo AI (by MiniMax) has quietly become one of the best video generation models for human subjects. While Sora and Runway get the headlines, Hailuo's face and hand rendering is arguably the best in the business — and it's a fraction of the price. We tested it across 50+ prompts over two weeks.

Who it's for: Content creators and marketers who need realistic human video on a budget. Anyone testing AI video before committing to Sora or Runway pricing.

Key features

Faces Best-in-class face rendering

Hailuo's model excels at realistic facial expressions, lip sync, and micro-expressions. Other models produce uncanny valley faces — Hailuo produces faces that pass the sniff test in most contexts.

Speed Fast generation

Most clips render in under 60 seconds. Compare that to Sora's 3-10 minute render times. For rapid iteration and prototyping, speed matters more than 4K resolution.

Free Generous free tier

100 credits per day on the free tier, with each clip costing 5-15 credits depending on length and resolution. That's 6-20 free clips daily — more than enough for experimentation.

i2v Image-to-video

Upload a still image and animate it. Works well for product shots, character art, and photographs. The motion is natural and doesn't feel like a cheap parallax effect.

The honest take

✓ What works

  • Best face and hand quality in the under-$20 price range
  • Generous free tier — 100 credits/day is enough for real use
  • Fast renders (under 60s for most clips)
  • Chinese model with strong global support and English prompts
  • API access for developers

✗ What doesn't

  • 6-second max clip length on standard tier
  • No native audio generation (silent clips only)
  • Limited camera control compared to Runway
  • Occasional artifacts in complex multi-person scenes

Verdict

Hailuo AI is the best-kept secret in AI video generation. If your content features humans — talking heads, product demos, social clips — it produces more realistic results than tools costing 3x more. The free tier is genuinely useful, not just a teaser. The main limitations are clip length and lack of audio, but for most social media and marketing use cases, it's more than enough.
