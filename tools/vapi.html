The fastest way to ship AI phone agents

Vapi is the platform we keep coming back to for AI voice agents in 2026. You write a system prompt, pick a voice (or fine-tune your own), wire up tools/functions, and you have a production-grade voice bot taking real calls within an hour. No Twilio wrangling, no ASR glue, no audio debugging.

Who it's for: Developers building inbound/outbound call automation, SDRs, customer support triage, restaurants, healthcare intake, scheduling — anyone whose bottleneck is "how do I get this thing on the phone."

Key features

⚡Sub-500ms latency

Vapi's stack (custom ASR + LLM streaming + TTS) keeps turn-taking latency under half a second. That sounds small. It's the difference between "I'm talking to a robot" and "I'm talking to a person."

🔌Tool/function calling

Wire your agent to any HTTP endpoint — calendar booking, CRM lookup, payment links, anything. Voice agents that actually do things, not just chat.

🗣️Voice library

ElevenLabs, Cartesia, OpenAI, LMNT, PlayHT — pick from 50+ voices or clone one. Per-language tuning, emotion control, interruption handling.

📞Telephony built in

Bring your own Twilio number or provision one in the dashboard. SIP trunk support. Voicemail detection. Call recording + transcription automatic.

The honest take

✓ What works

  • The fastest path from "I have an idea" to "real people are calling my agent"
  • Latency is the best in class — feels like a real conversation
  • Squad of voice models — swap without rewriting
  • Excellent docs and a helpful Discord community
  • Dashboard with call logs, transcripts, and cost analytics

✗ What doesn't

  • Usage-based pricing adds up fast — 1000 calls/mo is $300-$600 depending on length
  • Complex multi-language workflows require careful prompt engineering
  • No native sentiment/escalation logic — you build that yourself
  • Voice quality varies by provider; testing matters

Verdict

If you're a developer who wants to put AI on the phone in 2026, Vapi is the default. Retell and Bland are real competitors, but Vapi's docs, latency, and flexibility keep it ahead. The pay-as-you-go model is great for testing, but lock in volume pricing before you scale past a few hundred calls/day.
