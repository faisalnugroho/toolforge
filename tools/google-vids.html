Turn any Google Doc into a video in minutes

Google Vids is Google's answer to Canva and Loom — an AI video editor that lives inside Google Workspace. You feed it a Doc, Slide deck, or even a prompt, and it generates a narrated video with stock footage, transitions, and AI voiceover. The magic is that it's collaborative like Google Docs — your team can comment, edit clips, and share in real time.

Who it's for: Teams already on Google Workspace who need internal training videos, onboarding walkthroughs, or quick explainer videos without hiring a video editor.

Key features

Doc-to-Video Turn docs into video

Paste a Google Doc URL or upload text and Vids generates a storyboard, picks stock footage, adds voiceover, and assembles a full video. Editable scene-by-scene.

AI Voiceover Natural AI narration

Choose from Google's text-to-speech voices (powered by Gemini) to narrate your video. Sounds natural and supports multiple languages.

Collab Real-time editing

Just like Google Docs — multiple people can edit the same video simultaneously, leave comments, and see each other's cursors in real time.

Stock Built-in media library

Access to millions of stock video clips, images, and music tracks. The AI auto-selects relevant footage based on your script content.

The honest take

✓ What works

  • Free if you already pay for Google Workspace — no extra subscription
  • Best-in-class collaboration — true real-time multi-user video editing
  • Seamless integration with Docs, Slides, Drive, and Meet
  • AI storyboard generation saves hours of planning
  • Exports to MP4, embeds, or Google Meet

✗ What doesn't

  • Not a replacement for professional video editing — limited transitions and effects
  • AI voiceover is decent but not ElevenLabs-level
  • Stock footage selection can feel generic for niche topics
  • Requires a Google Workspace Business plan (not available on free Gmail)

Verdict

If your team lives in Google Workspace, Vids is a no-brainer. It's free, collaborative, and good enough for internal training videos and quick explainers. For client-facing marketing content, you'll still want Canva or a pro editor.
