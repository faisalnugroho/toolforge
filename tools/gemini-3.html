Google's strongest response to GPT-5 and Claude 4

Gemini 3 (released May 2026) is Google's clearest shot at the frontier. It doubles context to 4M tokens, ships native agentic tool use (no LangChain glue), and gets noticeably better at long-horizon coding tasks. The biggest jump is in agentic reliability — Gemini 3 can run a 50-step browser-use or coding-agent workflow and finish the task where 2.5 Pro gave up halfway. It also has the best real-time video understanding of any model: stream your webcam in and ask "what changed in the last second?" — it works.

Who it's for: Agent builders, video-understanding products, anyone who needs 1M+ context reliably, and Google Workspace shops that want the deepest integration with Gmail/Docs/Sheets.

Key features

4M Context window

4 million tokens — roughly 6,000 pages. Drop your entire company wiki, all your support tickets, and a year of design docs in one prompt. Actually retains detail across the whole window.

Agent Native tool use

First-class agent SDK with built-in browser, code-execution, search, and file-system tools. No more hand-rolled LangGraph loops. Production-ready agentic workflows in ~20 lines of code.

Video Real-time streaming

Stream webcam/video in and ask questions about frames. Latency is ~400ms. The killer demo: hold your phone camera at a math problem and have Gemini solve it while you film.

Work Workspace integration

Deepest Gmail, Docs, Sheets, and Calendar integration of any LLM. Summarize a 500-message thread, generate a slide deck from a spreadsheet, draft replies that know your schedule.

The honest take

✓ What works

  • Best multimodal video understanding of any model in 2026
  • Native agent SDK makes 50-step workflows actually reliable
  • 4M context window — drop entire codebases, knowledge bases
  • Tightest Google Workspace integration
  • Competitive pricing — $20/mo AI Plus is generous

✗ What doesn't

  • Creative writing still trails Claude 4 Opus and GPT-5
  • Free tier has tighter rate limits than ChatGPT and Claude
  • Vertex AI setup is heavier than OpenAI or Anthropic APIs
  • Some agent tasks still hallucinate tool arguments

Verdict

Gemini 3 is the most underrated frontier model of 2026 — and the best choice if you're building agents or video-understanding products. It's not the best at pure prose, but for any multimodal or agentic workflow it's genuinely better than GPT-5 or Claude 4. The Workspace integration alone makes it the default for Google-heavy teams.
