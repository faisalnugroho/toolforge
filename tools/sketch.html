The craft tool that went collaborative

Sketch defined vector UI design on the Mac. After years of playing catch-up, it returned with a cloud workspace, real-time collaboration, and Sketch Assistants plus new AI features for generating and editing layers. It remains the favourite of designers who want a fast, native Mac app.

Who it's for: Mac-based product designers and small studios who value speed, polish, and a native app over browser-only workflows.

Key features

AI Generate & edit with prompts

Sketch AI creates vector shapes, fills, and layouts from text, and cleans up handoff without breaking your components.

Workspace Cloud collaboration

Shared libraries, real-time editing, and web viewing bring Sketch into team workflows.

Symbols Reusable components

Mature symbol and style system that keeps large design systems consistent.

Inspect Dev handoff

Developers get clean specs, code snippets, and exports without a separate tool.

The honest take

✓ What works

  • Fast, native Mac performance
  • Clean, focused UI design experience
  • Strong component and symbol system
  • Affordable per-editor pricing
  • Good cloud collaboration now

✗ What doesn't

  • Mac-only — no Windows app
  • Smaller community than Figma
  • AI features trail Figma and Canva
  • No free tier for regular users

Verdict

Sketch is the comeback kid of 2026: a genuinely fast, AI-assisted Mac design tool with real collaboration. It will not dethrone Figma for large cross-platform teams, but for Mac designers who want craft and speed, it is the best it has ever been.
