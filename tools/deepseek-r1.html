What it is

DeepSeek R1 is the Chinese AI lab's open-weight reasoning model, released in January 2025 and iterated through 2026. It was the first open-weight model to match OpenAI's o1 on most math and coding benchmarks — and it did so with weights you can download and a free chat app anyone can use.

The 2026 R1 update added multimodal support, a 128K context window, and an even stronger coding variant (R1-Coder). It's the default "I need o1-level reasoning for free" answer. The catch: it's trained primarily in English and Chinese, and its writing voice is more utilitarian than Claude 4 Opus.

Who it's for: Developers, students, researchers, and anyone who needs o1-level reasoning without the $200/mo subscription.

Key features

o1-class reasoning Best open-weight reasoning model

On AIME, MATH, and Codeforces, R1 is within 2-3% of o1. The reasoning trace is exposed in the chat output — you can see it "think."

128K context Long-context reasoning

Smaller than the frontier closed models, but enough for most use cases. You can drop in a full book and ask specific questions.

Free chat app No subscription, no usage caps

The DeepSeek chat app is free for all users, with no daily limit on the standard tier. The API is also pay-per-token, with no monthly minimum.

Truly open MIT-licensed weights

The base and distilled variants are MIT-licensed. You can fine-tune, redistribute, and even sell derivatives without restrictions.

The honest take

✓ What works

  • Open-weight reasoning at o1 level — no other open model is close
  • Free chat app with no daily usage limits
  • Cheap API ($0.55 per million input tokens — 95% cheaper than o1)
  • Exposed reasoning trace is great for learning and debugging
  • MIT license — most permissive possible

✗ What doesn't

  • Writing voice is utilitarian — not as good as Claude 4 Opus for prose
  • Hosted in China — data privacy concerns for some enterprise use cases
  • Smaller community / ecosystem than Llama 4
  • No multimodal input (text-only as of 2026)

Verdict

If you need o1-level reasoning and don't want to pay $200/mo for ChatGPT Pro, DeepSeek R1 is the answer. It's the best open-weight model for math, coding, and structured reasoning. For everything else, GPT-5 and Claude 4 Opus are still a step ahead.
