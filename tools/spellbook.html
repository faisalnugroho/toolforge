The contract AI that lawyers actually use

Spellbook is the AI legal assistant built specifically for transactional lawyers who live in Microsoft Word. Founded by lawyers in Halifax, Canada in 2022 and trained on millions of contract clauses, Spellbook's claim to fame is that it speaks lawyer. It drafts contract language in plain English instructions ("add a mutual indemnity clause"), suggests missing clauses ("you forgot the data breach notification section"), flags risks, and proposes redlines — all inside the Word document you're already working in, via a Word add-in. By 2026 it's used by 4,000+ law firms including Am Law 200 firms and corporate legal departments.

Who it's for: Transactional lawyers (M&A, commercial, IP, employment), in-house counsel, and paralegals who spend 5+ hours/week drafting or reviewing contracts.

Key features

📝Word add-in

Installs as a Microsoft Word add-in. No new tab to open — suggestions appear in the right sidebar as you type. Zero workflow disruption. This is why lawyers actually use it.

⚖️Legal-trained GPT-4

Fine-tuned on legal corpora and contract data — not a generic chatbot. Knows what a "no-shop clause" is, when to flag "indemnification" as missing, and how to draft "limitation of liability" sections.

🔍Clause review

Catches missing clauses, ambiguous language, and risky terms in seconds. Scores the contract 1-10 on completeness. Lawyers report catching issues in 10-15 minutes that would have taken an hour manually.

🤖Drafting assistant

Type "draft a mutual NDA with a 3-year term and Delaware governing law" — Spellbook writes a usable first draft in 30 seconds. Not perfect, but a real 70% starting point that you edit.

The honest take

✓ What works

  • Lives in Word — zero workflow change for lawyers
  • Legal-trained model knows transactional vocabulary — far better than ChatGPT for contracts
  • Catches real issues — lawyers report 30-50% time savings on contract review
  • Privacy-focused — your contracts aren't used to train the model
  • Affordable for solo/small firm — $100/mo is much cheaper than Harvey AI's $1K+/mo

✗ What doesn't

  • Word only — no Google Docs or PDF-only workflows (Casetext wins here)
  • Not for litigation research — Harvey AI and Westlaw AI are better for case law
  • $100/mo adds up for a 50-attorney firm ($60K/year)
  • Still requires lawyer review — outputs are first drafts, not final contracts

Verdict

For transactional lawyers drafting or reviewing contracts weekly, Spellbook is the most cost-effective AI legal tool of 2026. For BigLaw corporate departments with bigger budgets, Harvey AI offers deeper capability. For litigation research, look at Westlaw AI or Lexis+ AI. Spellbook's sweet spot is the solo/small/mid-firm transactional lawyer who wants AI without the $1K/mo enterprise price.
