Topaz Labs is the OG of AI photo enhancement. Its flagship Photo AI and Video AI apps upscale, denoise, sharpen, and restore with models trained specifically for each job. Wedding photographers rescue noisy reception shots; editors upscale 1080p footage to 4K; archivists restore old film. It runs locally on your GPU.

Who it's for: Photographers, videographers, and restoration specialists who need maximum quality from imperfect source material.

Key features

Upscale Gigapixel upscaling

Upscale images up to 8x with face recovery and detail synthesis that beats naive resizing.

Denoise AI Denoise & Sharpen

Strip noise and recover focus from low-light and high-ISO shots without losing detail.

Upscale, deinterlace, and stabilize video; convert 30fps to 60fps with motion interpolation.

The honest take

✓ What works

  • Best-in-class upscaling and denoising quality
  • Runs locally — private, no subscription per use
  • Purpose-built models per task (faces, noise, motion)
  • One-time license (no forced subscription)
  • Batch processing for large libraries

✗ What doesn't

  • Expensive upfront cost ($199+ per app)
  • GPU-hungry — slow without a decent graphics card
  • UI is functional but not elegant
  • Overkill for quick casual touch-ups

Verdict

For serious photo and video restoration, Topaz is still the quality benchmark. If you regularly rescue noisy or low-res media, the one-time license pays for itself fast. Casual users can skip it for cheaper cloud upscalers.
