What Boomy does best

the AI music tool that crossed the chasm from 'cool demo' to 'people actually releasing this.' Pick a style (lo-fi, EDM, hip-hop, ambient), hit generate, and 30 seconds later you have a full song with structure, instruments, and vocals. The killer feature: Boomy releases to Spotify, Apple Music, and 40+ stores for you, so your AI tracks can actually earn streaming royalties.

Who it's for: Content creators who need background music for videos, podcasters who want intros, game developers who need SFX-adjacent loops, and musicians looking for starter ideas. Less ideal if you're trying to make 'real' music that competes with human artists.

Key features

🎵 Style presets

Lo-fi, EDM, hip-hop, ambient, synthwave, jazz, and 40+ more. Each style is a finetuned model trained on real genres.

⚡ 30-second generation

A full 2-3 minute song in under a minute. Faster than any other AI music tool.

🎙️ AI vocals

Add AI-generated lyrics and have them sung in your choice of voice. Surprisingly good.

🎚️ Stem export

Pro plan exports individual stems (drums, bass, vocals, melody) so you can remix in Ableton or Logic.

💰 Distribution built-in

Release to Spotify, Apple Music, TikTok, YouTube Music, and 40+ stores. Earn streaming royalties.

The honest take

✓ What works

  • Fastest AI music generator we've tested (sub-minute songs)
  • Distribution to Spotify/Apple is built-in — no DistroKid needed
  • Free tier actually lets you make songs (just not release them)
  • Stem export means you can polish in a DAW
  • Earn royalties on AI tracks (the legal landscape is evolving)

✗ What doesn't

  • Output quality is below Suno v4 and Udio for serious music
  • AI vocals can sound uncanny on complex melodies
  • Royalty share — Boomy takes 50% of streaming revenue (you keep 50%)
  • Limited control compared to Suno (no custom lyrics editing)
  • Style presets can feel repetitive after a few generations

Verdict

Boomy is the best AI music tool for creators who need lots of music, fast, and want to monetize it. If you're a YouTuber needing 50 background tracks this quarter, Boomy beats hiring composers. For 'art' music where quality is everything, Suno or Udio still lead.
