The scheduling tool that doesn't feel like Calendly.

SavvyCal is one of the most-used productivity tools in 2026. Consultants, freelancers, salespeople, and founders who schedule external meetings regularly and want a more polished experience than Calendly. Especially good for people who care about the recipient's booking experience.

Who it's for: Consultants, freelancers, salespeople, and founders who schedule external meetings regularly and want a more polished experience than Calendly. Especially good for people who care about the recipient's booking experience.

Key features

Overlay Scheduling overlay

Show your availability on top of someone else's calendar. Pick a slot that works for both of you without back-and-forth emails. Unique to SavvyCal.

Ranked Ranked availability

Set preferred meeting times and SavvyCal will rank open slots by your preference. Small thing, but it removes a daily micro-decision.

Polls Group scheduling

Send a poll to find a time that works for everyone. Much better than the email tennis of 'how about Tuesday' / 'no' / 'Wednesday?' / 'no'.

Custom URLs Branded booking links

Use your own domain for booking links (yoursite.com/meet). Custom branding, colors, and intake questions per meeting type.

The honest take

✓ What works

  • Scheduling overlay is genuinely novel — saves real back-and-forth time
  • The recipient's booking experience is the best in the category
  • Lifetime deal available ($149 one-time) — rare in SaaS
  • Built by a solo founder (Derrick Reimer of Drip fame) — fast iteration
  • Polished, opinionated UI without Calendly's bloat

✗ What doesn't

  • Fewer integrations than Calendly (no native Zoom/Slack enterprise features)
  • Smaller community — fewer tutorials and templates
  • Group scheduling (polls) is newer and less tested than Doodle's
  • No mobile app — web only

Verdict

If you schedule external meetings and care about the recipient's experience, SavvyCal is a meaningful upgrade over Calendly. The scheduling overlay alone pays for the subscription for anyone with regular 1:1s. The lifetime deal at $149 is a steal for solopreneurs.
