What it is and why people use it

Anyscale is one of the most popular ai infrastructure tools in 2026. ML platform teams and AI engineers running distributed training, fine-tuning, or batch inference at scale.

Who it's for: ML platform teams and AI engineers running distributed training, fine-tuning, or batch inference at scale.

Key features

Ray Managed Ray platform

Ray is the de-facto framework for distributed ML. Anyscale wraps it with a managed platform — autoscaling, observability, cost controls.

Fine-tune Custom models at scale

Fine-tune Llama, Mistral, and Qwen on your own data. Distributed training across hundreds of GPUs without the ops burden.

Multi-cloud AWS, GCP, Azure

Bring your own cloud account. Anyscale orchestrates; you keep the data residency and the cost controls.

Ray Serve Production inference

Deploy models as autoscaling endpoints. Handles traffic spikes, version rollouts, and GPU sharing automatically.

The honest take

✓ What works

  • Built by the team that built Ray — the platform knows the framework
  • Real cost savings vs. SageMaker or Vertex AI for distributed workloads
  • Cloud-agnostic — avoid vendor lock-in
  • Strong observability and debugging tools for distributed jobs

✗ What doesn't

  • Steep learning curve if you don't already know Ray
  • Not a fit for simple, single-GPU workloads
  • Pricing is opaque for enterprise tiers

Verdict

If you're running serious distributed ML — multi-node training, batch inference at scale, complex pipelines — Anyscale is the best-managed platform available. For a single LoRA fine-tune on a single GPU, it's overkill. Use Modal or Replicate instead.
