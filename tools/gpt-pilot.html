From idea to codebase, with an AI pair dev

GPT Pilot (by Pythagora) acts like a tech lead — it asks clarifying questions, plans the architecture, and writes code, handling debugging along the way. It's a favorite for scaffolding brand-new apps from scratch.

Who it's for: Founders and developers who want to bootstrap a new app quickly without hand-writing boilerplate.

Key features

🗺️ Spec to plan

Turns a product description into an architecture and task list.

💬 Clarifying questions

Asks the right questions before writing code.

🐞 Self-debugging

Runs the app and fixes errors iteratively.

🔓 Open source

Self-hostable; bring your own model key.

The honest take

✓ What works

  • Free and open source
  • Great for greenfield apps
  • Asks clarifying questions first
  • Works with multiple LLMs

✗ What doesn't

  • Still rough on large existing codebases
  • Needs human review
  • Debugging loops can stall
  • Best with GPT-4-class models

Verdict

The fastest free path from a napkin idea to a running app. For production maintenance on existing repos, Cursor or Cline are more practical.
