The original no-code automation platform — now with native AI.

Zapier has been the connective tissue of the SaaS world since 2011. In 2026, it's no longer just 'connect app A to app B' — it's a full AI orchestration layer with natural-language Zaps, AI Actions, and a Copilot that builds workflows for you. With 7,000+ app integrations and a brand-new AI builder, Zapier is the default choice when you want your tools to talk to each other without writing code.

Who it's for: Operations teams, founders, marketers, and anyone drowning in repetitive cross-app tasks. Especially powerful if you use 5+ SaaS tools daily.

Key features

7K+ apps Massive integration library

Connect Gmail, Slack, Notion, Stripe, HubSpot, Salesforce, Airtable, Google Sheets — basically anything with an API. The largest catalog of any automation platform.

AI Copilot Build Zaps from a sentence

Type 'when a new lead comes into HubSpot, send a Slack message and add them to a Google Sheet' and Zapier builds the whole workflow. Refines with follow-up prompts.

Tables + Interfaces Lightweight database + portal

Zapier Tables is a built-in Airtable alternative. Interfaces lets you build a customer portal or internal tool without code.

AI Actions Embed GPT, Claude, Gemini in workflows

Run LLM steps inside any Zap — summarize emails, classify tickets, generate draft responses. Pay-per-task, no API key required.

Zapier Agents Autonomous AI workers

New in 2026: Agents that monitor triggers, make decisions, and execute multi-step workflows without a human in the loop. Still early but powerful.

The honest take

✓ What works

  • 7,000+ app integrations — far more than Make (1,500), n8n (400), or Workato
  • AI Copilot actually builds working multi-step Zaps from natural language
  • Free tier is genuinely useful (100 tasks/mo, single-step Zaps)
  • Tables + Interfaces turn Zapier into a lightweight internal-tools platform
  • Best-in-class reliability — 99.9% uptime SLA on paid plans
  • Massive template library (1,000+ pre-built AI workflows)

✗ What doesn't

  • Gets expensive fast — multi-step Zaps with premium apps can hit $500+/mo
  • Slower than Make for complex branching logic (Make's visual editor wins)
  • AI Copilot still hallucinates app names occasionally — you have to verify
  • No self-hosted option (n8n has this for free)
  • Pricing is per-task, not per-workflow — heavy usage racks up bills

Verdict

If you've automated anything online in the last decade, you've used Zapier. In 2026, it's still the easiest on-ramp to no-code automation, and the AI Copilot makes it dramatically faster to build. The pricing will hurt at scale, but for most teams doing under 50,000 tasks/mo, Zapier is still the default — and now it's actually smart about it.
