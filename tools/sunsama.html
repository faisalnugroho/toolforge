The opposite of Todoist: a planner that helps you finish less, better

Sunsama is built on a contrarian idea: instead of a giant task list, you should plan your day with a fixed number of tasks, time-block each one, and review nightly. The product enforces that constraint. Every morning, you spend 5-10 minutes pulling tasks from Notion, Asana, Linear, Gmail, Slack — Sunsama unifies them and you pick what you'll work on. New AI features (2026) suggest the right tasks based on priority, energy level, and calendar context.

Who it's for: Knowledge workers drowning in tasks across 5 tools. Especially powerful for founders, PMs, and consultants whose day is reactive by default. Not for power-users of OmniFocus or who love a 200-task kanban.

Key features

Daily Plan Time-boxed intention

Each morning, plan 4-8 tasks for the day. Drag from a unified inbox (Notion, Asana, Linear, Gmail, GitHub, Slack). Estimate time. Sunsama blocks calendar slots for each.

Coach AI daily suggestions

New in 2026: the AI coach suggests which tasks to prioritize based on deadlines, energy level (you tell it), and meeting load. Doesn't decide for you, but it nudges.

Shutdown Ritual End-of-day review

The signature feature. 5-minute end-of-day flow: what got done, what rolled over, what to plan for tomorrow. Closes the loop. Users report 30% less Sunday anxiety.

Integrations Pull from everywhere

Native: Notion, Asana, Linear, Jira, ClickUp, Trello, Todoist, Gmail, Outlook, GitHub, Slack. Pulls tasks, two-way sync for many. No more "where did I write that task?"

The honest take

✓ What works

  • Forces a healthier relationship with tasks — less list anxiety, more daily finish
  • Integrations cover 90% of the modern knowledge worker's stack
  • Shutdown ritual is genuinely the most valuable end-of-day feature on the market
  • Time-blocking integrates with Google/Outlook calendars — no double-booking
  • AI coach suggestions are tasteful — augments, doesn't replace your judgment

✗ What doesn't

  • $20/mo is steep for a planner — Todoist is $5, Notion is free
  • Daily planning takes 5-10 minutes — real time cost on busy mornings
  • Not built for team collaboration — this is a personal tool, full stop
  • Mobile app is functional but light — desktop-first product
  • Strict daily cap can feel limiting if your work is genuinely 50 tasks/day

Verdict

Sunsama is the productivity tool for people who have tried everything and still feel scattered. It won't make you a 10x worker — no tool can — but it will give you the daily ritual that makes your existing 1x work feel less chaotic. The 14-day trial is long enough to feel the difference. For founders, PMs, and consultants drowning in cross-tool tasks, it's the best $20/mo in productivity. For everyone else, Notion + a calendar works fine.
