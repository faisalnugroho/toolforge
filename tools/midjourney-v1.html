From v1 to today: why Midjourney still leads on beauty

Midjourney set the visual bar for AI image generation and has shipped major version jumps — from the blurry v1 days to the photographic, coherent output of v6. It runs through Discord and a web app, and remains the default for illustrators, concept artists, and brand teams who care about aesthetics over raw control.

Who it's for: Illustrators, concept artists, marketers, and anyone who needs gorgeous, brand-ready imagery without a photoshoot.

Key features

Version evolution v1 through v6

Each release added coherence, prompt adherence, and realism — v6 is photographic.

--stylize Aesthetic control

Web app Beyond Discord

A polished browser editor with canvas, layers, and reordering.

Editor & zoom Iterative refinement

Pan, zoom, and vary any region without restarting the prompt.

The honest take

✓ What works

  • Best-in-class aesthetic quality
  • Strong community and prompt ecosystem
  • Steady, meaningful version improvements
  • Great for concept and mood art

✗ What doesn't

  • No true free tier
  • Discord-first heritage frustrates some
  • Less precise editing than Photoshop-style tools
  • Ethical and ownership debates continue

Verdict

Midjourney is still the beauty champion in 2026. The $10/mo Basic plan is enough to experiment; serious users want Standard or Pro. For maximum control, pair it with Stable Diffusion or Leonardo.
