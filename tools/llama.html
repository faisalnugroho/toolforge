The open model everyone builds on

Llama is Meta's open-weight model family and the backbone of a huge share of self-hosted and open AI apps. In 2026 Llama 4 spans efficient edge models to frontier-class multimodal versions, with a community and tooling ecosystem no other open model can match.

Who it's for: Builders who want the largest open model ecosystem — startups, researchers, and enterprises avoiding lock-in.

Key features

Open ecosystem Unmatched tooling

The widest support across Ollama, vLLM, Hugging Face, and every framework.

Multimodal Text, vision, more

Llama 4 handles images and long context across the family.

Scalable Edge to frontier

Tiny variants for devices, massive ones for data centers.

Fine-tunable Yours to adapt

LoRA, full fine-tunes, and distillation are all well documented.

The honest take

✓ What works

  • Largest open-source model community
  • Excellent quality-to-size ratio
  • Runs locally with Ollama/LM Studio
  • No per-token API cost
  • Constantly improving

✗ What doesn't

  • License restricts some large commercial use
  • You manage infra and GPUs
  • Frontier tasks still trail Claude/GPT
  • Quality varies by size

Verdict

Llama remains the default open model in 2026 for one reason: ecosystem. If you are building on open weights, the tooling and community around Llama save you weeks. Run it locally with Ollama and you own your AI stack.
