The managed RAG layer most teams should be using

Building retrieval-augmented generation from scratch means choosing embeddings, a vector DB, a chunking strategy, a reranker, a retrieval API, and an eval pipeline — that's a 2-week project minimum, more if you care about correctness. Ragie wraps all of it in a single hosted API: upload your PDFs, Notion, Google Drive, or raw text, and you get a retrieval endpoint with citations, multi-modal support, and streaming responses. It's the "Stripe for RAG" pitch, and it largely delivers.

Who it's for: Founders and full-stack engineers shipping a chatbot, support assistant, or "ask your data" feature without a dedicated ML team. Also a strong fit for internal-tool builders who want RAG without maintaining Pinecone + LangChain glue.

Key features

Connect Native connectors

One-click syncs for Google Drive, Notion, Confluence, Slack, and more. Auto-reindexes on source changes — no manual refresh pipeline to babysit.

Cite Inline citations

Every answer comes back with source spans linked back to the original document chunk. Critical for support/legal/compliance use cases where hallucinations cost money.

Multi-modal Image & table parsing

Ragie extracts text from tables, charts, and images inside PDFs — not just plain paragraphs. Most DIY RAG setups choke on the same documents Ragie handles out of the box.

SDK Drop-in SDKs

Official JavaScript/TypeScript and Python SDKs with a 5-line "chat with your data" example. Bring your own LLM key or use Ragie's hosted models.

The honest take

✓ What works

  • Real time-saver — a working RAG pipeline in an afternoon, not a sprint
  • Native connectors mean no custom ETL for common SaaS data sources
  • Citations and source spans are first-class, not bolted on
  • Generous free tier (1M chunks) covers most side projects
  • Honest, developer-focused docs with copy-paste examples

✗ What doesn't

  • Pro plan ($29/mo) is required for production-scale usage and SSO
  • Less control than rolling your own — can't tune custom reranking easily
  • Vendor lock-in: switching providers means re-indexing everything
  • For very large corpora (10M+ chunks), pricing scales faster than DIY

Verdict

If your business isn't "we built a better RAG pipeline," you probably shouldn't be building one. Ragie lets you ship the chatbot-your-data feature this week, and switch to a DIY stack later only if usage economics demand it. For 90% of teams, that trade is a no-brainer.
