What Gumloop does best

the automation platform that finally made 'no-code' honest. Instead of dragging boxes around a flowchart, you describe what you want ('when a new lead comes in from Typeform, enrich them with Clearbit, draft a personalized email with GPT-4, and add them to my HubSpot list') — Gumloop builds it. It's Zapier + Make + AI agents in one tool, and the learning curve is measured in minutes, not weeks.

Who it's for: Founders, ops teams, growth marketers, and anyone who needs automation but doesn't want to learn Zapier's 800-step flow UI. Especially strong for workflows where AI is a core step (enrichment, summarization, drafting).

Key features

💬 Prompt-to-workflow

Describe the automation in plain English. Gumloop's AI builds the flow — nodes, integrations, conditions, all of it.

🔌 100+ integrations

Slack, Notion, HubSpot, Salesforce, Google Sheets, Airtable, Typeform, Linear, and more. Same library as Zapier.

🧠 AI nodes built-in

GPT-4, Claude, Gemini, and custom models available as drag-and-drop steps. No API keys to manage.

🪝 Webhooks + HTTP

Hit any API with custom HTTP nodes. Real developers can extend; non-developers don't have to.

👥 Team workspaces

Share workflows, version them, and run them across your team with role-based permissions.

The honest take

✓ What works

  • AI-first design — every step can use an LLM without workarounds
  • Free tier actually useful (1,000 runs/month is enough to test)
  • Way faster to build than Zapier for AI-heavy flows
  • Real customer support (Slack channel, humans, <2hr response)
  • Scales from solo founder to enterprise ops teams

✗ What doesn't

  • Smaller integration library than Zapier (1,000+ vs 100+)
  • AI-generated flows sometimes need a manual fix
  • No mobile app — web only
  • Pricing gets steep at scale (Pro is $37/mo, Business is custom)

Verdict

If you've been meaning to automate something but Zapier's pricing or UI scared you off, Gumloop is the answer. The AI prompt-to-workflow is genuinely magical — describe what you want, get a working automation 80% of the time. For AI-heavy workflows (enrichment, drafting, routing), it's now our default over Zapier or Make.
