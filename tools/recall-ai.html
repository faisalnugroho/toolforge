Your second brain, powered by AI

Recall AI is a personal knowledge management tool that automatically summarizes any web content — articles, YouTube videos, podcasts, PDFs — and organizes it into an interconnected knowledge graph. Instead of bookmarking things you'll never revisit, Recall turns every link into a concise summary with key points, tags, and automatic connections to related content you've saved before.

Who it's for: Researchers, students, content creators, and knowledge workers who consume a lot of information and want to actually retain and retrieve it later. Especially powerful for people who already use tools like Notion or Obsidian.

Key features

Summarize Auto-summarization

Paste any URL and Recall generates a structured summary with key takeaways. Works on articles, YouTube videos, podcasts, and PDFs — extracting the signal from noise.

Link Knowledge graph

Automatically connects related notes. When you save a new article about "transformer architectures," Recall links it to your existing notes on attention mechanisms and neural networks.

Sync Cross-device access

Browser extension for one-click saving, plus iOS and Android apps. Your knowledge base syncs everywhere and works offline.

Chat Chat with notes

Ask questions about your saved content. "What did that article about tokenization say about BPE?" — Recall searches your knowledge base and answers with citations.

The honest take

✓ What works

  • Best-in-class summarization quality — captures nuance, not just keywords
  • Knowledge graph auto-linking is genuinely useful for research
  • Clean, fast UI that doesn't get in your way
  • Browser extension makes saving frictionless
  • Chat feature actually understands your notes context

✗ What doesn't

  • Free tier is very limited (10 summaries/month)
  • No native Obsidian or Notion integration for sync
  • Video and podcast summaries sometimes miss key points
  • Search can be slow with large knowledge bases (500+ notes)
  • Export options are limited — you're somewhat locked in

Verdict

If you consume a lot of content and struggle to remember or find what you've learned, Recall AI is one of the best tools in the "AI second brain" category. The auto-linking knowledge graph is its killer feature — it connects ideas you wouldn't have thought to link yourself. At $10/mo, it's a reasonable investment for serious researchers and learners.
