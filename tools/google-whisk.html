Drag, drop, remix — the image tool that thinks in layers

Google Whisk is a visual remixing tool from Google Labs. Instead of typing text prompts, you drag images into "subject" and "style" slots and Whisk generates a new image that combines them. Want your product photo rendered in the style of a watercolor painting? Drop both in and go. It's powered by Google's Gemini and Imagen models.

Who it's for: Designers, marketers, and content creators who want to quickly remix images without learning prompt engineering. Especially useful for product photography, social media content, and mood boards.

Key features

Visual Input Drag-and-drop remixing

No text prompts needed. Drag an image into the "subject" slot and another into the "style" slot. Whisk understands what's in each image and combines them intelligently.

Subject Swap Replace subjects

Keep the scene and style, but swap the main subject. Put your cat into a Renaissance painting, or your product into a lifestyle photo.

Style Transfer Apply art styles

Drop in a reference image (painting, sketch, photo style) and Whisk applies that aesthetic to your subject while keeping it recognizable.

Batch Generate variations

Each remix generates multiple variations. Pick your favorite, refine further, or download all of them for A/B testing on social media.

The honest take

✓ What works

  • Visual-first interface — no prompt engineering required
  • Excellent subject+style separation that text prompts struggle with
  • Free through Google Labs with generous usage limits
  • Powered by Imagen 3 — quality is competitive with Midjourney for many use cases
  • Fast generation — results in under 10 seconds

✗ What doesn't

  • Limited control compared to full prompt-based tools like Midjourney
  • Sometimes struggles with complex multi-subject scenes
  • No API — can't automate or integrate into workflows
  • Still in Labs — could be discontinued or moved behind a paywall

Verdict

Google Whisk is the easiest way to remix images without learning prompt engineering. It's free, fast, and produces surprisingly good results. For professional work, you'll want more control — but for quick social media content and creative exploration, it's hard to beat.
