PM that scales with the org

Wrike is built for companies where projects span departments. Its request intake forms feed a customizable workspace, Gantt timelines keep dependencies honest, and role-based dashboards give every leader the view they need. It's heavier than Asana or Trello, but that's the point — it handles real organizational complexity.

Who it's for: Operations, marketing, and PMO teams at mid-size and enterprise companies running cross-functional work.

Key features

Workflows Custom request intake

Dynamic request forms auto-route work into the right project and assign owners.

Gantt Timeline view

Visualize dependencies and reschedule by dragging — changes cascade automatically.

Dashboards Cross-team views

Real-time widgets roll up status across teams for leadership without status meetings.

Automation Rules engine

Trigger status changes, assignments, and alerts when conditions are met.

The honest take

✓ What works

  • Powerful for cross-functional, multi-team work
  • Request intake is genuinely best-in-class
  • Flexible dashboards and reporting
  • Strong permissions and governance
  • Free tier supports small teams

✗ What doesn't

  • Steeper learning curve than Asana/Trello
  • Interface can feel busy
  • Best features locked to higher tiers
  • Setup takes real admin effort

Verdict

Wrike earns its keep when projects cross team boundaries and you need governance. The $9.80 Team plan is a low-risk way to pilot it before scaling.
