Find the right paper in seconds

Semantic Scholar indexes over 200 million papers and uses NLP to surface TLDRs, key claims, and influence graphs. Ask a question and get cited sources ranked by impact, not just recency. The free API powers half the research startups on the web.

Who it's for: Students, academics, and R&D teams who need to navigate the literature without paywalls or endless PubMed scrolling.

Key features

TLDRs AI summaries

Every paper gets a one-sentence TLDR so you can triage 50 results in minutes.

Citation graph Influence tracing

See which papers a work builds on and which cite it — skip the noise.

Ask Natural language

Search in plain English and get relevant, cited passages, not keyword matches.

API Free access

Programmatic access to the corpus for your own research tooling.

The honest take

✓ What works

  • Completely free, no article paywall for metadata
  • TLDRs save enormous triage time
  • Best-in-class citation and influence graphs
  • Open API for builders
  • Covers 200M+ papers across fields

✗ What doesn't

  • Doesn't host full text — links out to publishers
  • Search can miss very new preprints
  • Less polished UX than commercial rivals
  • No built-in reference manager

Verdict

For literature discovery, Semantic Scholar is the free default in 2026. Pair it with Elicit or Consensus for synthesis, and you've got a research stack that costs nothing.
