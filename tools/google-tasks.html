Sometimes simple is the best feature

Google Tasks is the no-frills task manager that lives inside Gmail and Google Calendar. It does one thing — manage checklists — and does it well. No AI, no databases, no custom views. If you've ever dragged an email to your task list or added a task from a calendar event, that's Google Tasks working behind the scenes. It's the tool you didn't know you were already using.

Who it's for: Anyone who lives in Gmail and Google Calendar and just wants a simple checklist. Perfect for lightweight task management without the overhead of a full productivity system.

Key features

Gmail Gmail integration

Drag any email to the Tasks sidebar to create a task. The task links back to the email, so you never lose context. The fastest email-to-action workflow.

Calendar Calendar sync

Tasks with due dates show up on Google Calendar. See your to-dos alongside your events without opening a separate app.

Lists Multiple lists

Create separate lists for Work, Personal, Shopping, etc. Switch between them with one click. Star important tasks for quick access.

Subtasks Subtasks

Nest tasks under parent tasks for simple project breakdown. Not as powerful as a real project tool, but enough for most personal use cases.

The honest take

✓ What works

  • Completely free — no premium tier, no upsell
  • Deep Gmail and Calendar integration is the killer feature
  • Dead simple — zero learning curve
  • Cross-platform — web, iOS, Android, and in Gmail sidebar
  • Fast and lightweight — opens instantly

✗ What doesn't

  • No AI features whatsoever
  • No natural language input (like Todoist or TickTick)
  • No calendar view within the app itself
  • No collaboration — purely individual
  • Very limited — no tags, no priorities, no custom fields

Verdict

Google Tasks won't replace Todoist or Things 3 for power users, but it doesn't try to. It's the best free task manager for people who just want to drag emails to a checklist and move on. If you're a Gmail user who hasn't tried it, open the Tasks sidebar right now — it's already there.
