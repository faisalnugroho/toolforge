Canva with Adobe Firefly under the hood

Adobe Express is what happens when Adobe takes on Canva with their Firefly AI in tow. In 2026, it's a legitimate Canva competitor with deep Adobe Creative Cloud integration, commercial-safe AI image generation (Firefly), and surprisingly good templates. If you're already paying for Photoshop/Illustrator, Express comes bundled. If you're not, the free tier is a fine entry point — though Canva still wins on UX polish.

Who it's for: Adobe Creative Cloud subscribers who want quick social graphics, marketers who need commercial-safe AI images (Firefly is indemnity-protected), educators and small businesses.

Key features

🔥 Firefly AI built in

Generate images, edit photos, remove backgrounds, generate text effects — all powered by Adobe Firefly. Commercially safe (trained on licensed data only) and fully indemnified for enterprise use.

📚 Templates + assets

100K+ templates, 25M+ Adobe Stock photos included on Premium. Drag-and-drop editor with full Adobe Fonts library. Same quality assets as Photoshop users get.

🎬 Video + animation

Quick video editor for social media (Reels, TikTok, Shorts). Animate text, add transitions, use templates. Not Premiere Pro — but faster for short-form content.

🔗 CC integration

Open and edit Photoshop (.psd), Illustrator (.ai), and InDesign files in Express. Round-trip with full Creative Cloud apps. Bridges quick design and pro design.

The honest take

✓ What works

  • Firefly AI is commercially safe and indemnified (key for enterprise)
  • Deep Creative Cloud integration if you're already paying for Adobe
  • Free tier is genuinely usable (no watermarks on AI outputs)
  • Adobe Stock photos included on Premium plan
  • Quick actions (remove background, resize, animate) save real time

✗ What doesn't

  • UX still feels rougher than Canva (loading times, navigation)
  • Smaller template library than Canva
  • Free tier limits AI generations (250/month)
  • Video editor is basic — CapCut is better for serious work

Verdict

Adobe Express is the right choice if you're already in the Adobe ecosystem or need commercial-safe AI image generation. For pure UX polish and template variety, Canva still wins. The free tier is solid; the $10 Premium is worth it only if you also pay for Creative Cloud. Standalone, Canva Pro is better value.
