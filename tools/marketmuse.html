Content strategy, not just SEO suggestions

MarketMuse treats your content like a product portfolio. It runs a content audit on your entire site, identifies gaps in your topic coverage, scores every page against competitors, and recommends exactly what to write next. It's not a keyword tool — it's a content intelligence platform that thinks in topics, not strings.

Who it's for: Content teams publishing 20+ articles/month, agencies managing 10+ clients, and SEO directors who need defensible content strategy.

Key features

Topic authority Cluster-level scoring

Instead of per-page keyword score, MarketMuse scores your authority on entire topics. See exactly where you dominate and where you're missing.

Content audit Whole-site diagnostics

Crawl your site, get a heatmap of every URL's quality and value. Cut the thin content. Prioritize the page-and-a-half old pieces that just need a refresh.

Optimize Real-time brief generator

Type your target keyword — get a content brief with suggested subtopics, questions to answer, internal links, target word count, and competitive difficulty.

Research Competitive intelligence

Type any topic, see the entire search landscape: who's ranking, what angle they're taking, what they're missing, and where you can out-expert them.

The honest take

✓ What works

  • Cluster-level topic scoring — unique in the category
  • Content audit is genuinely useful for cleaning up old sites
  • Brief generator saves editors hours of research per article
  • Free audit tool is the best in the industry
  • Used by Forbes, HubSpot, IBM — proven at scale

✗ What doesn't

  • $149/mo is steep for solo creators and small blogs
  • Learning curve — feels like enterprise software
  • Less real-time SERP tracking than Ahrefs or SEMrush
  • AI-generated drafts are still rough — needs human editing

Verdict

If you're publishing more than 20 articles a month across a team, MarketMuse will pay for itself by stopping you from writing the wrong things. The content audit alone justifies the price for sites with 500+ pages. Smaller publishers should start with Surfer SEO ($89/mo) and only upgrade to MarketMuse when they outgrow it.
