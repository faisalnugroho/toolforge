What DocuSign actually does

DocuSign invented the e-signature category and still owns it. Send a contract, get it signed in minutes, automatic audit trail, legally binding in 180+ jurisdictions. The 2026 version adds AI contract analysis (highlights risky clauses, suggests edits), CLM (Contract Lifecycle Management), and bulk send.

Who it's for: Anyone who signs contracts — sales teams, HR, legal, real estate, finance, freelancers sending SOWs.

Key features

✍️ Legally binding e-signatures

Compliant with ESIGN, eIDAS, UETA, and 180+ other regulations. The original and still the gold standard.

🤖 AI contract analysis (Iris)

Auto-extracts parties, dates, payment terms, and renewal clauses. Flags unusual or risky language.

📦 Bulk send & templates

Send the same contract to 1,000 recipients with one click. Auto-merge fields from a spreadsheet.

🔗 1,000+ integrations

Salesforce, HubSpot, Slack, Google Workspace, Microsoft 365, Workday, NetSuite — every major app.

The honest take

✓ What works

  • The original and most legally defensible e-signature
  • Massive integration ecosystem
  • AI contract analysis is genuinely useful (catches renewal traps)
  • Mobile signing actually works on every device
  • Enterprise-grade security (SOC 2, HIPAA, FedRAMP)

✗ What doesn't

  • $25/mo for the basic plan is steep for individuals
  • Free tier is unusable (3 envelopes/month)
  • CLM features are locked behind Enterprise tier ($60+/user/mo)
  • UI is dated in places

Verdict

DocuSign is the default for good reason — it works, it's legally defensible everywhere, and it integrates with everything. For occasional use (1-5 signatures/month), Dropbox Sign or HelloSign at $15/mo is more cost-effective. For 50+ signatures/month, DocuSign Standard is the right pick.
