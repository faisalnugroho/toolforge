The honest take

✓ What works

  • Truly no-code: a non-technical user can train a robot in 2 minutes
  • Pre-built robots for LinkedIn, Zillow, Google Maps, and 200+ other sites save hours of setup
  • Change monitoring + Slack alerts = real-time competitive intel without manual checking
  • Handles JavaScript-heavy sites and infinite scroll that break simpler scrapers
  • Bulk run lets you scale from 1 URL to 10,000 without writing parallelization code

✗ What does not

  • Credit-based pricing punishes mistakes — a misconfigured robot can burn through credits fast
  • Not as flexible as code-based scrapers (Playwright, Scrapy) for highly custom data structures
  • Free tier (50 credits) is essentially a 1-day trial — you cannot actually run a real workflow on it
  • Some sites actively block scrapers; you will occasionally need to switch to their API or use a proxy add-on
  • Less suited to authenticated scraping at scale (login sessions expire and need babysitting)

Verdict

Browse AI is the best no-code web scraper in 2026 for the 80% case: extract structured data from a list of pages, monitor for changes, and push to a spreadsheet or CRM. The pre-built robots are a huge shortcut for common sites. If your needs are simple (lead lists, price monitoring, listings), Browse AI is faster and cheaper than hiring a developer to write a custom scraper. If you need to scrape authenticated dashboards, parse unstructured PDFs, or build a scraper for a one-off research project, the credit costs add up fast and you are better off with Apify or a freelance developer.
