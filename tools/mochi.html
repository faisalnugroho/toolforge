Open text-to-video that actually moves

Mochi, from Genmo, was one of the first open text-to-video models to rival closed rivals on motion quality. The open weights let you self-host; the hosted app gives you prompts-to-clip with surprisingly coherent movement and decent prompt following.

Who it's for: Creators and builders who want video generation without a paywall, or who need to self-host for control and privacy.

Key features

T2V Prompt to video

Describe a scene and get a few seconds of coherent motion.

Open weights Self-host

Run Mochi-1 locally if you have the GPU — no usage caps.

Motion Coherent movement

Better limb and physics handling than many early open models.

I2V Image to video

Animate a still image with directed camera and motion.

The honest take

✓ What works

  • Free and open — no credits to burn
  • Strong motion coherence for an open model
  • Self-hostable for privacy and scale
  • Active open-source community
  • Good prompt adherence

✗ What doesn't

  • Clips are short (seconds, not minutes)
  • Needs serious GPU to self-host well
  • Trails Runway/Kling on realism
  • Editing and stitching is manual

Verdict

Mochi is the open video generator to watch in 2026. Use the hosted app for quick experiments; self-host the weights when you need volume or privacy. Pair with Runway or Kling for production finish.
