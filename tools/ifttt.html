What IFTTT actually does

IFTTT (If This Then That) is the OG consumer automation platform — predating Zapier by a year. While Zapier targets businesses with complex workflows, IFTTT targets consumers and small business owners who want simple "if X happens, do Y" automations across 800+ services. The killer use cases are personal: auto-save Instagram photos to Dropbox, get an SMS when a stock crosses a threshold, turn on Hue lights when your Uber arrives. New in 2025: AI queries let you trigger automations on natural-language events ("if email mentions an invoice, save to Google Drive folder").

Who it's for: Smart home enthusiasts, solo creators and freelancers, social media managers on personal brands, and anyone who wants simple cross-app automations without the complexity of Zapier.

Key features

🔌 800+ service integrations

Every major app, smart home device, and SaaS. Including services Zapier doesn't support well: Twitter/X, Sonos, Tesla, LIFX, Strava, Todoist.

🤖 AI Query (new in 2025)

Trigger automations from natural language: "if email contains an invoice", "if article mentions your company", "if tweet has over 1000 likes". Solves the brittle "exact keyword match" problem.

📱 Mobile-first design

Native iOS and Android apps. Most useful automations are mobile-triggered (location, photos, calls, SMS) — and IFTTT handles those better than web-only competitors.

🏪 Marketplace of pre-built applets

Thousands of community-built automations you can activate in one click. Discoverable UI means non-technical users can build useful flows without starting from scratch.

The honest take

✓ What works

  • Cheapest automation tool on the market at $5/mo
  • Mobile + smart home + location triggers that Zapier doesn't support
  • AI Query closes the gap on natural-language triggers
  • Marketplace means non-technical users can build in 30 seconds
  • Reliability has improved dramatically post-2023 platform rewrite

✗ What doesn't

  • One trigger → one action is too simple for business workflows (use Zapier or Make)
  • Free tier limited to 5 active applets — usable but tight
  • Some integrations lag behind Zapier for B2B SaaS (Salesforce, HubSpot, etc.)
  • Execution speed slower than Zapier (typical trigger-to-action is 5-15 minutes)

Verdict

IFTTT is the right tool for personal automations, smart homes, mobile-triggered flows, and solo creators. It's the wrong tool for business workflows with multiple branches, error handling, and data transformations — that's Zapier or Make territory. At $5/mo it's almost a no-brainer for the personal use cases.
