What Uizard actually does

Uizard started as "AI design from sketches" — snap a photo of a whiteboard drawing and get a real digital mockup. It's since added text-to-UI, screenshot-to-edit, and a clickable prototype mode. The most accessible AI design tool on the market.

Who it's for: Non-designers, founders, and PMs who need to mock up app ideas without design skills.

Key features

Sketch Sketch-to-mockup

Photograph a hand-drawn wireframe — Uizard converts it into a real digital design.

Screenshot Screenshot-to-edit

Upload any app screenshot and Uizard turns it into an editable mockup.

Text Text-to-design

Type a description and get a fresh mockup. Less polished than Galileo but more iterative.

Proto Clickable prototypes

Link screens together for a real prototype in minutes — no code needed.

The honest take

✓ What works

  • Free tier is actually usable for solo work
  • Sketch-to-mockup is genuinely magical and unique
  • Best "non-designer friendly" AI design tool
  • Active development, new features monthly

✗ What doesn't

  • Output polish is below Galileo AI and v0
  • Component library is smaller than Figma's
  • No real-time multi-user editing
  • AI features sometimes hallucinate layout choices

Verdict

If you're not a designer and you need to communicate product ideas visually, Uizard is the most accessible AI design tool out there. The sketch-to-mockup alone is worth the $12/mo.
