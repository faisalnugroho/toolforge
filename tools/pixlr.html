Photoshop power, browser convenience

Pixlr has been the go-to web photo editor for over a decade, and in 2026 its AI features — cutout, generative fill, and a batch editor — make it a genuine lightweight Photoshop. No download, no $20/mo CC subscription. Open a tab, edit, export. It's the pragmatic choice for quick but serious image work.

Who it's for: Casual and semi-pro photographers, social media managers, and small businesses who need real editing without Adobe's cost or complexity.

Key features

Cutout AI cutout & remove

One-click subject selection and background removal. The 'Remove' tool erases objects with surprisingly clean edges.

Fill Generative fill

Select an area and describe what should be there. Pixlr's AI fills it in contextually — extend backgrounds or swap scenes.

Batch Batch editor

Apply resize, watermark, and filter changes across hundreds of images at once. A lifesaver for catalogs.

Templates Templates & mobile

Thousands of social and print templates, plus full iOS/Android apps so edits travel with you.

The honest take

✓ What works

  • No install — runs entirely in the browser
  • Genuinely powerful for the price (or free)
  • Batch editor saves hours on catalogs
  • Long track record and stable product
  • Good mobile apps

✗ What doesn't

  • Free tier shows ads and watermarks some exports
  • Not a true Photoshop replacement for advanced retouching
  • Interface is busier than Canva
  • AI fill trails dedicated tools like Firefly
  • Heavy files can be slow in-browser

Verdict

Pixlr is the smart default for browser-based editing in 2026. If you don't need raw Photoshop, the free tier covers 90% of jobs and Premium at $7.99/mo undercuts Adobe massively. Keep Photoshop for the 10%.
