Scholarcy is the closest thing to an academic reading assistant that exists

Scholarcy is the AI summarizer that actually understands academic paper structure. It extracts the abstract, methods, findings, and limitations into a clean summary card — like a SparkNotes for research papers. Built by PhDs, for PhDs.

Who it's for: PhD students, academic researchers, literature review writers, and anyone drowning in PDFs.

Key features

Card Summary cards

Auto-generates a structured summary card with: key findings, methods, sample size, limitations.

Ref Reference extraction

Pulls all citations into a clean reference list with DOI links. Saves hours of manual extraction.

Compare Cross-paper compare

Compare multiple paper summaries side-by-side. Great for lit reviews.

Chrome Browser extension

Right-click any PDF or arXiv link → instant summary card.

The honest take

✓ What works

  • Built by researchers, for researchers — knows academic structure
  • Reference extraction saves 2-3 hours per literature review
  • Browser extension works on arXiv, PubMed, Google Scholar
  • Generous free tier (3/day) for casual use
  • Pro plan at $9.99/mo is cheaper than Elicit or Scite

✗ What doesn't

  • Best for academic papers — struggles with legal/business PDFs
  • No real "chat with paper" — focused on summaries only
  • Free tier limited to 3 summaries/day
  • Reference extraction accuracy is ~85% — still needs manual cleanup

Verdict

Scholarcy is the closest thing to an academic reading assistant that exists. It won't replace actually reading the paper, but it will tell you in 30 seconds whether a 40-page paper is worth your afternoon. For PhD students doing lit reviews, the $9.99/mo Pro plan pays for itself in the first week.
