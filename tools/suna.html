An agent that actually finishes the job

Suna is the open-source answer to closed generalist agents. Give it a goal — 'research competitors and drop a summary in my doc' — and it opens a real browser, clicks, types, runs Python, and ships output instead of just chatting about it. Because it's open source, you can self-host for privacy and unlimited runs.

Who it's for: Founders, ops leads, and researchers who need a tireless assistant for multi-step web and data work.

Key features

Browser Real autonomy

Suna opens a real browser, clicks, types, and fills forms to complete multi-step tasks end to end.

Code Runs your work

Write and execute Python, manipulate files, and call APIs to produce actual output, not just text.

Open source Self-hostable

Full source on GitHub. Run it on your own infra for privacy and unlimited use.

Integrations Connects to your stack

Gmail, calendar, GitHub, and more — Suna plugs into the tools you already use.

The honest take

✓ What works

  • Genuinely autonomous — completes full tasks, not just chat
  • Open source and self-hostable
  • Strong for research, data, and ops automation
  • Active community and fast iteration
  • Generous free cloud tier

✗ What doesn't

  • Still young — occasional agent missteps
  • Self-hosting needs technical setup
  • Compute credits can run out on heavy use
  • Less polished UX than closed rivals

Verdict

Suna is the most exciting open generalist agent of 2026. For hands-off research and automation it beats chatbots by a mile, and self-hosting kills the 'am I sending my data where?' worry. Pair it with Manus if you want a managed alternative.
