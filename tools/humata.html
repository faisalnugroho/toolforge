If you read PDFs for work or school, Humata pays for itself in the first week

Humata is the AI PDF reader that took academic and legal circles by storm in 2024-2025. Upload a 200-page research paper, legal brief, or financial report, and ask plain-English questions — it returns cited answers with page references. No more Ctrl-F for an hour.

Who it's for: Researchers, lawyers, students, analysts, and anyone who reads dense PDFs for a living.

Key features

Cite Cited answers

Every response includes the exact page numbers and highlighted passages from the source PDF. No more hallucinated answers.

Unlimited Long documents

Handles 200+ page documents in a single upload. Most competitors cap at 50-100 pages.

Summarize Auto-summary

One-click summaries at any length — TL;DR, executive summary, or detailed chapter breakdown.

Compare Multi-doc search

Upload 10+ PDFs and ask questions across the whole corpus. Useful for literature reviews.

The honest take

✓ What works

  • Best-in-class citation accuracy — every answer links to source pages
  • Free tier is genuinely useful (60 pages, no credit card)
  • Handles scanned PDFs via OCR (rare for AI PDF tools)
  • Fast — 200-page paper answers in under 10 seconds
  • HIPAA-compliant team plan for healthcare/legal use cases

✗ What doesn't

  • Free tier limited to 60 pages per document
  • Pro tier ($14.99/mo) is per-seat — gets expensive for teams
  • No offline mode — needs internet to process
  • Citation formatting limited to APA/MLA — no Chicago/Turabian

Verdict

If you read PDFs for work or school, Humata pays for itself in the first week. The citation feature alone is worth the $14.99/mo — no other AI PDF tool comes close on accuracy. Skip it only if you need offline access or process thousands of pages a month (then look at Adobe Acrobat AI or ChatPDF Enterprise).
