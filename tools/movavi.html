The editor normal people actually finish with

Movavi has been the "I just need to cut this and export it" editor for two decades. In 2026 it ships AI upscaling, automatic background removal, noise suppression, and one-click enhancement — without the subscription treadmill or the learning cliff of Premiere. It's the tool you reach for when the job is done, not when you want to become an editor.

Who it's for: Content creators, small-business owners, and educators who need reliable edits fast without a steep learning curve.

Key features

UpscaleAI resolution boost

Push 720p footage toward 4K with detail recovery — ideal for older clips and phone videos.

BG removalChroma-free cutouts

Strip backgrounds from video and photos automatically. No green screen required.

DenoiseAudio cleanup

One click removes hum, hiss, and room echo from voice tracks recorded on the fly.

Convert120+ formats

The classic Movavi superpower: batch convert and compress anything to anything.

The honest take

✓ What works

  • Gentle learning curve — usable in minutes
  • One-time-ish yearly pricing, no heavy subscription
  • AI upscale and denoise punch above the price
  • Rock-solid format conversion
  • Runs fine on modest hardware

✗ What doesn't

  • Not a substitute for pro NLEs on complex timelines
  • AI features trail dedicated tools like Topaz
  • Interface feels dated next to CapCut
  • Some effects are template-thin

Verdict

Movavi is the sensible default for "I need this edited today." At $55/yr it's cheaper than a month of most pro suites, and the AI adds just enough polish that your exports don't look like 2019. Reach for CapCut or Runway when you need generative magic; reach for Movavi when you need it done.
