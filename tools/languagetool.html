Grammarly, but open and multilingual

LanguageTool is the grammar checker for people who are not writing in English — or who care about privacy. It supports 30+ languages, flags style and tone issues (not just typos), and offers a self-hosted enterprise edition. The free tier is solid; Premium adds paraphrasing and advanced style.

Who it's for: Multilingual writers, Europeans, privacy-conscious teams, and developers who want to self-host a checker.

Key features

30+ languages Truly multilingual

One checker for English, German, Spanish, French, and more.

Style + tone Beyond typos

Flags wordiness, informal tone, and inconsistent style.

Privacy mode No storage

Option to process text without saving it.

Self-host Own your data

Enterprise edition runs fully on your infrastructure.

The honest take

✓ What works

  • Best multilingual grammar checker
  • Privacy-first, including self-hosting
  • Free tier is capable
  • Browser + Office + API coverage

✗ What doesn't

  • English suggestions less polished than Grammarly
  • Premium paraphrasing is weaker
  • UI is plainer
  • No dedicated mobile keyboard like Grammarly

Verdict

If you write in more than one language, or you care about privacy, LanguageTool beats Grammarly outright. English-only writers who want the slickest experience may still prefer Grammarly — but LanguageTool is the more open, more honest choice.
