What Mailchimp actually does

Mailchimp is the 800-pound gorilla of email marketing. Acquired by Intuit for $12B, it now ships with GPT-powered subject line generation, AI send-time optimization, predictive segments, and a creative assistant that writes email copy from a prompt. Best-in-class deliverability and integrations with every major ecommerce and CRM platform.

Who it's for: Small businesses, ecommerce brands, and marketers who need reliable email + basic automations.

Key features

AI Creative Assistant

GPT-4 writes email copy, subject lines, and CTAs based on your brand voice and campaign goal.

Predict Predictive segments

Likelihood-to-purchase scoring, churn risk detection, and recommended products per customer.

Auto Customer Journey Builder

Visual automation builder with branching paths, A/B testing, and 100+ pre-built journeys.

Ecom Ecommerce integrations

Native Shopify, WooCommerce, BigCommerce integrations. Dynamic product blocks in emails.

The honest take

✓ What works

  • Most well-known email platform — easiest to get buy-in from non-technical stakeholders
  • AI Creative Assistant writes decent copy from one-line prompts
  • Free tier covers small lists (up to 500 contacts)
  • Integrates with everything (Shopify, Salesforce, WordPress, Zapier)

✗ What doesn't

  • Pricing escalates quickly at scale (10K contacts = $200+/mo)
  • Automation builder is less powerful than ActiveCampaign or ConvertKit
  • Templates feel dated compared to newer competitors
  • Support quality has dropped since Intuit acquisition

Verdict

For small businesses, ecommerce brands, and marketers who want a familiar platform with AI features, Mailchimp is still the safe choice. For automation-heavy creators and solopreneurs, ConvertKit or Beehiiv are better. For enterprise-grade automations, ActiveCampaign wins.
