The recording studio that runs in a Chrome tab

Riverside is the recording platform that took over podcasting in 2023-2025 and now dominates AI video workflows. The killer feature: every participant is recorded locally in their browser (or app), so even if someone's WiFi drops, you get studio-quality 4K video and 48kHz WAV audio. Then AI does the rest — transcripts, captions, short-form clips, translations. It's Descript, Opus Clip, and a recording booth in one.

Who it's for: Podcasters, YouTubers, course creators, B2B marketers doing webinars, and anyone who interviews guests remotely more than twice a month.

Key features

4K Local 4K recording

Each guest's video and audio is recorded locally on their machine — not over the internet. If your call drops to 144p, the recording stays 4K. The single biggest technical upgrade over Zoom for serious content.

AI AI transcripts in 100+ languages

Auto-generated transcripts with speaker labels and timecodes. 95%+ accuracy on clear English, slightly worse on heavy accents but improving fast. Edit the transcript and the video edits itself.

Magic Clips AI short-form clips

Hit 'Magic Clips' and Riverside finds the 5-15 most viral moments, reframes them to 9:16, adds captions, and exports to TikTok/Reels/Shorts-ready MP4s. Saves 1-2 hours per long video.

Caption Auto-captions with styles

Burn-in animated captions (Hormozi-style, Ali Abdaal-style, karaoke-style). One click, fully editable, exported with the video.

The honest take

✓ What works

  • Local recording is genuinely best-in-class — survives bad internet
  • Magic Clips is the best built-in 'Opus Clip alternative' we've tested
  • Up to 4K video + 48kHz uncompressed WAV audio — overkill in a good way
  • Mobile app works as a wireless lapel mic + webcam
  • Free tier is actually usable (2 hours/mo, watermarked exports)

✗ What doesn't

  • No true multi-track editor — for that you still need Descript or Premiere
  • Magic Clips sometimes picks weird moments — still needs a human skim
  • $24/mo Standard is steep if you only record solo content
  • Browser-only editor is improving but slower than Descript's text-based editor

Verdict

If you record remotely with guests — for a podcast, course, YouTube interview, or client testimonial — Riverside is the new default in 2026. The local recording is the real moat; everything else (Magic Clips, AI captions) is gravy. Descript still wins for text-based editing, but for raw capture quality, nothing else comes close.
