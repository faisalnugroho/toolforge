The fastest AI video generator

Genmo started as a research preview and quietly shipped the most underrated video model of 2025. Replay v2 generates 8-second 1080p clips in under 90 seconds — 3-5x faster than Runway Gen-3 and Pika 2.0. The catch: shorter clips, less control, fewer editing features. The win: you can iterate on ideas without burning a Saturday.

Who it's for: Solo creators, marketers, and indie founders who need fast video drafts and don't need 30-second cinematic output.

Key features

90s Fastest gen in class

Replay v2 generates an 8-second clip in 60-90 seconds. Runway and Pika take 3-5 minutes. You can iterate 10x in the time it takes to make one Runway clip.

I2V Image-to-video

Drop in a Midjourney or Flux image, get a moving 8-second clip that respects the source. Useful for animating thumbnails, social posts, and concept boards.

100 free Generous free tier

100 credits/month free forever. Enough for 12-15 short clips. Pro plan at $10/mo gives 1,500 credits (≈200 clips) — best $/credit ratio in the category.

Compose Multi-shot stories

Compose mode chains multiple clips into a 30-60 second sequence with consistent characters. The closest thing to a real AI short film tool.

The honest take

✓ What works

  • Fastest video generation in 2026 — iterate quickly
  • Best $/credit ratio for budget-conscious creators
  • Free tier is generous enough to actually use
  • Image-to-video workflow is excellent for thumbnail animation
  • Multi-shot Compose mode is genuinely novel

✗ What doesn't

  • 8-second max clip length (vs Runway's 16s, Sora's 20s)
  • Less control over camera motion and character consistency
  • Resolution tops out at 1080p — no 4K
  • No native audio — need to add sound in a separate tool

Verdict

Genmo is the indie creator's pick. If you need fast, cheap video drafts and you don't need Hollywood-grade 30-second output, it's the best $/video in 2026. Power users still want Runway or Sora for the longer-form work. Most solo creators should start on Genmo's free tier and only graduate when they need longer clips or finer control.
