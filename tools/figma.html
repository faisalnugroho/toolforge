The design tool teams actually live in

Figma turned design into a real-time, multiplayer activity. In 2026 it shipped Figma AI: generate UI drafts, rename layers, make prototypes talk, and turn screenshots into editable files. It is the default for product teams and a serious threat to legacy desktop tools.

Who it's for: Product designers, UX teams, front-end devs, and founders who need shared, version-controlled design files without installing anything.

Key features

Figma AI First Draft & generation

Describe a screen and Figma AI generates a starter layout with real components. Turn a prompt, image, or screenshot into an editable frame.

Make Design-to-code & flows

Figma Make turns designs into working front-end code and interactive prototypes you can iterate on with prompts.

FigJam Whiteboarding

The built-in jamboard for flows, retros, and wireframes — now with AI summarise and organise.

Multiplayer Real-time collaboration

Infinite cursors, live comments, and dev mode with inspectable specs — the reason teams never go back to files.

The honest take

✓ What works

  • Best-in-class real-time collaboration
  • Browser-native, nothing to install
  • Figma AI closes the design-to-code gap
  • Massive plugin + community ecosystem
  • Free tier is genuinely usable

✗ What doesn't

  • AI add-on costs extra on top of seats
  • Can get sluggish on very large files
  • Steeper learning curve than Canva for non-designers
  • Offline mode is limited

Verdict

If you build digital products with other people, Figma is non-negotiable in 2026. The AI add-on is pricey but pays for itself the first time it drafts a screen you would have spent an hour building. Pair it with a code tool and you ship faster.
