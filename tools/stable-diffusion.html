What it does best

Stable Diffusion is the open-source image model that democratized AI art. Released by Stability AI in 2022, it kicked off the open-source generative AI wave and forced every closed-source competitor to drop their prices. In 2026, Stable Diffusion 3.5 / SDXL and its derivatives (Flux, SD3) are what you use when Midjourney or DALL-E won't give you the image you want — or when you want to run it locally on your own GPU, fine-tune it on your face, and never pay per image.

Who it's for: Tinkerers, indie creators, and studios who want full control over image generation without per-image fees or content filters.

Key features

Open source Fully self-hostable

The weights are public. Run it on a gaming GPU with 8GB+ VRAM, or use ComfyUI / Automatic1111 for a no-code UI. No internet, no rate limits, no content filter you didn't configure.

Flux / SDXL State-of-the-art quality

SDXL and the Flux variants match Midjourney V6 on most prompts. Fine details, readable text, photorealistic faces — all there in 2026.

LoRA training Fine-tune on your own data

Train a 50-image LoRA in 30 minutes on a 4090. Your face, your product, your art style. Closed-source models can't do this — they don't expose fine-tuning to mere mortals.

ComfyUI Node-based workflows

Chain models, controlnets, upscalers, inpainters. Build a node graph once, run it 1,000 times. The Photoshop-of-AI-image-gen for people who hate clicking through menus.

The honest take

✓ What works

  • Truly free and open-source — no per-image costs, ever
  • Run locally for total privacy (great for medical/legal/NDA work)
  • Best fine-tuning ecosystem in the world (LoRA, DreamBooth, Textual Inversion)
  • Massive community — 100K+ models on Civitai, endless controlnets and LoRAs
  • No content filter unless you opt in — needed for legitimate artistic and medical use

✗ What doesn't

  • Requires a decent GPU (RTX 3060 12GB minimum for SDXL, ideally 4090 for Flux)
  • Setup is technical — ComfyUI has a learning curve
  • Raw SDXL outputs are often worse than Midjourney V6 out-of-the-box; needs prompt skill
  • Hardware costs add up — a good GPU is $1,500-2,500
  • Closed-source model APIs are easier if you just want pretty pictures

Verdict

If you generate more than 1,000 images a month, want privacy, or need fine-tuning, Stable Diffusion is the only rational choice. The $2,000 GPU pays for itself in 6 months versus Midjourney Pro at $60/mo. If you just want pretty pictures and don't mind paying, Midjourney is still easier.
