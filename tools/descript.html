If you can edit a doc, you can edit a podcast

Descript turns audio and video into a transcript you edit like a Google Doc. Cut a word in the text and it is gone from the media. It is the fastest way for non-editors to ship polished episodes.

Who it's for: Podcasters, YouTubers, and marketers who dread the timeline but live in docs.

Key features

Transcription Word-accurate captions

Auto-transcribe with speaker labels; fix the text, fix the media.

Overdub Text-to-speech in your voice

Type a correction and Descript speaks it in a clone of your voice.

Studio Sound One-click noise cleanup

Turn a laptop mic into a treated-studio recording.

Video Timeline-free video editing

Edit video by cutting the script; scenes reflow automatically.

The honest take

✓ What works

  • Radically simple editing model
  • Great transcription
  • Overdub saves re-records
  • Studio Sound is magic
  • Free tier exists

✗ What doesn't

  • Heavy projects can lag
  • Overdub needs voice training
  • Video is basic vs Premiere
  • Collaboration is paid

Verdict

Descript is the reason most indie podcasters ship at all. At $12/mo the Creator plan pays for itself the first time you fix a verbal tick by deleting a word instead of re-recording. The best editing-for-non-editors tool on the market.
