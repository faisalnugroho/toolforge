The aesthetic ceiling for AI image generation

Midjourney is the AI image generator that artists and designers gravitate to. While DALL-E and Stable Diffusion chase prompt adherence, Midjourney chases aesthetic quality — and wins. v7 (released late 2025) added photorealism that genuinely fools most viewers, character consistency across multiple generations, and a web app (no more Discord-only, though Discord is still there). The output has a 'Midjourney look' that has become a genre unto itself.

Who it's for: Concept artists, illustrators, designers, content creators who need visuals that don't look like AI. Less ideal for product photos that need pixel-accurate prompt following.

Key features

🎨 v7 Model

Late-2025 release with photorealism, native 2K output, and dramatically better text rendering in images. Still the gold standard for aesthetic quality.

🖼️ Web App

No more Discord-only workflow. The web app includes an editor, history, and a community feed. Discord remains as a power-user interface.

🔁 Character Consistency

Use --cref with a reference image to keep the same character across multiple generations. Game-changing for comics, storyboards, and brand mascots.

⚙️ Advanced Parameters

--ar, --style, --stylize, --chaos — granular control over aspect ratio, aesthetic intensity, and variation. Power users can dial in exactly the look they want.

The honest take

✓ What works

  • Highest aesthetic quality of any AI image model
  • v7 finally delivers on photorealism
  • Web app + Discord = two interfaces for different workflows
  • Character consistency (--cref) is a game-changer for stories and brands
  • Active community with constant prompt sharing and feedback

✗ What doesn't

  • No free tier — minimum $10/mo (though that's still cheap)
  • Less prompt-accurate than DALL-E — you steer the aesthetic, not the literal content
  • Text in images still has occasional spelling errors
  • Moderation is sometimes overzealous — won't generate 'a person drinking water'

Verdict

The default for any creative professional. If you need images that look like a designer made them, Midjourney is the answer. If you need pixel-accurate product photos or diagrams, DALL-E is better. Most people want both — and both are cheap enough to subscribe to.
