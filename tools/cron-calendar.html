The calendar that made time blocking beautiful

Cron started as an indie calendar app that prioritized design and speed above all else. It was so good that Notion acquired it in 2022, rebranding it as Notion Calendar while keeping the core experience intact. It syncs with Google Calendar and iCloud, adds a beautiful interface on top, and integrates deeply with Notion for task-to-calendar workflows.

Who it's for: Knowledge workers and Notion power users who want a beautiful, fast calendar that integrates with their workspace. Best for those who live in time blocks and need to see Notion tasks alongside calendar events.

Key features

Speed Lightning-fast

Opens instantly, navigates with keyboard, and never lags. The fastest calendar app on desktop — period. Cmd+T to jump to any date.

TZ Multi-timezone

See multiple timezones side by side. Essential for remote teams. Hover any time slot to see it across all your team's timezones instantly.

Notion Notion integration

Notion tasks with dates automatically appear on your calendar. Drag to reschedule, mark complete from the calendar view, and sync both ways.

Cmd+K Command palette

Full keyboard navigation with a command palette. Create events, switch views, jump dates — all without touching the mouse.

The honest take

✓ What works

  • Best-in-class design and speed on desktop
  • Free — completely, no paywall on core features
  • Excellent multi-timezone support for distributed teams
  • Deep Notion integration if you're in the ecosystem
  • Keyboard-first navigation saves real time

✗ What doesn't

  • No native mobile app yet (iOS/Android still limited)
  • Requires Google Calendar or iCloud — can't standalone
  • No AI features for scheduling or meeting prep
  • Windows version still catching up to Mac
  • Notion acquisition means uncertain long-term roadmap

Verdict

Cron (now Notion Calendar) is the best-looking and fastest calendar app on desktop, and it's free. If you use Notion and Google Calendar, it's a no-brainer. The lack of a mature mobile app and AI features holds it back from being the complete package, but for desktop time-blockers, nothing is more pleasant to use.
