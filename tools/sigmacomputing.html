The spreadsheet your data warehouse deserved

Sigma brings a familiar grid interface to cloud data warehouses like Snowflake and BigQuery. Analysts build models with clicks and formulas — not SQL — while every cell is a live query against billions of rows. It's the bridge between 'I live in Excel' and 'we need governed, scalable BI'.

Who it's for: Analysts and finance teams who want self-serve BI without abandoning spreadsheet intuition, on top of a modern warehouse.

Key features

Grid Familiar grid UX

Point-and-click formulas just like Sheets, but backed by warehouse-scale compute.

Live Live warehouse queries

No extracts or row caps — every value is computed live on Snowflake/BigQuery.

Govern Governed datasets

Central teams define trusted models; analysts explore without breaking things.

Embed Embed & API

Embed Sigma workbooks into customer portals or internal apps.

The honest take

✓ What works

  • Spreadsheet familiarity drives fast analyst adoption
  • Live warehouse compute, no stale extracts
  • Strong governance for enterprise scale
  • Great for finance and operational analytics

✗ What doesn't

  • Enterprise pricing, no small-team tier
  • Best value requires a cloud warehouse already
  • Less suited to highly custom visualizations
  • Learning curve on the modeling layer

Verdict

Sigma is the smartest pick in 2026 for warehouse-first teams who don't want to retrain analysts in yet another BI tool. If you're already on Snowflake or BigQuery, the time-to-value is excellent. Just budget for the enterprise plan.
