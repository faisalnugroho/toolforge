Edit video like a document

Descript transcribes your video, then lets you edit it like a text doc. Cut a word in the transcript? It's gone from the video. It's the fastest way to edit podcasts, interviews, and screen recordings. The AI overdub feature can even fix misspoken words without re-recording.

Who it's for: Podcasters, YouTubers, course creators, and anyone who edits talking-head video. Not for cinematic editing or VFX.

Key features

TranscriptionAutomatic and accurate

Descript transcribes video/audio automatically. Speaker detection, timestamps, and 22+ languages. Edit the transcript to edit the video.

OverdubAI voice cloning

Clone your voice with 2 minutes of audio. Fix misspoken words without re-recording. Scary good, slightly creepy.

Studio SoundNoise removal

One click removes background noise and echo. Makes phone audio sound like a studio mic. Essential for remote interviews.

Screen recordingRecord + edit

Record your screen and camera, then edit in Descript. Great for tutorials and demos. No separate recording tool needed.

The honest take

✓ What works

  • Fastest way to edit talking-head video — cut words in transcript
  • Overdub is magic — fix mistakes without re-recording
  • Studio Sound makes bad audio sound professional
  • Screen recording + editing in one tool
  • Good collaboration features (comments, sharing)

✗ What doesn't

  • $12/mo adds up for teams (per-seat pricing)
  • Not for cinematic editing or VFX — docs aren't timelines
  • Overdub needs ethics guardrails (deepfake potential)
  • Occasional transcription errors with heavy accents

Verdict

Descript is the best tool for editing talking-head video. If you make podcasts, courses, or YouTube videos with people talking, it'll cut your editing time in half. The $12/mo is worth it for Studio Sound alone.
