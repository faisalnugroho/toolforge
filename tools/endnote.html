The institutional heavyweight

EndNote is the reference manager institutions have standardized on for decades. With 21,000+ citation styles, a shared online library for labs, and deep Word integration, it is built for large, long-horizon projects — theses, grants, and multi-author papers where style compliance is non-negotiable.

Who it's for: PhD candidates, principal investigators, and anyone at an institution that provides EndNote and needs exhaustive style support.

Key features

Styles 21,000+ citation styles

Virtually every journal and publisher format is supported out of the box.

Online Lib Shared online library

Collaborate with co-authors in a synced library, with per-group permissions.

Word Word plugin

Insert citations and rebuild bibliographies directly inside Microsoft Word.

Annotate PDF annotation

Read, highlight, and annotate PDFs with notes linked back to references.

The honest take

✓ What works

  • Most comprehensive style coverage of any manager
  • Excellent for large collaborative libraries
  • Strong institutional licensing and support
  • Mature, predictable, battle-tested

✗ What doesn't

  • Expensive compared to free alternatives
  • UI is old-fashioned and clunky
  • Overkill for casual users
  • Desktop-first, weaker mobile story

Verdict

EndNote is worth it when your institution pays or when you need style coverage no other tool matches. For independent researchers and students, Zotero or Mendeley deliver 90% of the value for free. Choose EndNote for compliance and scale, not for delight.
