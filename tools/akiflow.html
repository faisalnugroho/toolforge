The universal inbox for tasks — that actually schedules them on your calendar

Akiflow solves the most painful problem in productivity: tasks scattered across email, Slack, Notion, Linear, Trello, and 10 other apps. Akiflow's universal inbox pulls them all in, lets you triage in 60 seconds, then drag-and-drops them onto your calendar. The 2026 AI features auto-prioritize, auto-schedule, and protect focus time. It's like Motion, but cheaper and more keyboard-driven for the Linear/Notion crowd.

Who it's for: Founders, operators, PMs, and anyone with 50+ tasks/week across multiple tools who actually wants to finish them.

Key features

Inbox Universal task inbox

Pull tasks from Gmail, Slack, Notion, Linear, Asana, Trello, ClickUp, HubSpot, Salesforce, and 40+ more. One Cmd+K away. The killer feature for anyone drowning in tools.

Time Drag-and-drop time-blocking

See your calendar and tasks side-by-side. Drag a task from the inbox onto a time slot. Done in 5 minutes a day, not 30.

AI AI auto-scheduling

Hit 'auto-plan' and Akiflow slots your unscheduled tasks into the gaps in your calendar, weighted by priority and deadline. Re-runs every morning.

Shortcuts Keyboard-first design

Cmd+K everywhere. Capture, snooze, schedule, defer — without ever leaving the keyboard. Built for the Linear/Things/Sunsama crowd.

The honest take

✓ What works

  • Universal inbox is the killer feature — 40+ integrations actually work
  • Drag-and-drop time-blocking is the fastest way to plan a day
  • AI auto-scheduling saves 10-15 min/day of planning
  • Keyboard-first design makes it feel like Linear for your calendar
  • Snooze, defer, and 'no time today' workflows are best-in-class

✗ What doesn't

  • No mobile app (web only — they promise native soon)
  • $15/mo is a lot if you only have 10 tasks/day
  • Steeper learning curve than Motion (more options = more setup)
  • Calendar sync is solid but Google Calendar only (Outlook is beta)

Verdict

Akiflow is the power-user choice for daily planning in 2026. If you live in 5+ task tools, the universal inbox alone pays for itself. The auto-scheduling isn't as polished as Motion's, but Akiflow wins on integrations and keyboard UX. Try it for the 14-day trial — you'll either love it or bounce in 3 days.
