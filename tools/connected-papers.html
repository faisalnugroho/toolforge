See the shape of a research field in one graph

Connected Papers builds a visual graph of related academic papers around any seed paper you drop in. Instead of scrolling endless search results, you get a network showing which works are most similar, which are foundational, and which are recent — perfect for literature reviews and finding prior art fast.

Who it's for: Grad students, academics, and anyone doing a literature review, meta-analysis, or competitive patent search.

Key features

🕸️ Literature graph

A node map of the most relevant papers to your seed reference.

📚 Prior & derivative works

Instantly see foundational papers and the newest offshoots.

🔎 Prior art search

Find what already exists before you write the grant or file.

📄 Export & cite

Save graphs and pull references into your manager.

The honest take

✓ What works

  • Makes literature discovery visual and fast
  • Excellent for onboarding to a new field
  • Free to generate graphs
  • Saves hours versus manual searching

✗ What doesn't

  • Not a full citation manager
  • Coverage depends on indexed corpus
  • Best for fields with rich arXiv/Semantic Scholar data

Verdict

The fastest on-ramp to a new research area we've used. For a literature review or a 'what's been done' check, Connected Papers beats keyword search every time. Pair it with Semantic Scholar or Scite for citation context.
