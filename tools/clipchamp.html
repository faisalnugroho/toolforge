The editor normies actually finish with

Clipchamp is Microsofts browser-based video editor. It pairs a drag-and-drop timeline with AI assists — silence removal, auto-captions, and text-based editing — at a price free users can live with. It is not Premiere, but it is the editor most people actually finish a video in.

Who it's for: Small business owners, educators, and marketers who need to ship decent video without learning pro software.

Key features

Auto-captions Speech to text

Generate accurate captions in one click across many languages.

Silence removal Clean cuts

Trim dead air automatically to tighten talking-head footage.

Templates Brand kits

Start from thousands of templates with brand colors and logos.

Stock library Built-in media

Access royalty-free audio and video without leaving the app.

The honest take

✓ What works

  • Free tier is genuinely usable
  • Runs in browser, no install
  • Auto-captions save hours
  • Microsoft account integration

✗ What doesn't

  • Watermark on some free exports
  • Less powerful than CapCut or Premiere
  • Premium needed for 4K
  • Occasional render slowdowns

Verdict

Clipchamp is the safe default for browser video editing. If you are on Windows and want captions fast, start here before paying for anything else.
