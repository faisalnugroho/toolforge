Remote recording that does not sound remote

Zencastr records each participant locally and uploads the lossless track, so your remote interview sounds like everyone was in the same room. Add AI editing — trimming filler words, auto-leveling, and a built-in video editor — and it is one of the most complete podcast workflows for solo creators.

Who it's for: Podcasters, interviewers, and video creators who record remote guests and want broadcast quality without a studio.

Key features

Local recording Lossless tracks

Each guest records on their machine, so connection drops never ruin audio.

AI editing Filler removal

Automatically cut umms, ahhs, and long pauses.

Video + audio Multitrack

Record separate video and audio tracks for each participant.

Soundboard Live effects

Trigger intros, ads, and stings during the live recording.

The honest take

✓ What works

  • Audio quality is genuinely studio-grade
  • AI editing saves hours of post
  • Free tier covers basics
  • Reliable even on weak connections

✗ What doesn't

  • Pro required for 4K video
  • Not a full DAW replacement
  • Steeper price than Riverside free
  • Browser-only, no desktop app

Verdict

If you record guests remotely, Zencastr removes the quality roulette of Zoom calls. The $15/mo Pro is worth it the first time a guest joins from a bad connection.
