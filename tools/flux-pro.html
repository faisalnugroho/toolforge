Photoreal images from text, without the Midjourney tax

Flux.1 Pro by Black Forest Labs is the open-weights image generation model that closed the gap with Midjourney in late 2024 and has only improved since. It produces near-photorealistic images, handles complex compositions with multiple subjects, and follows prompts with a fidelity that makes it the go-to for product mockups, marketing assets, and concept art. Unlike subscription-locked competitors, Flux runs on your own GPU or via any of a dozen API providers for pennies per image.

Who it's for: Marketing teams, e-commerce stores, game studios, and independent creators who need high-quality images without a monthly subscription lock-in.

Key features

🎨 Near-photoreal output

Flux.1 Pro produces images with skin textures, lighting, and reflections that rival studio photography. Less of the 'AI gloss' that plagues older models.

📝 Prompt adherence

Multi-subject prompts like 'a cat on the left, a dog on the right, both wearing hats' actually work. Spatial reasoning is notably better than SDXL.

🔧 Open weights

The model weights are available for local deployment. Run it on a single A100 or rent GPU time for a fraction of a Midjourney subscription.

📐 Aspect ratio control

Native support for landscape, portrait, and square outputs at up to 2048x2048 without cropping artifacts.

🌐 Multi-provider API

Replicate, fal.ai, Together AI, and others host Flux Pro. Switch providers without changing your pipeline.

The honest take

✓ What works

  • Best-in-class photorealism at this price point
  • No subscription required, pay per image
  • Open weights mean you can self-host
  • Excellent prompt following for complex scenes
  • Strong text rendering inside images

✗ What doesn't

  • Slower than Midjourney for batch generation
  • Local deployment requires a powerful GPU (24GB+ VRAM)
  • Less artistic styling out of the box, needs prompt engineering for stylized looks
  • No built-in community gallery or prompt discovery

Verdict

Flux.1 Pro is the smart choice if you need photorealistic AI images without committing to a $30/month Midjourney subscription. The open-weights model means you are never locked in, and the quality is competitive with or better than closed alternatives for product and marketing imagery. For artistic/stylized work, Midjourney still has an edge, but for everything else Flux Pro is the new default.
