The reference manager that respects your research

Zotero is the open-source standard for managing citations and PDFs. One click captures a paper, book, or webpage; Zotero stores the metadata, attaches the PDF, and drops a formatted citation into your word processor. After 20 years it's battle-tested by grad students, professors, and journalists who refuse to pay for EndNote.

Who it's for: Graduate students, academics, journalists, and anyone who reads more than 20 papers a year and needs citations that don't break.

Key features

Connect One-click capture

The Zotero Connector browser extension saves any page, PDF, or preprint to your library with full metadata in a single click.

PDF Built-in PDF reader

Highlight, annotate, and extract notes from PDFs inside Zotero. Notes are searchable and sync across devices.

Cite ZoteroBib citations

Generate a bibliography in 10,000+ citation styles (APA, MLA, Chicago) without installing anything — perfect for quick citations.

Sync Cross-platform sync

Your library syncs to Windows, macOS, Linux, iOS, and Android. Free tier includes 300MB of cloud storage; paid plans start at $20/yr.

The honest take

✓ What works

  • Completely free and open-source — no paywalls on core features
  • Best-in-class browser connector for one-click capture
  • 10,000+ citation styles via CSL
  • Strong community and plugin ecosystem (ZotFile, Better BibTeX)
  • Runs fully offline and respects your privacy

✗ What doesn't

  • Free cloud storage is small (300MB) — heavy PDF users need a paid plan
  • Interface feels dated next to Notion-style tools
  • No native AI summarization (yet) — pair with SciTe or Elicit
  • Collaborative group libraries can get messy at scale
  • Mobile apps are functional but barebones

Verdict

If you're in academia, research, or serious nonfiction writing, Zotero is the reference manager to beat in 2026. It's free, it's private, and it just works. Spend the $20/yr on storage and never lose a citation again.
